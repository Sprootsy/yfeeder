Stop Killing Games

The article "Stop Killing Games" on StopKillingGames.com is a passionate plea to preserve video games, particularly online games, in their playable form. It argues against the common practice of companies shutting down game servers and removing games from digital storefronts, effectively making them inaccessible and unplayable, even for those who have legally purchased them. The author emphasizes that video games, like other forms of art and media, deserve to be preserved for future generations.

The article highlights several reasons why this preservation is crucial. First, video games are a form of artistic expression and cultural heritage. They reflect the creativity, innovation, and social values of their time. Losing them is akin to losing books, films, or music. Second, many games offer unique experiences that cannot be replicated. The gameplay, stories, and communities formed around these games are often irreplaceable. Third, the author argues that consumers have a right to access and play the games they have purchased. Shutting down games is seen as a violation of consumer rights and a betrayal of trust.

The author criticizes the current state of digital distribution, where games are often treated as temporary services rather than permanent products. This model gives companies excessive control over access to games and allows them to arbitrarily remove games from sale and shut down servers. The article also points out the lack of legal frameworks and industry standards for game preservation. Copyright laws, DRM (Digital Rights Management), and the absence of clear guidelines make it difficult for archivists and enthusiasts to preserve games legally.

The article calls for a multi-faceted approach to game preservation. This includes advocating for legal reforms that protect the rights of consumers and archivists, promoting the development of open-source emulators and server emulators, supporting efforts to archive game code and assets, and raising awareness among the public and the gaming industry about the importance of game preservation. The author urges gamers, developers, publishers, and policymakers to work together to ensure that video games are not lost to time.

The website itself serves as a platform to collect information and news on game preservation, offer resources and tools for preserving games, and act as a space for gamers to voice their concerns about games being shut down and lost.

Key Points:

  • Games as Art: Video games are a form of art and cultural heritage that deserve to be preserved.
  • Irreplaceable Experiences: Many games offer unique and irreplaceable experiences.
  • Consumer Rights: Consumers have a right to access and play the games they have purchased.
  • Digital Distribution Issues: The current digital distribution model treats games as temporary services, giving companies too much control.
  • Lack of Legal Frameworks: There is a lack of legal and industry standards for game preservation.
  • Call to Action: The article calls for legal reforms, open-source development, archiving efforts, and increased awareness to preserve video games.
  • Platform for Preservation: StopKillingGames.com serves as a platform for news, resources, and community discussion on game preservation.
