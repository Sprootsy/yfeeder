Summary

On October 20, 2023, the National Transportation Safety Board (NTSB) issued a press release regarding its investigation into a fatal runway collision that occurred on February 22, 2023, at the South Texas Regional Airport in Hondo, Texas. The collision involved a single-engine Piper PA-28-140 aircraft, N140PL, and a twin-engine Beechcraft BE-58 airplane, N987RA. Both airplanes were destroyed, and all three occupants of the Piper and both occupants of the Beechcraft died.

The Piper was departing from runway 17, while the Beechcraft was performing touch-and-go landings on the same runway. Preliminary information indicates that the Piper began its takeoff roll without clearance from air traffic control. Simultaneously, the Beechcraft was on its landing roll after a touch-and-go. The collision occurred when the Piper became airborne and struck the Beechcraft.

The NTSB investigation is ongoing and will focus on several areas, including air traffic control procedures, pilot actions, and operational factors. The NTSB is working to determine the probable cause of the accident.
