The Tesla Model Y Just Scored the Worst Reliability Rating in a Decade

Summary: According to Consumer Reports' latest reliability survey, the Tesla Model Y has the worst reliability rating among all cars, not just EVs, making it the least reliable car surveyed in the past year. This is a significant downturn for the Model Y, which had already been struggling with reliability issues. Consumer Reports bases its reliability ratings on data collected from its members regarding problems they've experienced with their vehicles in the previous 12 months. The survey covers 17 trouble areas, including issues with the engine, transmission, and electrical system, as well as problems with the car's infotainment system, climate system, and body hardware. The Model Y's problems, according to Consumer Reports, stem from issues with body hardware, paint, trim, and in-car electronics.

Key Points:

  • The Tesla Model Y received the worst reliability rating in the latest Consumer Reports survey.
  • The Model Y's reliability issues include problems with body hardware, paint, trim, and in-car electronics.
  • Consumer Reports bases its reliability ratings on data from its members about problems experienced in the previous 12 months.
