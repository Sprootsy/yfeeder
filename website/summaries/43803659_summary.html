Thank you for holding my duck (2021)

Summary

The article "Thank You for Holding My Duck" is a personal reflection on the author's experience attending the National Association for Media Literacy Education (NAMLE) conference. The author uses the metaphor of someone asking them to hold a duck to represent the various interactions and exchanges that occurred during the conference.

The author describes the conference as a vibrant and engaging space filled with passionate individuals dedicated to media literacy education. They highlight the feeling of being immersed in conversations and presentations that challenged their thinking and broadened their understanding of media literacy concepts. The "duck" metaphor is employed to capture the feeling of trust and responsibility that comes with engaging in meaningful dialogue and collaboration with fellow educators. The act of holding someone's "duck" symbolizes being entrusted with their ideas, perspectives, and vulnerabilities.

Throughout the article, the author emphasizes the importance of active listening, empathy, and open-mindedness in fostering effective media literacy education. They suggest that true learning and growth occur when educators are willing to step outside their comfort zones, embrace diverse viewpoints, and engage in constructive dialogue. The author also reflects on the significance of creating a safe and supportive learning environment where individuals feel comfortable sharing their thoughts and experiences.

The author reflects on specific sessions and conversations that resonated with them, discussing topics such as critical thinking, misinformation, and the role of media in shaping public opinion. They highlight the value of learning from experienced educators and practitioners who are actively working to promote media literacy in their communities. The author also acknowledges the challenges and complexities of teaching media literacy in a rapidly changing media landscape.

In conclusion, the article expresses gratitude for the opportunity to attend the NAMLE conference and the connections made with fellow educators. The author emphasizes the importance of ongoing professional development and collaboration in advancing the field of media literacy education. The "duck" metaphor serves as a reminder to approach interactions with empathy, responsibility, and a willingness to learn from others.
