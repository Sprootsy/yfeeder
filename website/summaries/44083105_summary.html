What I discovered when I asked Amazon to tell me everything Alexa had heard

The article is a first-person account of the author's experience requesting and receiving all the data Amazon had collected from their Alexa devices. The author details the process of requesting the data, the format in which it was received, and the surprisingly large amount of information Amazon had stored. This included not only voice recordings of commands and queries but also transcripts of those recordings, data about music played, skills used, and even inferences about the author's habits and routines based on their interactions with Alexa.

The author expresses concern about the sheer volume of data collected and the potential implications for privacy. They were surprised to find recordings of conversations they didn't realize were being captured, triggered perhaps by accidental activation of the device. They also highlight the fact that Amazon retains this data indefinitely unless the user actively deletes it. The author notes the difficulty in sifting through the data to understand exactly what was recorded and how it might be used.

While the author acknowledges the convenience of Alexa and similar voice-activated assistants, they raise questions about the trade-off between convenience and privacy. They encourage readers to be aware of the data these devices collect, to review their privacy settings, and to regularly delete recordings to minimize the amount of personal information stored by these companies. The piece serves as a cautionary tale about the extent of data collection by smart home devices and the importance of taking steps to protect one's privacy in an increasingly connected world.

Key Points:

  • The author requested and received all data Amazon collected from their Alexa devices.
  • Amazon stored a large amount of data, including voice recordings, transcripts, usage data, and inferred habits.
  • The author was surprised by the amount and nature of the data collected, including recordings of unintended conversations.
  • Amazon retains data indefinitely unless the user actively deletes it.
  • The article raises concerns about privacy implications of smart home devices.
  • The author recommends users to review privacy settings and regularly delete recordings.
