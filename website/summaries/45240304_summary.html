Bank of Thailand freezes 3M accounts, sets daily transfer limits to curb fraud

The Bank of Thailand (BOT) has taken measures to combat the rising problem of financial scams, which have resulted in losses of approximately 6 billion baht. In response to these increasing fraudulent activities, the BOT has frozen over 3 million bank accounts linked to scams and has established daily transfer limits for online transactions. The new transfer limits are set between 50,000 to 200,000 baht, depending on the risk profile of the account, aiming to reduce the potential damage from each scam incident. The BOT's actions reflect an intensified effort to protect consumers and maintain the integrity of the financial system amidst the growing sophistication and prevalence of online fraud.

Key Points:

  • The Bank of Thailand (BOT) has frozen over 3 million bank accounts involved in financial scams.

  • These actions are intended to protect consumers and reduce financial scam damages.
