The share of Americans having regular sex keeps dropping

The Institute for Family Studies article, "The Sex Recession: The Share of Americans Having Regular Sex Keeps Dropping," discusses the decline in sexual activity among Americans, particularly among younger generations. The author analyzes data from the General Social Survey (GSS) and other sources to illustrate this trend and explore potential contributing factors.

The article begins by highlighting the overall decrease in the frequency of sex reported by Americans. The data shows a consistent downward trend over the past few decades, with a notable drop in recent years. This decline is observed across various demographic groups, including married couples, single individuals, and different age cohorts. However, the trend is particularly pronounced among younger adults, especially those under 30.

Several potential explanations for this "sex recession" are explored. One factor discussed is the rise of digital entertainment and technology. The author suggests that increased screen time and the availability of online entertainment options may be diverting time and attention away from sexual activity and relationship building. The article also touches on the potential impact of social media and its influence on body image and self-esteem, which may affect sexual desire and confidence.

Another contributing factor examined is the changing nature of relationships and marriage. The article notes that marriage rates have been declining, and people are marrying later in life, which can affect the frequency of sexual activity. Additionally, the rise of cohabitation and non-traditional relationship structures may also play a role in shaping sexual behaviors and patterns.

The author also considers the role of economic factors, such as financial stress and job insecurity, which can contribute to increased stress levels and decreased libido. Furthermore, the article acknowledges the potential impact of cultural and societal shifts, including changing attitudes towards sex and relationships, as well as increased awareness of issues such as consent and sexual harassment, which may be influencing sexual behaviors and attitudes.

The article also highlights some differences across gender and education levels. For example, men with less education have seen a particularly sharp decline in sexual activity, potentially due to economic factors impacting their perceived attractiveness as partners.

Overall, the article suggests that the decline in sexual activity is a multifaceted issue with no single cause. It is likely a combination of factors, including technological, social, economic, and cultural changes, that are contributing to this trend. The author concludes by emphasizing the importance of further research and discussion to better understand the implications of this "sex recession" for individuals, relationships, and society as a whole.

Key Points:

  • There is a decline in the frequency of sex among Americans.
  • The decline is more pronounced among younger adults.
  • Potential factors include increased screen time and digital entertainment.
  • Changing relationship patterns and declining marriage rates may contribute.
  • Economic factors and financial stress could play a role.
  • Cultural shifts and changing attitudes towards sex may also be influencing the trend.
  • Men with less education have seen a sharp decline in sexual activity.
