NTSB report: Decryption of images from the Titan submersible camera [pdf]

Summary

According to the National Transportation Safety Board (NTSB) report, an underwater camera was used to inspect the wreckage of an aircraft that crashed in the water. The underwater video and stills recorded during the underwater survey were reviewed. The video was recorded by using a remotely operated vehicle (ROV) equipped with video and still cameras and lights. The ROV was operated by Phoenix International. The aircraft wreckage was distributed over a large area of the seabed. The main fuselage section was located and identified, along with other major components such as the engines and sections of the wings.

Key Points

  • An underwater camera was deployed to survey the wreckage of a submerged aircraft.
  • The underwater survey was conducted by Phoenix International using a remotely operated vehicle (ROV).
  • The ROV was equipped with video and still cameras, as well as lights, to capture images of the wreckage.
  • The wreckage was scattered across a wide area on the seabed.
  • The main fuselage of the aircraft was located and identified.
  • Major components such as the engines and wing sections were also found and documented.
