South Korea's President says US investment demands would spark financial crisis

South Korea's President Lee is preparing for a potential global financial crisis triggered by a possible Trump victory in the upcoming US presidential election. President Lee's administration is drawing parallels to the 1997 Asian financial crisis and the 2008 global financial crisis, both of which significantly impacted South Korea's economy. They are concerned that Trump's policies, particularly in trade and foreign policy, could destabilize global markets and negatively affect South Korea. Specific worries include potential trade wars, currency manipulation accusations, and shifts in the US security umbrella over the Korean peninsula. President Lee has established a task force to monitor the situation and develop contingency plans. These plans involve measures to stabilize the South Korean won, manage capital flows, and support key industries. The government is also considering increasing its foreign exchange reserves and strengthening cooperation with other countries in the region. The article highlights the anxiety within the South Korean government regarding the potential economic fallout from a second Trump term and the proactive steps they are taking to mitigate the risks.

  • South Korea's President Lee is preparing for a potential global financial crisis linked to a possible Trump victory in the US presidential election.
  • The South Korean government is drawing comparisons to past financial crises that affected the country.
  • Concerns exist about Trump's potential policies on trade, currency, and foreign policy.
  • A government task force has been created to monitor the situation and develop contingency plans.
  • The plans involve stabilizing the won, managing capital flows, and supporting key industries.
  • South Korea is considering increasing foreign exchange reserves and strengthening international cooperation.
