Over 80% of sunscreen performed below their labelled efficacy (2020)

Summary:

The Hong Kong Consumer Council tested 29 sunscreen products, assessing their sun protection factor (SPF), UVA protection, and ingredient labeling. The test revealed that over 70% of the tested sunscreen products did not meet their labeled SPF claims. While the declared SPF values ranged from 30 to 50+, the actual SPF values were lower. Some sunscreens failed to provide adequate UVA protection, despite claiming broad-spectrum protection. Ingredient labeling discrepancies were also found in some products. The tests were conducted according to international standards. The Consumer Council recommends consumers to apply a sufficient amount of sunscreen, reapply every two hours or after swimming/sweating, and choose broad-spectrum sunscreens with both UVA and UVB protection. They also advise manufacturers to improve the accuracy of their SPF labeling and ingredient information.

Key Points:

  • Tests were conducted on 29 sunscreen products.
  • More than 70% of sunscreens failed to meet their labeled SPF claims.
  • Some sunscreens did not provide adequate UVA protection.
  • Discrepancies were found in ingredient labeling.
  • The Consumer Council advises consumers to apply sufficient sunscreen, reapply regularly, and choose broad-spectrum options.
  • Manufacturers are urged to improve SPF labeling accuracy and ingredient information.
