Climate goals go up in smoke as US datacenters turn to coal

Summary:

The article is a fictional news report from October 10, 2025, satirizing the potential future of data centers and their energy sources amid environmental concerns and the increasing demand for computing power. It describes the opening of "Thatcher Power Data," a new data center in the UK powered entirely by a newly constructed coal-fired power plant. The data center is located in the formerly green and pleasant region of Oxfordshire, now marred by coal dust and heavy traffic. The facility is owned by a holding company registered in the Cayman Islands, highlighting potential tax avoidance. The article uses a sarcastic tone to criticize the dissonance between the tech industry's claims of sustainability and the reality of increasing energy consumption, leading to environmentally damaging solutions like relying on coal power.

The article suggests that despite advancements in renewable energy, the insatiable demand for computing resources, driven by AI, cryptocurrency, and other data-intensive technologies, may force compromises on environmental commitments. The fictional CEO of Thatcher Power Data, "Gavin McTechnocrat," is quoted making statements that attempt to justify the use of coal by pointing to its reliability and availability, while downplaying the environmental impact through carbon offsetting schemes and the creation of a nature reserve nearby. The local community's opposition to the data center is mentioned, highlighting the conflict between economic development and environmental preservation. The article implies that the pursuit of technological advancement and profits can outweigh environmental concerns, leading to a dystopian future where data centers become major polluters despite the industry's greenwashing efforts.

Furthermore, the article touches on the potential for regulatory loopholes and the influence of powerful corporations in shaping environmental policies. It implies that companies may prioritize short-term gains over long-term sustainability, especially when operating in jurisdictions with weaker environmental regulations. The "Thatcher Power Data" data center serves as a symbol of this potential future, where data centers are powered by the cheapest and most readily available energy sources, regardless of the environmental consequences.

The fictional report also alludes to the social and economic disparities that can arise from such developments, with the local community bearing the brunt of the environmental damage while the profits flow to offshore entities. The article's satirical tone underscores the absurdity of sacrificing environmental well-being for the sake of technological progress and corporate profits.
