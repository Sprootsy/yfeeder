Halt and Catch Fire Syllabus (2021)

This article is a syllabus for a fictional course inspired by the TV show "Halt and Catch Fire." The course explores the early history of personal computing, the rise of the internet, and the cultural and societal impact of technology through a blend of fictional television episodes and non-fiction readings. The course aims to examine themes of innovation, collaboration, competition, failure, and the human cost of technological progress. The syllabus outlines the course objectives, required texts (including episodes of "Halt and Catch Fire"), a weekly schedule of topics, readings, and assignments, and grading policies.

The course is structured around weekly themes, each combining "Halt and Catch Fire" episodes with relevant academic readings and other materials. Topics include the origins of the PC, reverse engineering, the culture of innovation, the rise of online communities, the dot-com boom and bust, and the social impact of technology. The syllabus details specific episodes from each season of "Halt and Catch Fire" paired with book chapters, articles, and other resources that provide historical context and critical perspectives. For example, the week focusing on the origins of the PC combines episodes from season 1 with chapters from "Accidental Empires" by Robert X. Cringely, and other relevant readings.

Assignments listed in the syllabus include weekly response papers, participation in class discussions, a midterm exam, and a final project. The response papers require students to analyze and reflect on the week's readings and episodes, connecting the fictional narrative to real-world history and theory. The final project offers a choice between a traditional research paper or a more creative project, such as creating a website, podcast, or video essay. The syllabus also describes the grading breakdown, which emphasizes critical thinking, writing skills, and engagement with the course material.

The author, Ashley Blewer, designed the syllabus to provide a framework for understanding the complex relationship between technology and society. The course uses "Halt and Catch Fire" as a springboard for exploring the human stories behind technological innovation, acknowledging both the excitement and the struggles of the people who shaped the digital world. The syllabus serves as a resource for educators or anyone interested in delving deeper into the history and culture of computing through a combination of entertainment and academic inquiry.
