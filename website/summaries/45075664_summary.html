Summary

GAO-25-107196, a report by the Government Accountability Office, examines the federal government's efforts to address marine debris. Marine debris, defined as persistent solid waste in the marine environment, poses significant threats to marine ecosystems, wildlife, and human health, and it can also have economic consequences. The report assesses the roles and responsibilities of federal agencies, the strategies they employ, and the challenges they face in combating this pervasive problem.

The report identifies the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA) and the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) as key federal agencies involved in addressing marine debris. NOAA primarily focuses on research, prevention, and removal efforts, while the EPA addresses land-based sources of marine debris through regulatory and voluntary programs. The report also acknowledges the contributions of other federal agencies, such as the Coast Guard, in addressing specific aspects of the issue.

GAO's analysis highlights the importance of interagency coordination in tackling marine debris effectively. The report acknowledges the existence of the Interagency Marine Debris Coordinating Committee (IMDCC), which aims to enhance collaboration among federal agencies. However, the report suggests that opportunities exist to improve coordination and ensure that efforts are aligned and complementary.

The report also examines the challenges associated with measuring the effectiveness of marine debris programs. Quantifying the impacts of these programs is difficult due to the complex nature of the problem and the lack of standardized metrics. The report emphasizes the need for improved data collection and analysis to better assess the effectiveness of different strategies and inform future decision-making.

Furthermore, the report addresses the issue of international cooperation in addressing marine debris, since it is a global problem that requires international solutions. The report acknowledges the United States' engagement in international forums and initiatives aimed at reducing marine debris. However, the report suggests that there are opportunities to strengthen international partnerships and promote more effective global action.

In conclusion, GAO-25-107196 provides a comprehensive overview of the federal government's efforts to address marine debris. The report highlights the roles of key federal agencies, the importance of interagency coordination, the challenges of measuring program effectiveness, and the need for international cooperation. The findings and recommendations presented in the report aim to inform policymakers and stakeholders and to enhance the effectiveness of efforts to combat marine debris and protect marine environments.

Key Points

  • Measuring the effectiveness of marine debris programs is challenging due to the complex nature of the problem and the lack of standardized metrics.
  • International cooperation is essential for addressing marine debris on a global scale.
