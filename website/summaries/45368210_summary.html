Homeowner baffled after washing machine uses 3.6GB of internet data a day (2024)

Summary:

A homeowner, Amber Carden, was surprised to discover that her smart washing machine was using a significant amount of internet data, averaging between 3GB and 6GB per month. This discovery was made when Carden investigated unusually high internet usage and identified the washing machine as the culprit. She posted about the issue on TikTok, seeking explanations and solutions. The smart washing machine, manufactured by LG, is connected to the internet to provide users with features such as remote control and diagnostic information through a smartphone app. Experts suggest that the high data usage could be due to a few reasons. One possibility is software bugs or glitches that cause the machine to continuously transmit data. Another is the frequency of data updates to the app, or even unintended streaming of data. Additionally, the potential exists for unauthorized access or hacking of the device, leading to unusual activity. Some comments indicate the possibility of the machine sending video data. Carden has contacted LG for assistance, but as of the time of reporting, the company has not provided a clear explanation or solution. The situation raises concerns about the data consumption of smart home devices and the potential for unexpected internet usage charges. It also highlights the importance of monitoring data usage and understanding the functionalities of connected appliances. The incident serves as a cautionary tale for owners of smart devices, indicating a need to remain vigilant about data usage. The need to consider cybersecurity vulnerabilities with IoT devices that may transmit private user data is also an important consideration. It also encourages further investigation into the data consumption habits of internet-connected appliances.
