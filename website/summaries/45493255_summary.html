The worrying kink in this job openings, unemployment curve

The article discusses the state of the U.S. labor market and the Federal Reserve's monetary policy in October 2025. The labor market is described as remaining surprisingly robust despite the Fed's efforts to cool it down through interest rate hikes.

Unemployment has ticked up slightly to 4%, but job growth has continued at a steady pace, defying predictions of a significant slowdown. The participation rate remains steady, indicating people are still actively seeking employment. Wages are still increasing.

The Fed is grappling with the challenge of balancing its dual mandate of price stability and maximum employment. Inflation has come down from its peak but remains above the Fed's 2% target, complicating the decision-making process regarding further interest rate adjustments. Some economists argue the Fed should pause rate hikes, while others warn against premature easing, fearing a resurgence of inflation.

The article highlights the unusual dynamics at play: a resilient labor market coexisting with persistent inflationary pressures. This has created uncertainty and debate among economists and policymakers about the appropriate course of action.

Key points:

  • The U.S. labor market remains strong in October 2025, with steady job growth.
  • Unemployment has risen slightly to 4%.
  • Inflation remains above the Fed's 2% target, despite previous interest rate hikes.
  • The Fed faces a dilemma in balancing inflation control and maintaining a healthy labor market.
  • Economists are divided on whether the Fed should continue raising interest rates or pause.
