UC Berkeley gives personal information for 150 students and staff to government

The Daily Californian reports that UC Berkeley inadvertently shared the personal information of over 150 students and staff members with the U.S. Department of Labor's Office of Federal Contract Compliance Programs (OFCCP). This disclosure occurred during a compliance review related to the university's obligations as a federal contractor. The information included names, employee identification numbers, genders, ethnicities, and compensation details.

According to UC Berkeley spokesperson, the university promptly notified those affected and offered identity theft protection services. The OFCCP has stated that it is reviewing its data security practices to prevent similar incidents. The university is also conducting an internal review to determine how the error happened and to implement measures to avoid future breaches.

The disclosure has raised concerns about data privacy and the protection of sensitive personal information. The university is working to address these concerns and reassure the community that steps are being taken to safeguard personal data.
