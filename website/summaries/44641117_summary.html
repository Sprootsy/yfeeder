LetsEncrypt Outage

Summary

The provided content is a status page for Let's Encrypt, a free, automated, and open Certificate Authority (CA). It provides real-time information on the operational status of Let's Encrypt's various systems and services. The status page is hosted by Status.io.

The page displays the overall system status, indicating whether all systems are operational or if there are any ongoing incidents. It breaks down the status of individual components, such as:

  • API: The main API endpoint for requesting and managing certificates.
  • OCSP: The Online Certificate Status Protocol responder, which provides real-time revocation status of certificates.
  • Website: The Let's Encrypt website (letsencrypt.org).
  • Documentation: Access to Let's Encrypt's documentation.
  • Boulder: The ACME CA software that Let's Encrypt uses.
  • Revocation Services: Services related to certificate revocation.
  • ACME v2 Production Endpoint: The production endpoint for the ACME v2 protocol.
  • ACME v2 Staging Endpoint: The staging endpoint for the ACME v2 protocol, used for testing.

Each component's status is indicated by a color-coded symbol, typically green for operational, yellow for under maintenance or experiencing performance issues, orange for partial outage, and red for a major outage. The page also includes a history of past incidents, allowing users to review any previous disruptions and their resolutions. Each incident report typically includes a timeline of updates, detailing the issue, the steps taken to resolve it, and the final resolution.

Users can subscribe to receive notifications via email, SMS, webhook, or RSS feed to stay informed about any changes in status or upcoming maintenance events. This allows for proactive awareness of potential disruptions to Let's Encrypt services.

Key Points

  • Let's Encrypt uses Status.io to provide a real-time status page for its services.
  • The page monitors the status of key components, including the API, OCSP responder, website, documentation, and Boulder.
  • Status indicators (green, yellow, orange, red) show the operational status of each component.
  • Historical incident reports provide details on past disruptions and their resolutions.
  • Users can subscribe to notifications to receive updates on status changes and maintenance events.
  • The status page helps users quickly assess if any issues are related to Let's Encrypt's infrastructure or are caused by something else.
