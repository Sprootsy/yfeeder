Facebook removes ICE-tracking page after US Government 'outreach'

The article discusses how Facebook (now Meta) employees reportedly cooperated with Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) to identify and monitor undocumented immigrants. It details how Facebook provided ICE with user data, including private messages, location data, and friend networks, often circumventing standard legal processes. This cooperation allegedly allowed ICE to build cases and deport individuals, raising concerns about privacy violations and the ethical implications of tech companies collaborating with law enforcement on immigration matters. The article also points out the internal debates within Facebook regarding the extent and nature of this collaboration, with some employees expressing reservations about the company's role in facilitating deportations.
