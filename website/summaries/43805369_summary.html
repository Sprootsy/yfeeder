The Seven-Year Rule

Summary:

The article discusses the concept of the "Seven-Year Rule," a personal framework the author uses to make decisions about technology, habits, and various aspects of life. The core idea is to re-evaluate commitments and technologies every seven years to determine if they still align with current goals and needs. This rule isn't about arbitrarily discarding things but about intentional reflection and recalibration.

The author outlines several key areas where the Seven-Year Rule is applied. Regarding technology, the author periodically reviews the software and hardware used in their workflow. This review helps identify outdated tools that need replacing and highlights the technologies that continue to provide value. It's not about chasing the latest trends but about ensuring the tech stack remains efficient and effective. Furthermore, the author mentions regularly thinking about the technologies he uses and how they help or hurt him. The rule also extends to habits. The author emphasizes the importance of questioning established routines to ensure they still serve a purpose. Habits that were once beneficial may become outdated or even detrimental over time, so regular evaluation is crucial. The article specifically touches on the concept of building habits to the point of being "automatic", but then periodically reassessing them to verify that they still suit one's aims.

Another application is in personal and professional life. The author discusses re-evaluating career paths, hobbies, and relationships. This involves assessing whether these areas still bring fulfillment and align with evolving values and aspirations. It's about making conscious choices to invest time and energy in activities and relationships that are meaningful and supportive.

The author stresses that the Seven-Year Rule is not a rigid mandate but a flexible guideline. It encourages a proactive approach to personal development and ensures that life choices are deliberate rather than defaults. It encourages thinking about what the author wants to do versus what he is actually doing. In short, the Seven-Year Rule is a system of constant reflection. The goal is to not allow life to simply happen to you.
