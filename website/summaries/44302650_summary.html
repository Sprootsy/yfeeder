Long live Xorg, I mean Xlibre

Summary

The article is a review of X-Distrib, a custom-built Linux distribution created by the author. X-Distrib is built using Debian as a base and aims to provide a pre-configured, ready-to-use desktop experience with a focus on aesthetics, usability, and a curated selection of applications. The author details the process of building X-Distrib, including the initial setup of a Debian base system, the choice of the XFCE desktop environment, and the installation and configuration of various software packages.

The author emphasizes the importance of a clean and consistent visual theme, achieved through the use of custom icons, wallpapers, and GTK themes. They also highlight the inclusion of essential software for common tasks such as web browsing, office productivity, multimedia playback, and system administration. The article provides specific examples of the applications included in X-Distrib and the reasoning behind their selection.

Furthermore, the article discusses the customization of the XFCE desktop environment, including the configuration of panels, menus, and keyboard shortcuts to optimize the user experience. The author also describes the process of creating custom scripts and utilities to automate common tasks and enhance system functionality.

The article also touches upon the challenges and considerations involved in creating a custom Linux distribution, such as managing dependencies, ensuring system stability, and providing user support. The author shares their experiences and lessons learned throughout the development process.

The article concludes with a reflection on the benefits of creating a custom Linux distribution, including the ability to tailor the system to specific needs and preferences, as well as the opportunity to learn about the inner workings of Linux. The author encourages readers to explore the possibility of building their own custom distributions and provides resources for further learning.

Key Points

  • X-Distrib is a custom Linux distribution based on Debian.
  • It uses the XFCE desktop environment.
  • Focuses on aesthetics, usability, and a curated set of applications.
  • Includes custom themes, icons, and wallpapers for a consistent visual experience.
  • Pre-installed with software for web browsing, office productivity, multimedia, and system administration.
  • XFCE desktop environment is customized with optimized panels, menus, and keyboard shortcuts.
