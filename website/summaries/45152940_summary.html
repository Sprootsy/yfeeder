Show HN: I'm making an open-source platform for learning Japanese

Summary:

The Kanadojo website serves as a portal to learn about and engage with the world of Japanese swordsmanship, specifically focusing on Kenjutsu and Iaido. It highlights the teachings and philosophy of Carl Longsensei, who leads the Kanadojo dojo. The site offers a range of information, from the history and lineage of the martial arts practiced to practical details about training, membership, and the dojo's schedule.

The site emphasizes the traditional aspects of Kenjutsu and Iaido, tracing their roots back to classical samurai traditions. It presents Kenjutsu as a combative sword-drawing art, encompassing not only physical techniques but also mental discipline and ethical principles. Iaido, on the other hand, is presented as the art of drawing the sword and responding to a sudden attack, cultivated as a martial art and a way of self-improvement.

Visitors can find information on the dojo's location in the Greater Toronto Area, Ontario, Canada, as well as details on class times and how to get involved. The site promotes a welcoming environment for beginners while maintaining a commitment to preserving the authenticity and rigor of the traditional martial arts. The focus is on fostering a community of dedicated practitioners under the guidance of an experienced instructor.

The Kanadojo website also serves as a platform for sharing insights and updates on seminars, events, and other relevant news. It provides a way for prospective students to learn about the dojo's philosophy and approach to training before committing to joining. The site aims to be a comprehensive resource for anyone interested in learning about Kenjutsu and Iaido within the specific context of the Kanadojo dojo and the teachings of Carl Longsensei.
