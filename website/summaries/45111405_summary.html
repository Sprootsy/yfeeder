Zig Software Foundation 2025 Financial Report and Fundraiser

Zig is an open-source programming language focused on robustness, optimality, and maintainability. The Zig Software Foundation (ZSF) is the non-profit organization that supports the development and adoption of the Zig programming language.

The article provides a detailed overview of the Zig Software Foundation's (ZSF) financials for 2023 and projections for 2024 and 2025. The primary purpose is to provide transparency to the Zig community regarding the financial health and sustainability of the project.

2023 Retrospective:

  • The ZSF's income for 2023 was approximately $689,000, with significant contributions from GitHub Sponsors, individual donations, and corporate sponsorships.
  • Expenses for 2023 totaled around $448,000. The largest expense category was payroll, compensating core contributors, followed by infrastructure costs and grants.
  • The ZSF ended 2023 with a net surplus of approximately $241,000, boosting the organization's reserves.

2024 Outlook:

  • The projected income for 2024 is around $850,000, anticipating growth in corporate sponsorships and continued support from the community.
  • Expected expenses for 2024 are budgeted at approximately $864,000. Payroll remains the largest expense, with plans to increase the number of compensated core contributors. Infrastructure costs are also expected to rise, and there's a budget for potential legal fees.
  • The projected net result for 2024 is a slight deficit, drawing slightly from the reserves built in 2023.

2025 Projections:

  • The projected income for 2025 is approximately $1,175,000. This forecast anticipates continued growth in corporate sponsorships, increased GitHub Sponsors contributions, and revenue from other sources.
  • Expenses for 2025 are budgeted at approximately $1,150,000. Payroll remains the largest expense, reflecting a continued commitment to compensating core contributors. The budget also includes significant allocations for infrastructure, grants, and potential marketing efforts.
  • The projected net result for 2025 is a surplus of approximately $25,000, indicating a return to financial sustainability after the projected deficit in 2024.

Key Strategic Goals and Initiatives:

  • Compensating Core Contributors: A primary focus is on providing financial support to core contributors, enabling them to dedicate more time to Zig's development.
  • Infrastructure Investment: The ZSF is committed to investing in the infrastructure required to support Zig's growing community and development efforts.
  • Grant Program: Allocating funds for grants to support community projects and initiatives that contribute to the Zig ecosystem.
  • Marketing and Outreach: Exploring opportunities to increase awareness and adoption of Zig through marketing and outreach activities.

Financial Health and Sustainability:

  • The ZSF aims to maintain sufficient reserves to cover operating expenses for at least one year.
  • Diversification of income streams is a priority to reduce reliance on any single source of funding.
  • The organization is committed to transparency and providing regular updates to the community regarding its financial performance.

In conclusion, the ZSF's financial outlook for 2024 and 2025 demonstrates a commitment to supporting the continued development and growth of the Zig programming language through strategic investments in core contributors, infrastructure, and community initiatives. The projections reflect a path toward long-term financial sustainability, with a focus on diversifying income streams and maintaining healthy reserves.
