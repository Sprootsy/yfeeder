United States Digital Service Origins

Summary

The article delves into the origins and creation of the United States Digital Service (USDS), highlighting the circumstances and key individuals involved in its formation. It emphasizes the critical need for improved digital services within the government and recounts the events that led to the USDS's establishment, primarily the problematic rollout of Healthcare.gov. The failure of Healthcare.gov served as a catalyst, exposing the government's struggles with technology procurement, development, and implementation.

The article details how a small, dedicated team of technologists and entrepreneurs, many from the private sector, were brought together to rescue Healthcare.gov. Their success in turning the project around demonstrated the potential for modern technology practices to significantly improve government services. This success spurred the idea of creating a permanent organization focused on bringing those practices to other government agencies.

Key figures such as Todd Park, Mikey Dickerson, and Haley Van Dyck are mentioned as playing crucial roles in advocating for and shaping the USDS. The article explains how they navigated the complexities of the government bureaucracy, building support from the White House and various agencies. It also describes the challenges they faced in recruiting talented individuals willing to work within the government system and in establishing credibility and trust with existing government employees.

Furthermore, the piece discusses the initial focus areas of the USDS, including veterans' services, immigration, and criminal justice. It highlights the organization's approach of embedding small teams of experts within agencies to work on specific projects, emphasizing user-centered design, iterative development, and data-driven decision-making.

In essence, the article chronicles the journey of the USDS from an idea born out of crisis to a functioning organization aimed at transforming the way the government delivers digital services to the American people. It underscores the importance of attracting top tech talent to public service and adopting modern technology practices within government.

Key Points

  • The disastrous launch of Healthcare.gov was the primary catalyst for the creation of the USDS.
  • A small team of technologists successfully rescued Healthcare.gov, demonstrating the potential for improved digital services in government.
  • Todd Park, Mikey Dickerson, and Haley Van Dyck were instrumental in advocating for and shaping the USDS.
  • The USDS was created to bring modern technology practices and talent to government agencies.
  • Initial focus areas included veterans' services, immigration, and criminal justice.
  • The USDS uses small, embedded teams, user-centered design, and iterative development.
