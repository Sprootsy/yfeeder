Tomorrow's emoji today: Unicode 17.0

The article discusses the upcoming Unicode 17.0 update, focusing on potential new emojis. It emphasizes that the process of adding emojis is complex and lengthy, involving proposals, reviews, and technical considerations. While new emojis are exciting, the author highlights the importance of Unicode's primary function: ensuring consistent text encoding across different systems. The article also mentions that Unicode 17.0 is scheduled to be released in September 2024, and touches on the speculative nature of predicting the final list of new emojis, as the selection process involves many factors. Overall the article emphasizes that there are always considerations around language support and other character encoding before emojis are considered.

Key points:

  • Unicode 17.0 is scheduled for release in September 2024.
  • The process of adding new emojis is complex and takes time.
  • Predicting the final list of new emojis is speculative.
  • Unicode's main function is text encoding, not just emojis.
  • Emoji addition is a factor of many considerations, including language support.
