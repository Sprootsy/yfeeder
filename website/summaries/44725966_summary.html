ACM Transitions to Full Open Access

Summary:

The article discusses the Association for Computing Machinery's (ACM) approach to open access (OA) publishing. ACM has a long-standing commitment to disseminating research widely and has been experimenting with OA models for many years. The article outlines the different OA options ACM offers to authors and institutions, highlighting the ACM Open model, which allows institutions to provide OA to all research published by their affiliated authors.

The article emphasizes ACM's belief that a sustainable OA transition requires a collaborative approach involving publishers, researchers, and institutions. It also addresses the challenges associated with OA, such as funding models and ensuring the quality and integrity of published research.

Overall, the article positions ACM as an organization actively working towards a future where computing research is more accessible, while also maintaining the high standards of peer review and ethical publishing practices that are crucial to the scientific community.

Key Points:

  • ACM is committed to open access publishing.
  • ACM offers various OA options for authors and institutions.
  • ACM Open model enables institutions to provide OA to research by affiliated authors.
  • ACM believes in a collaborative approach to sustainable OA transition.
  • ACM addresses challenges like funding models and research quality in OA.
