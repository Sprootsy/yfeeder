2025 Nikon Small World in Motion Competition Winners

Summary

The article announces the opening of the 2025 Nikon Small World in Motion Competition for entries. This competition celebrates short videos and time-lapse movies showcasing the beauty and complexity of life sciences through the microscope. It's a sister competition to the long-standing Nikon Small World Photomicrography Competition, but specifically focuses on moving images. The competition is open to anyone with an interest in photomicrography and scientific imaging. There are no specific eligibility requirements regarding professional or amateur status.

Entrants are invited to submit videos of up to 60 seconds in length that capture dynamic processes in biology, medicine, and other related scientific fields. The submissions are judged based on originality, informational content, technical proficiency, and visual impact. A panel of experts in science and visual communication evaluates the entries. The winners receive prizes, and their work is showcased to a global audience, promoting both the art and science of photomicrography.

The Nikon Small World in Motion Competition provides a platform for researchers, scientists, and artists to share their captivating microscopic movies, fostering a greater understanding and appreciation for the intricate details of the natural world at a microscopic level. The competition encourages innovative approaches to microscopy and imaging techniques.

Key Points

  • The 2025 Nikon Small World in Motion Competition is open for entries.
  • It is a competition for short videos and time-lapse movies of life sciences subjects under a microscope.
  • The competition is open to anyone, regardless of professional or amateur status.
  • Videos must be 60 seconds or less.
  • Judging criteria include originality, informational content, technical proficiency, and visual impact.
  • Winners receive prizes and global recognition.
  • The competition promotes the art and science of photomicrography.
