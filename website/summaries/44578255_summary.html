Congress moves to reject bulk of White House's proposed NASA cuts

Summary:

The article discusses the US Congress's response to the White House's proposed budget cuts for NASA in fiscal year 2026. The House Appropriations Committee has moved to reject a significant portion of these proposed cuts, particularly those targeting crucial programs like the Mars Sample Return mission. The White House had sought to reduce NASA's overall budget by $250 million, with more substantial reductions to specific projects. The committee's action indicates strong bipartisan support for NASA and a reluctance to scale back ambitious space exploration endeavors. The article highlights the specific areas where Congress is pushing back against the proposed cuts, including the Mars Sample Return, which had faced a potential restructuring or delay due to budget constraints. It also mentions the committee's support for other NASA programs, suggesting a broader commitment to maintaining the agency's current trajectory despite the administration's fiscal concerns. Overall, the article portrays a congressional effort to protect NASA's funding and strategic priorities from the White House's proposed budget reductions.

Key points:

  • Congress is moving to reject the bulk of the White House's proposed budget cuts for NASA in fiscal year 2026.
  • The House Appropriations Committee is pushing back against a $250 million reduction in NASA's overall budget proposed by the White House.
  • The committee is specifically resisting cuts to the Mars Sample Return mission, which had been targeted for potential restructuring or delays.
  • The congressional action indicates bipartisan support for NASA and a commitment to maintaining its current programs and strategic goals.
  • Other NASA programs are also receiving support from the committee, suggesting a broader effort to protect the agency's funding.
