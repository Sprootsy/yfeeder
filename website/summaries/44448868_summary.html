Websites hosting major US climate reports taken down

The U.S. government has released its Fifth National Climate Assessment, detailing the intensifying impacts of climate change across the nation. The report, mandated by Congress, emphasizes that climate change is not a future threat but a present reality, with every U.S. region already experiencing its effects. It warns of escalating risks to human health, the economy, and ecosystems. The assessment highlights that the Southeast is particularly vulnerable to rising temperatures and hurricanes, while the Southwest faces severe water shortages. Coastal communities are grappling with increased flooding, and the Midwest's agricultural productivity is threatened. The report stresses that while some actions to reduce emissions and adapt to the changing climate are underway, they are insufficient to avoid the most severe consequences. It also underscores the disproportionate impact of climate change on marginalized communities, exacerbating existing inequalities. The assessment calls for urgent and substantial action to mitigate greenhouse gas emissions and build resilience to the unavoidable impacts of climate change.

Key Points:

  • The Fifth National Climate Assessment confirms that climate change is already impacting every region of the U.S.
  • Rising temperatures, extreme weather events, and sea-level rise are posing significant threats to human health, the economy, and ecosystems.
  • The Southeast is vulnerable to rising temperatures and hurricanes, the Southwest to water shortages, coastal areas to flooding, and the Midwest to agricultural disruptions.
  • Marginalized communities are disproportionately affected by climate change.
  • Current efforts to reduce emissions and adapt to climate change are insufficient to avoid the most severe consequences.
  • The report calls for urgent and substantial action to mitigate greenhouse gas emissions and build resilience to climate change impacts.
