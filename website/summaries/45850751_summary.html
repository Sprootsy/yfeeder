VLC's Jean-Baptiste Kempf Receives the European SFS Award 2025

Summary

The Free Software Foundation Europe (FSFE) article discusses the upcoming deadline for compliance with the Radio Equipment Directive (RED) 2014/53/EU regarding the installation of radio equipment with the possibility to install software. As of December 28, 2024, manufacturers placing radio equipment on the market in the EU must ensure compliance with Article 3(3)(i) of the RED, which aims to ensure that software controlling the radio aspects of the equipment can be installed by different parties than the manufacturer.

The FSFE highlights that this requirement is a significant step towards empowering users and promoting Free Software. It enables users to install and modify the software on their radio equipment, fostering innovation, competition, and security. The article emphasizes that this compliance is not just about adhering to a regulation, but about enabling user freedom and promoting a more open and democratic technological landscape.

The article details the requirements of Article 3(3)(i), explaining that radio equipment must support the installation of software by parties other than the manufacturer, which allows for community-driven development and customization. This includes the possibility for users to install alternative operating systems or software stacks that better suit their needs or address specific security concerns. The FSFE stresses that the practical implementation of this directive is crucial and that manufacturers need to provide the necessary technical information and interfaces to facilitate software installation.

Furthermore, the FSFE acknowledges the potential challenges in implementing the directive but points out that the benefits of compliance far outweigh the difficulties. Compliance fosters innovation, as it enables independent developers and researchers to experiment with radio technology and create new applications. It also enhances security, as it allows security experts to audit and improve the software running on radio equipment.

The FSFE calls on manufacturers to take the necessary steps to ensure compliance with the RED by the deadline. They also urge policymakers to actively enforce the directive and ensure that manufacturers are held accountable. The FSFE encourages users and Free Software developers to engage with manufacturers and demand compliance, emphasizing that their involvement is essential to ensuring the successful implementation of Article 3(3)(i).

Finally, the FSFE emphasizes that compliance with the RED is not just a one-time event but an ongoing process. Manufacturers need to continuously update their products and processes to ensure that they remain compliant with the directive as technology evolves. The FSFE remains committed to working with manufacturers, policymakers, and users to promote compliance and ensure that the benefits of Free Software are realized in the radio equipment market.

Key points

  • The Radio Equipment Directive (RED) 2014/53/EU requires radio equipment to allow software installation by third parties.
  • Article 3(3)(i) of the RED mandates this capability for radio equipment placed on the market after December 28, 2024.
  • This regulation empowers users and promotes Free Software by enabling them to modify and customize their devices.
  • Compliance fosters innovation, competition, and security in the radio equipment market.
  • Manufacturers must provide the technical means for users to install alternative software.
  • The FSFE urges manufacturers to comply with the directive and policymakers to enforce it.
  • Users and developers are encouraged to demand compliance from manufacturers.
  • Compliance is an ongoing process that requires continuous updates and adaptation.
