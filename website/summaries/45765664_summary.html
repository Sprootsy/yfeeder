Denmark reportedly withdraws Chat Control proposal following controversy

According to The Record, Denmark has reportedly withdrawn its support for the European Union's proposed "chat control" legislation. This legislation aimed to combat child sexual abuse material (CSAM) online. Concerns regarding privacy and the potential for mass surveillance have been raised by critics. Denmark's withdrawal follows similar reservations expressed by other EU member states, signaling a growing resistance to the proposal in its current form.
