Arizona resident dies from the plague less than 24 hours after showing symptoms

Summary:

A county in Arizona has reported its first human case of the plague this year, which resulted in the death of the infected individual. Health officials in Yavapai County confirmed the case and are investigating where the person contracted the disease. They are also taking preventative measures by inspecting the area around the person's residence for fleas and testing any animals that may have been exposed.

The plague is a bacterial disease that can be transmitted to humans through infected fleas, rodents, and other animals. Symptoms of the plague can include fever, headache, weakness, swollen lymph nodes, and other flu-like symptoms. The disease can be treated with antibiotics, but it can be fatal if left untreated. Health officials are urging residents to take precautions to avoid contact with rodents and fleas, such as avoiding rodent habitats, wearing protective clothing, and using insect repellent.

Yavapai County health officials are working to prevent the spread of the plague by educating the public about the disease and how to protect themselves. They are also working with local veterinarians to monitor animals for signs of the plague.
