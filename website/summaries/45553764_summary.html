Google blocks Android hack that let Pixel users enable VoLTE anywhere

Summary:

The article discusses an issue affecting some Google Pixel users after installing the October 2023 Android update. The issue revolves around a broken IMS (IP Multimedia Subsystem) which impacts core phone functionalities. Specifically, users are reporting problems with making and receiving phone calls, sending and receiving SMS messages, and in some cases, even accessing mobile data. The problem seems to be triggered by the update and is not universal, affecting different Pixel models and carriers seemingly at random.

The article details the symptoms experienced by affected users, highlighting that basic communication features become unreliable or completely non-functional. This disruption severely limits the usability of the Pixel phones as communication devices.

Android Authority notes that the issue is widespread enough to be acknowledged, and Google is reportedly aware of the problem and working on a fix. The article mentions that Google has requested bug reports and is actively investigating the root cause of the IMS failure.

While waiting for an official update, the article suggests some potential temporary workarounds for users experiencing these issues. These include toggling airplane mode, restarting the device, resetting network settings, and contacting their carrier for assistance. However, the effectiveness of these workarounds varies, and they are not guaranteed to resolve the problem permanently.

The article emphasizes the frustration and inconvenience caused by this bug, particularly for those who rely heavily on their Pixel phones for communication. It underscores the importance of Google providing a swift and effective solution to restore the affected functionalities. The situation is ongoing, and users are advised to monitor official channels for updates and fixes from Google.

Key Points:

  • October 2023 Android update is causing IMS issues on some Pixel phones.
  • IMS failure leads to problems with calls, SMS, and mobile data.
  • The issue is not universal, affecting different Pixel models and carriers.
  • Google is aware of the problem and investigating.
  • Temporary workarounds like toggling airplane mode, restarting the device, and resetting network settings may offer temporary relief.
  • Users are awaiting a permanent fix from Google.
