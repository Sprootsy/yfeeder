Link Khan: Activision-Blizzard buyout is 'harming both gamers and developers'

The article discusses the concerns raised by former FTC chair Lina Khan regarding Microsoft's acquisition of Activision Blizzard. It highlights that, following the acquisition, Microsoft has laid off thousands of employees and increased the prices of its Game Pass subscription service. Khan suggests that these actions validate the concerns raised during the FTC's attempt to block the acquisition, arguing that it would harm both gamers and developers. The article points out the layoffs affected various departments within Activision Blizzard and Microsoft's gaming divisions. The price hike for Game Pass has made the service more expensive for consumers. Khan's perspective is that the acquisition has led to negative consequences for the gaming industry, impacting employment and consumer costs, which aligns with the FTC's initial arguments against the deal. The piece serves as a commentary on the potential downsides of large tech acquisitions and their effects on the broader market.
