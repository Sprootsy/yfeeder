Vancouver Stock Exchange: Scam capital of the world (1989) [pdf]

The article "Scam Capital" details Vancouver's reputation as a hub for various fraudulent schemes, focusing primarily on stock manipulation, boiler rooms, and related activities. It highlights how Vancouver's unique combination of factors, including a history of resource extraction, a lax regulatory environment, and a culture that sometimes tolerates questionable business practices, has contributed to its status as a haven for scammers. The article describes different types of scams, such as pump-and-dump schemes involving penny stocks traded on the TSX Venture Exchange. It also discusses the role of individuals and companies that facilitate these scams, including brokers, promoters, and shell companies. The author emphasizes the negative impact of these fraudulent activities on investors, the legitimate business community, and the reputation of Vancouver as a whole. The piece further explores the challenges in prosecuting these types of crimes, including jurisdictional issues and the complexity of financial fraud.

Key Points:

  • Vancouver has gained a reputation as a "Scam Capital" due to the prevalence of stock manipulation and other fraudulent schemes.
  • The TSX Venture Exchange is often used as a platform for pump-and-dump schemes involving penny stocks.
  • Boiler rooms, staffed by high-pressure salespeople, are used to aggressively promote these stocks to unsuspecting investors.
  • A lax regulatory environment and a culture that sometimes tolerates questionable business practices contribute to the problem.
  • These scams have a negative impact on investors, the legitimate business community, and Vancouver's reputation.
  • Prosecuting these crimes is challenging due to jurisdictional issues and the complexity of financial fraud.
