Internal emails reveal Ticketmaster helped scalpers jack up prices, FTC says

According to an FTC lawsuit, Ticketmaster, owned by Live Nation, has been intentionally harming fans by employing illegal and monopolistic practices. The lawsuit claims that Ticketmaster controls about 80% of major concert venues' ticketing, which allows them to impose excessive fees, hinder competition, and stifle innovation in the ticketing industry. The FTC alleges that Live Nation uses its dominance to prevent venues from using other ticketing services by threatening to withhold Live Nation-controlled concerts. This alleged monopolistic control has led to fans paying billions of dollars in overcharges. Additionally, the lawsuit points out that Live Nation's behavior violates a 2010 consent decree established after Live Nation merged with Ticketmaster. The FTC is seeking structural remedies that may include breaking up Live Nation and Ticketmaster to restore competition.

Key points:

  • The FTC is suing Ticketmaster and Live Nation for allegedly operating an illegal monopoly in the live event ticketing market.
  • Ticketmaster controls approximately 80% of ticketing for major concert venues.
  • Live Nation allegedly uses its market dominance to prevent venues from using competing ticketing services.
  • The FTC claims these practices have resulted in fans being overcharged billions of dollars.
  • The lawsuit alleges that Live Nation's behavior violates a 2010 consent decree.
  • The FTC is seeking remedies that could include breaking up Live Nation and Ticketmaster.
