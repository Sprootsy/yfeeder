Auth.js is now part of Better Auth

Summary:

The article announces that Auth.js, an open-source authentication library for web applications, has joined Better Auth, a company focused on providing authentication and user management solutions. This collaboration aims to enhance the capabilities of both platforms and provide developers with more comprehensive and user-friendly authentication tools.

Auth.js is a popular library that simplifies the process of adding authentication to web applications, offering support for various authentication providers and strategies. Better Auth, on the other hand, provides a broader range of authentication and user management features, including things like, multi-factor authentication (MFA), enterprise federation, and user provisioning. By bringing Auth.js into the Better Auth ecosystem, the companies aim to offer developers a one-stop-shop for all their authentication needs.

The acquisition will see the Auth.js project benefit from Better Auth's resources and expertise, enabling it to grow and evolve further. At the same time, Better Auth will integrate Auth.js's capabilities into its platform, giving its users access to a wider range of authentication options and making it easier for them to integrate authentication into their applications.

The article also mentions that the Auth.js project will remain open-source and community-driven. Better Auth is committed to supporting the Auth.js community and ensuring that the project continues to thrive. The collaboration between Auth.js and Better Auth is expected to result in a more powerful and versatile authentication solution for developers, making it easier for them to secure their web applications and manage their users.
