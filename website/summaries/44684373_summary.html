Women dating safety app 'Tea' breached, users' IDs posted to 4chan

A dating safety app called Tea, marketed towards women, experienced a significant data breach that resulted in the exposure of users' personal information. The breach involved the unauthorized access and posting of sensitive data, including user IDs, to the online forum 4chan. Tea is designed to allow women to share information and experiences about their dates. The app's privacy policy stated that user data would be encrypted and inaccessible to the company itself, however this proved to be false. The exposed user IDs could potentially be used to identify individuals and piece together their app activity.
