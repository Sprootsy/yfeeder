Layoffs hit highest level for the month since 2003

Summary:

According to the October 2023 Challenger Report, U.S.-based employers announced 153,074 job cuts in October, which is a significant increase compared to the 33,843 cuts announced in September and a 417% increase from the 29,625 cuts announced in October 2022. This marks the highest monthly total since January 2023, when 102,943 cuts were recorded. The report indicates that cost-cutting was the primary reason cited for the job cuts, followed by business closures. The technology sector continues to be heavily impacted, with 42,867 job cuts, marking a 1,235% increase compared to the previous year. The transportation industry experienced a notable increase in job cuts as well. The report also points out that companies are increasingly citing artificial intelligence as a reason for job cuts, with 3,933 cuts attributed to AI this year. Hiring announcements were also tracked, with 13,743 hiring plans announced in October, bringing the year-to-date total to 117,385.

Key Points:

  • October 2023 saw 153,074 job cuts announced by U.S. employers, the highest monthly total since January 2023.
  • This represents a 417% increase compared to October 2022.
  • Cost-cutting is the top reason cited for job cuts.
  • The technology sector continues to be heavily impacted by job cuts.
  • Artificial intelligence is increasingly cited as a reason for job cuts, with 3,933 cuts attributed to AI this year.
  • Hiring announcements totaled 13,743 in October.
