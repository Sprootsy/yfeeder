This article discusses the potential sale of Paramount Global and the various parties interested in acquiring the media conglomerate. Shari Redstone, who controls Paramount Global through National Amusements, is reportedly considering offers for the company. David Ellison's Skydance Media is presented as a leading contender, having been in talks with Paramount Global for some time. Ellison's plan involves merging Skydance with Paramount, with financial backing from firms like RedBird Capital, KKR, and potentially even Sony.

Another potential bidder mentioned is Apollo Global Management, which reportedly made a $26 billion offer for all of Paramount Global. However, there are complexities surrounding Apollo's bid, including potential regulatory hurdles due to its ownership of television stations and concerns about its plans for Paramount's assets. Reports suggest that Apollo might be open to partnering with another company, such as Sony, to overcome these obstacles.

The article explores the challenges and considerations involved in selling Paramount Global, including the declining linear TV business, the need to invest in streaming, and the significant debt held by the company. The special committee formed by Paramount's board is tasked with evaluating the offers and determining the best path forward for the company and its shareholders.

The article also touches upon the potential roles of key figures like Bob Bakish, the CEO of Paramount Global, and the importance of securing the best possible deal for Shari Redstone and National Amusements. The future of Paramount's various assets, including Paramount Pictures, CBS, and its streaming services, hangs in the balance as the bidding process unfolds.
