Apple announces American Manufacturing Program

Apple announced an increase in its US investment commitment to $600 billion over the next five years. This represents a $150 billion increase from its previous commitment. The investment will focus on several key areas:

Innovation Through R&D: Apple is driving American innovation through research and development (R&D) spending. A significant portion of the $600 billion commitment will be directed toward this, supporting jobs in fields like silicon engineering, 5G, and software development.

Job Creation: The investment will support job creation across the country, directly employing over 2 million Americans. This includes direct Apple employees, employees at US suppliers and manufacturers, and developers in the App Store ecosystem.

Supporting American Suppliers: Apple is significantly increasing its spending with American suppliers and manufacturers. The company says it supports hundreds of thousands of jobs by sourcing parts and materials from over 9,000 domestic suppliers across all 50 states.

App Store Ecosystem: The App Store continues to be a major engine for economic growth, supporting millions of jobs for app developers. Apple will invest in programs and resources to help developers create innovative apps and reach customers around the world.

Data Centers: Apple continues to invest in data centers across the US, which are powered by 100% renewable energy. These data centers support Apple's services and help to ensure the privacy and security of user data.

Other Investments: Apple has made direct investments in numerous companies across the country. Apple also supports educational initiatives and other programs in communities across the country.

Key Points:

  • Apple is increasing its US investment commitment to $600 billion over the next five years.
  • The investment will focus on R&D, job creation, supporting American suppliers, and the App Store ecosystem.
  • Apple directly employs over 2 million Americans and supports jobs at US suppliers and manufacturers.
  • The App Store is a major engine for economic growth, supporting millions of jobs for app developers.
  • Apple continues to invest in US data centers that are powered by 100% renewable energy.
