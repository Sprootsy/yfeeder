10k pushups and other silly exercise quests that changed my life

Summary:

The article "10,000 Pushups" by W.J. Gilmore documents the author's personal challenge to complete 10,000 pushups in a month. Gilmore details his initial struggles, the evolution of his workout routine, and the mental and physical benefits he experienced throughout the challenge. Initially, Gilmore's approach was unstructured, leading to muscle soreness and inconsistency. He quickly realized the need for a more systematic plan. He began breaking down the total number of pushups into daily targets, further dividing these into smaller, more manageable sets spread throughout the day. He experimented with different set and rep schemes, ultimately settling on a routine that allowed him to complete the required volume without excessive fatigue or risk of injury. Gilmore emphasizes the importance of consistency and discipline. He highlights how the challenge forced him to incorporate pushups into his daily routine, fitting them in during various moments, such as before meals or during breaks from work. He also discusses the mental aspect of the challenge, noting how it improved his focus, determination, and overall sense of accomplishment. He found that completing the pushups each day instilled a sense of discipline that carried over into other areas of his life. The article also touches upon the physical benefits Gilmore observed. While he didn't focus on building massive muscle gains, he noticed improvements in his upper body strength, endurance, and muscle definition. He also mentions the importance of proper form to avoid injuries and maximize the effectiveness of the exercise. Moreover, Gilmore reflects on the lessons learned from the experience. He emphasizes the value of setting achievable goals, breaking down large tasks into smaller steps, and maintaining consistency. He also highlights the mental fortitude required to overcome challenges and the satisfaction that comes from achieving a difficult goal. The 10,000 pushups challenge became a metaphor for tackling other obstacles in life, demonstrating the power of perseverance and a structured approach. The author notes that it's not about the pushups themselves, but about the discipline and mental strength gained along the way.
