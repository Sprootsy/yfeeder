Dropbox Passwords discontinuation

Dropbox is discontinuing the Dropbox Passwords app. The app will no longer be available to download as of April 3, 2024, and the app will stop syncing and be inaccessible on May 2, 2024. Users are advised to export their passwords before May 2, 2024.

Summary

Dropbox is discontinuing its password management application, Dropbox Passwords. The announcement details that the app will no longer be available for download starting April 3, 2024. More critically, the application will cease to function entirely on May 2, 2024. After this date, Dropbox Passwords will no longer sync data, and users will be unable to access their stored passwords within the app.

The core message of the announcement revolves around urging users to take immediate action to safeguard their password data. Since the app will become unusable, users must export their stored passwords before May 2, 2024, to avoid losing access to them. The provided information emphasizes the importance of this step, as users will not be able to retrieve their passwords through Dropbox Passwords after the discontinuation date.

The announcement serves as a notification and a call to action. Users who rely on Dropbox Passwords for their password management need to find an alternative solution and migrate their data to that new platform before the deadline. The information is clear: the app is being retired, and users are responsible for ensuring the continued accessibility of their password information.

Key Points

  • Dropbox Passwords is being discontinued.
  • The app will no longer be available for download on April 3, 2024.
  • The app will stop syncing and become inaccessible on May 2, 2024.
  • Users must export their passwords before May 2, 2024.
