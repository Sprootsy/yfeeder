The Chan-Zuckerbergs stopped funding social causes

This article discusses the closure of The Primary School, a network of schools founded by Mark Zuckerberg and Priscilla Chan, in East Palo Alto and Menlo Park, California. The closure is set for the end of the 2024-2025 school year. The Primary School was established in 2016 with the aim of integrating healthcare and education for low-income students. The schools served children from infancy through eighth grade.

The decision to close the schools was described as a difficult one. According to a statement, the closure will allow The Primary School to deepen its partnerships with other schools and community organizations in the Bay Area and across the country. The goal is to broaden the impact of its work.

The Primary School distinguished itself by providing on-site medical and mental health services in addition to traditional education. It aimed to address the various challenges that can impact a child's ability to learn. The school's approach was rooted in the belief that a child's well-being is closely linked to their academic success.

Despite the closure of the physical schools, the organization intends to continue its work by focusing on sharing its model and resources with other educational institutions and community programs. The details of these partnerships are not fully detailed in the article.

The news of the closure has understandably generated varied reactions from the community, with some expressing disappointment over the loss of a valuable resource. Others are interested in seeing how The Primary School's knowledge and resources will be used to benefit other schools and organizations.

The article emphasizes that while the physical schools are closing, the mission of The Primary School will continue through collaborative efforts with other institutions.
