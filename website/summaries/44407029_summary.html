Sinaloa cartel used phone data and surveillance cameras to find FBI informants

Summary

According to the U.S. Justice Department, the Sinaloa cartel, one of the most powerful drug trafficking organizations in the world, has been using sophisticated methods to maintain its operations and evade law enforcement. These methods include hacking into phones and surveillance cameras to identify and target FBI informants. The cartel's cyber capabilities have allowed them to monitor communications, track individuals, and gain insights into law enforcement activities, giving them a significant advantage in their illicit operations. This was revealed in a recent case where a Sinaloa cartel member pleaded guilty to money laundering and drug trafficking charges. Court documents revealed the extent of the cartel's technological capabilities.

Key Points

  • The Sinaloa cartel is hacking phones and surveillance cameras.
  • The cartel is using these methods to identify FBI informants.
  • The Justice Department revealed the cartel's cyber capabilities in a recent case.
  • The cartel's methods give them an advantage in illicit operations.
  • A Sinaloa cartel member pleaded guilty to money laundering and drug trafficking charges.
