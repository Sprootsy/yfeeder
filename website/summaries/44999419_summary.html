Good EU regulations

Summary

Actually Good Regulations is a platform dedicated to promoting and advocating for well-designed and effective regulations within the European Union. It emphasizes that regulations, when crafted thoughtfully, can be a powerful tool for achieving positive outcomes across various sectors. The platform aims to foster a deeper understanding of what constitutes "good" regulation and how it can contribute to a better society, environment, and economy.

The core philosophy of Actually Good Regulations revolves around the idea that regulations are not inherently negative or burdensome. Instead, they can be enablers of innovation, protectors of public health and safety, and drivers of sustainable development. The platform highlights the importance of evidence-based policymaking, stakeholder engagement, and transparent processes in the creation and implementation of regulations. It also advocates for regulations that are proportionate, targeted, and adaptable to changing circumstances.

Actually Good Regulations addresses various aspects of the regulatory process, including the initial design phase, the consultation process, the implementation phase, and the evaluation of regulatory outcomes. It stresses the need for regulations to be clear, unambiguous, and easily accessible to those who are affected by them. Furthermore, the platform emphasizes the importance of minimizing unintended consequences and avoiding unnecessary burdens on businesses and individuals.

The platform serves as a resource for policymakers, businesses, civil society organizations, and citizens who are interested in improving the quality of regulation in the EU. It provides information, analysis, and best practices on a wide range of regulatory topics, including environmental protection, consumer protection, data protection, financial regulation, and competition policy. It also features case studies of successful regulations and examples of regulatory failures, with the aim of drawing lessons and informing future regulatory efforts.

Actually Good Regulations actively promotes dialogue and collaboration among different stakeholders in the regulatory process. It organizes events, workshops, and online forums to facilitate the exchange of ideas and experiences. The platform also encourages the public to participate in the regulatory process by providing opportunities to comment on proposed regulations and to share their views with policymakers. By fostering greater transparency and participation, Actually Good Regulations seeks to ensure that regulations are more responsive to the needs and concerns of society.

The ultimate goal of Actually Good Regulations is to contribute to a more effective and accountable regulatory system in the EU, one that promotes innovation, protects fundamental rights, and enhances the well-being of all citizens. By championing the principles of good regulation, the platform aims to help create a more prosperous, sustainable, and equitable future for Europe.
