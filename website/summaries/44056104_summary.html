Rocky Linux 10 Will Support RISC-V

The article announces the general availability of Rocky Linux support for the RISC-V architecture. This includes cloud images and packages for the community, and is available across all supported regions. This milestone marks a significant expansion of Rocky Linux's support for diverse architectures, providing the community with greater flexibility and choice.

The RISC-V port was made possible through the collaboration with several organizations and individuals within the Rocky Linux community. Key contributions and support came from Ampere Computing, Aliyun, IHEP (Institute of High Energy Physics, Chinese Academy of Sciences), and others who provided infrastructure, hardware, and expertise.

The availability of Rocky Linux on RISC-V aims to address the increasing demand for open-source operating systems on this architecture. RISC-V, being an open and modular instruction set architecture (ISA), offers significant advantages in terms of customization, control, and innovation. This makes it attractive for a wide range of applications, from embedded systems to high-performance computing.

The announcement details how users can access the RISC-V builds of Rocky Linux, including information on cloud images and package availability. Users can now deploy Rocky Linux on RISC-V hardware, leveraging the stability and reliability of the distribution.

The article also highlights the ongoing efforts and future plans for the RISC-V port. This includes continued optimization, support for additional hardware platforms, and further integration with the Rocky Linux ecosystem. The community is encouraged to contribute to the RISC-V effort through testing, development, and providing feedback.

Overall, the release of Rocky Linux for RISC-V represents a significant step forward in the adoption of RISC-V.
