Bluesky Goes Dark in Mississippi over Age Verification Law

Bluesky, a social media platform, has temporarily disabled sign-ups in Mississippi due to concerns about complying with the state's new age verification law, HB 1172, which is set to take effect on July 1, 2024. The law mandates that social media companies use third-party vendors to verify the age of Mississippi users and obtain parental consent for minors under 18. Bluesky, which operates as a public-benefit limited liability company, has expressed concerns about the privacy implications, cost, and effectiveness of such age verification methods. The situation highlights the broader challenges that decentralized social media platforms face in navigating varying and potentially burdensome regulations across different jurisdictions.
