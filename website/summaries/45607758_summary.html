Gemini 3.0 spotted in the wild through A/B testing

The article is a blog post by Rick Lamers discussing the observation of what appears to be Gemini 1.5 Pro in the wild, specifically being used within Google's AI Studio. The author details how they discovered this new model while experimenting with the AI Studio platform and noticed the model name 'gemini-1.5-pro-0415' appearing in the network requests. This suggests that Google is actively testing or deploying the Gemini 1.5 Pro model, even though it hasn't been officially released to the general public yet.

The author was experimenting with different prompts, including intentionally problematic ones, to evaluate the model's capabilities. They provide examples of the prompts used and the responses received. The responses suggest a higher level of sophistication and understanding compared to previous models. This is evidenced by the model's ability to follow complex instructions, and exhibit reasoning capabilities.

Further analysis of the network requests revealed specific parameters and endpoints related to the Gemini 1.5 Pro model. Lamers notes that the model appears to have a larger context window, enabling it to handle more extensive inputs and generate more detailed and contextually relevant outputs. The author also delves into the technical aspects of interacting with the model, including the API endpoints and request formats used by Google AI Studio.

The author emphasizes that while this discovery provides valuable insights into Google's AI development efforts, there are still many unknowns regarding the full capabilities and intended use cases of Gemini 1.5 Pro. The post acknowledges that Google may be intentionally limiting access to the model for testing and evaluation purposes. The author also discusses the ethical considerations associated with advanced AI models and the importance of responsible development and deployment.

The article concludes by highlighting the significance of these observations for the AI community. It suggests that Gemini 1.5 Pro represents a substantial advancement in language models and that its official release could have significant implications for various applications.
