The article "40k Baby" is a personal essay reflecting on the author's journey into fatherhood and how it parallels his long-standing passion for the tabletop war game Warhammer 40,000. The author uses the intricate and often overwhelming world of 40k as a metaphor for the anxieties and joys of becoming a parent.

This mirrors his feelings about impending parenthood - the feeling of being unprepared and facing an unknown, overwhelming challenge.

The author humorously recounts his past experiences with 40k, from painting miniatures to engaging in epic battles with friends. He reminisces about the time invested in building armies and learning the game's rules, drawing a parallel to the time and effort parents invest in raising a child. He recognizes the hobby as an all-consuming and at times, frustrating obsession. The author reflects on his time spent with his gaming group and the sense of friendship and community it provided, acknowledging that he had stepped away from the hobby to focus on other priorities. He uses the game as a framework for understanding the profound changes and responsibilities that come with fatherhood.

The author explores the theme of change. He acknowledges that his life, like a carefully planned 40k army, is about to be disrupted by the arrival of his child. He contemplates how his hobbies and routines will need to adapt to accommodate the needs of a new baby. However, he doesn't express solely apprehension; there's a sense of excitement and acceptance of the unknown.

The parallels between Warhammer 40,000 and parenthood are further drawn as the author delves into the idea of legacy. Just as players invest time and effort into building and painting their armies, parents invest in raising their children and shaping their future. He hopes to pass on certain values and experiences to his child, mirroring the way players introduce others to the game they love.

Ultimately, the article is about embracing the chaos and uncertainty of both Warhammer 40,000 and parenthood.
