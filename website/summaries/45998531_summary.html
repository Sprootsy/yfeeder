Gmail can read your emails and attachments to train its AI, unless you opt out

Summary

According to Malwarebytes, Gmail is utilizing user emails and attachments to train its AI models. This practice occurs without explicit user consent unless users actively opt out through their Google account settings. The data from emails, including sensitive and personal information, is being used to improve Google's AI capabilities, which raises privacy concerns among users. The article provides guidance on how to disable this data collection within Gmail settings to prevent personal data from being used for AI training purposes.

Key Points

  • Gmail is using user emails and attachments to train its AI models.
  • This data collection is enabled by default.
  • Users' personal and sensitive data is being used for AI training.
  • Users must manually opt out to prevent their data from being used.
  • The article provides instructions on how to disable this data collection in Gmail settings.
  • This practice raises privacy concerns.
