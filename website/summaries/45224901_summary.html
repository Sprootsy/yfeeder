An embarrassing failure of the US patent system: Nintendo's latest patents

According to a video game IP lawyer, Nintendo's recent patents regarding certain Pokémon mechanics represent a failure of the U.S. patent system. The lawyer argues that these patents, specifically related to aspects like Pokémon evolution and trading, should not have been granted. The core of the argument is that the patented mechanics were either already obvious or previously implemented in other games, and therefore lack the novelty required for patent protection. This raises concerns about the patent office's examination process and the potential for stifling innovation within the gaming industry by allowing companies to patent well-known or commonly used concepts.

Key Points:

  • Nintendo received patents for Pokémon mechanics.
  • A video game IP lawyer believes these patents should not have been granted.
  • The lawyer argues the mechanics lack novelty and were either obvious or previously implemented.
  • This raises concerns about the US patent system's examination process.
  • The lawyer suggests that allowing patents on well-known concepts could stifle innovation in the gaming industry.
