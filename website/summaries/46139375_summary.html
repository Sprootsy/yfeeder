After nearly 30 years, Crucial will stop selling RAM to consumers

Crucial, a brand of Micron, will stop selling RAM directly to consumers after nearly 30 years. The company has been a major player in the consumer RAM market, offering memory modules popular among PC builders and upgraders. According to a statement released by Crucial, this decision will allow Micron to focus on its core business of manufacturing memory chips. Micron will continue to produce DRAM and NAND flash memory, which it will then sell to other companies that will assemble and market the memory modules to consumers.

Crucial's exit from the consumer RAM market marks the end of an era for many PC enthusiasts who have relied on the brand for affordable and reliable memory solutions. The move is part of a broader strategy shift at Micron, which aims to streamline its operations and concentrate on its strengths in memory chip production. The company believes that by focusing on its core competencies, it can better serve the overall memory market and drive innovation.

Micron has assured customers that existing Crucial RAM products will continue to be supported through their warranty periods. The company will also work with its existing partners to ensure a smooth transition for consumers who have come to rely on Crucial RAM.
