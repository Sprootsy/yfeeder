The German automotive industry wants to develop open-source software together

Summary

The German Association of the Automotive Industry (VDA) announced that leading automotive manufacturers, suppliers, and tech companies have signed a Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) to establish the Catena-X Automotive Network as a legally independent company by the end of 2024. This initiative aims to create a standardized, open, and secure data ecosystem across the automotive value chain.

Catena-X seeks to enable seamless data exchange among all players in the automotive industry, fostering collaboration and innovation. The network will facilitate various use cases, including quality management, supply chain optimization, and sustainable manufacturing processes. By establishing a common data infrastructure, companies can improve efficiency, reduce costs, and enhance the resilience of their operations.

The MoU signifies a commitment from industry stakeholders to contribute to the development and implementation of Catena-X. The goal is to enhance the competitiveness of the automotive industry by leveraging the power of data and fostering a collaborative environment. The new company will be responsible for governing the network, setting standards, and ensuring data security and sovereignty. By the end of 2024, the legal framework for this company should be in place, marking a significant milestone in the digital transformation of the automotive sector.
