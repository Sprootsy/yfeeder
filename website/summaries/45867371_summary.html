Summary

A new study has found that human intelligence is not declining, contrary to some previous research suggesting a "reverse Flynn effect." The study, encompassing over a million participants across four decades, indicates that while specific cognitive skills may have changed, overall intelligence has remained stable. Researchers analyzed data from intelligence tests conducted in the UK, the US, and Australia between 1970 and 2010. The results showed that while performance in some areas, such as logical reasoning, declined, other areas, such as spatial reasoning, improved. These shifts suggest that cognitive abilities are evolving rather than declining, possibly due to changes in educational practices and societal demands. The research challenges the notion that modern society is leading to a decrease in intellectual capacity and highlights the adaptability of human cognition in response to environmental changes. This is in part due to society placing more emphasis on these skills.

Key Points

  • Human intelligence is not declining, challenging the "reverse Flynn effect" theory.
  • The study analyzed intelligence test data from over a million participants across four decades (1970-2010) in the UK, US, and Australia.
  • While performance in specific cognitive skills like logical reasoning declined, others like spatial reasoning improved.
  • These shifts indicate an evolution of cognitive abilities rather than a decline in overall intelligence.
  • Changes in educational practices and societal demands may be contributing to the observed shifts in cognitive skills.
  • The research suggests human cognition is adaptable to environmental changes.
