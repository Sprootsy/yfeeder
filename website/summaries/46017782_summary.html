Markdown Is Holding You Back

The article argues that while Markdown is widely used and generally considered simple, it has several limitations that hinder its potential as a writing and publishing tool. The author contends that Markdown's simplicity comes at the cost of expressiveness and extensibility, leading to inconsistencies and a fragmented ecosystem. Different Markdown implementations, like CommonMark, GitHub Flavored Markdown, and others, offer varying features, creating compatibility issues and making it difficult to create truly portable documents.

The article highlights the lack of a formal specification and the reliance on regular expressions for parsing as contributing factors to these inconsistencies. The author also criticizes Markdown's limited support for complex formatting, such as tables and mathematical equations, often requiring extensions or HTML workarounds. Furthermore, the author suggests that Markdown's focus on plain text readability limits its ability to handle more structured content, such as metadata and semantic information.

The article then proposes AsciiDoc as a potential alternative. AsciiDoc is presented as a more powerful and feature-rich markup language that addresses many of Markdown's shortcomings. It offers a formal specification, a more robust parsing model, and built-in support for complex formatting and structured content. The author emphasizes AsciiDoc's extensibility, allowing users to customize and extend the language to meet their specific needs. AsciiDoc also supports features like cross-references, bibliographies, and semantic markup, which are difficult or impossible to achieve in Markdown without resorting to hacks or extensions.

The article acknowledges that AsciiDoc has a steeper learning curve than Markdown. However, the author argues that the increased power and flexibility of AsciiDoc are worth the investment for those who need a more capable markup language. The article concludes by encouraging readers to consider AsciiDoc as a viable alternative to Markdown, especially for projects that require more than basic formatting and structured content.

Key Points:

  • Markdown's simplicity leads to limitations in expressiveness and extensibility.
  • Inconsistencies arise due to the lack of a formal specification and reliance on regular expressions.
  • Markdown has limited support for complex formatting and structured content.
  • AsciiDoc is presented as a more powerful alternative with a formal specification and robust parsing.
  • AsciiDoc offers built-in support for complex formatting, structured content, and extensibility.
  • AsciiDoc supports features like cross-references and bibliographies that are difficult to achieve in Markdown.
  • AsciiDoc has a steeper learning curve, but its power and flexibility may be worth the investment.
