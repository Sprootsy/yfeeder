When a stadium adds AI to everything, it's worse experience for everyone

Summary:

The article discusses the integration of Artificial Intelligence (AI) at BMO Stadium in Los Angeles and argues that it has led to a worse experience for everyone. It focuses on how AI was implemented to optimize operations, from parking and concessions to security and crowd management, but instead created a tech-reliant environment that increased inefficiency. The author presents evidence of increased wait times, technological glitches, and an overall impersonal atmosphere. The article criticizes the move towards AI-driven solutions in contexts where human interaction and flexibility are crucial for a positive user experience, such as at live events. The author believes that BMO stadium, in pursuit of a tech-utopian ideal, diminished the actual experience for fans, staff, and ultimately, the community it serves.

Key Points:

  • BMO Stadium in Los Angeles implemented AI across various operations, including parking, concessions, security, and crowd management.
  • The introduction of AI led to increased inefficiency, longer wait times, and technological glitches.
  • The author criticizes the impersonal atmosphere created by the over-reliance on AI, diminishing the human element crucial for live event experiences.
  • The author argues that AI integration, intended to optimize and improve the experience, has instead worsened it for fans, staff, and the broader community.
  • The article suggests that AI is not always the optimal solution, particularly in contexts where human interaction and adaptability are paramount.
