However, it acknowledges the increasing pressure on tech workers in competitive environments like Silicon Valley, driven by factors such as ambitious deadlines, the desire to impress investors, and the influence of companies with international operations where 996 is more normalized.

The article presents perspectives from tech workers in San Francisco, showcasing a range of experiences. Some report occasionally working long hours to meet project goals, while others express concerns about the potential for burnout and the impact on work-life balance. The perspectives reveal that while the strict 996 schedule is not universally adopted, the pressure to work long hours and remain constantly connected can be significant.

Furthermore, the article notes that the discussion around 996 has increased due to greater awareness of work-life balance and mental health.

Finally, the article concludes by emphasizing the importance of open communication between employers and employees regarding work expectations and the need for companies to prioritize employee well-being and sustainable work practices.
