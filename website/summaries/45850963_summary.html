YouTube Removes Windows 11 Bypass Tutorials, Claims 'Risk of Physical Harm'

The article reports that YouTube has removed numerous videos providing tutorials on how to bypass Windows 11's hardware requirements, specifically the TPM 2.0 and Secure Boot requirements. These videos demonstrated methods for installing Windows 11 on older or unsupported hardware. The takedowns were reportedly due to copyright claims filed by Microsoft. While the exact legal justification for the copyright claims is unclear, it appears Microsoft is asserting copyright over elements within the Windows 11 installation process that are being modified or circumvented in these bypass methods. The removal of these videos makes it more difficult for users with older PCs to find instructions on installing Windows 11 without meeting the official hardware specifications. The article suggests that these actions by YouTube are a result of Microsoft's efforts to enforce its hardware requirements for Windows 11.

Key Points:

  • YouTube has removed many videos demonstrating how to bypass Windows 11 hardware requirements (TPM 2.0 and Secure Boot).
  • The removals are reportedly due to copyright claims made by Microsoft.
  • The legal basis for Microsoft's copyright claims is not explicitly stated, but it likely pertains to unauthorized modification of the Windows 11 installation process.
  • The removal of these videos makes it harder for users with unsupported hardware to install Windows 11.
  • Microsoft appears to be actively enforcing its hardware requirements for Windows 11.
