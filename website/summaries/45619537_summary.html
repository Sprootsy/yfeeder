Claude Skills are awesome, maybe a bigger deal than MCP

The article is a blog post by Simon Willison discussing his experiences and observations regarding the AI model Claude, particularly its strengths and weaknesses compared to other models like GPT-4. He highlights Claude's impressive ability to follow complex, multi-stage instructions and its consistent adherence to constraints, making it suitable for tasks requiring structured output or specific formats. Willison shares examples of using Claude for tasks such as generating SQL schemas from natural language descriptions and converting data into structured YAML formats. He notes that Claude excels at maintaining the specified format and avoiding deviations, even when faced with ambiguous or incomplete data. He contrasts this with GPT-4, which he finds more prone to making creative interpretations or ignoring constraints. The author also touches upon Claude's capacity for processing larger amounts of context, indicating its potential for handling more extensive and complex tasks. While acknowledging that Claude may sometimes require more explicit and detailed instructions compared to GPT-4, Willison emphasizes that its reliability in adhering to instructions and constraints makes it a valuable tool for specific applications where precision and structured output are paramount.

Key Points:

  • Claude excels at following complex, multi-stage instructions.
  • It is very good at consistently adhering to constraints and output formats.
  • It is useful for tasks requiring structured output like SQL schemas or YAML.
  • Claude is more reliable in adhering to instructions than GPT-4, which sometimes ignores constraints.
  • Claude can process large amounts of context.
  • It sometimes requires more explicit and detailed instructions than GPT-4.
  • Claude's strength lies in precision and structured output.
