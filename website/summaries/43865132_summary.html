Third Party Cookies Must Be Removed

This document outlines the web's transition away from third-party cookies and proposes alternative mechanisms to address various use cases, aiming to balance privacy with functionality. It acknowledges that third-party cookies, while historically useful for features like personalized advertising and single sign-on, have significant privacy implications and contribute to cross-site tracking, which users increasingly find unacceptable. The document details several key use cases that rely on third-party cookies and suggests potential replacements, primarily focusing on browser-mediated solutions that offer enhanced privacy controls and user agency.

The core use cases discussed include:

  • Personalized Advertising: The document explores ways to deliver relevant ads without pervasive tracking. Proposals include using aggregated, anonymized data, contextual advertising (basing ads on the content of the current page), and browser-supported privacy-preserving targeting mechanisms.
  • Conversion Measurement: Measuring the effectiveness of advertising campaigns without tracking individual users across the web is crucial. Solutions involve techniques like aggregated conversion reporting and privacy-preserving attribution methods.
  • Fraud Prevention: Identifying and preventing fraudulent activities like click fraud and bot traffic is essential for maintaining a healthy advertising ecosystem. The document suggests using aggregated signals and privacy-preserving techniques to detect and mitigate fraud.
  • Federated Login (Single Sign-On): Providing users with a seamless login experience across multiple websites without relying on third-party cookies is important for usability. The document proposes using browser-mediated identity solutions and standardized protocols that prioritize user privacy.
  • Embedded Content: Allowing content from different origins to be embedded within a website while respecting user privacy is a key consideration. The document discusses techniques like sandboxing and privacy-preserving communication mechanisms to enable secure and private embedded content.

The document emphasizes the importance of transparency, user control, and privacy-preserving techniques in all proposed solutions. It advocates for browser-mediated mechanisms that empower users to manage their privacy preferences and limit cross-site tracking. It also highlights the need for collaboration between browser vendors, advertisers, publishers, and other stakeholders to develop and implement these alternative solutions effectively. The overall goal is to create a web ecosystem that respects user privacy while still supporting essential functionalities and business models.

Key Points:

  • Third-party cookies are being phased out due to privacy concerns.
  • The document proposes alternative solutions for key use cases like personalized advertising, conversion measurement, fraud prevention, federated login, and embedded content.
  • Solutions should prioritize user privacy, transparency, and control.
  • Browser-mediated mechanisms are favored to empower users and limit cross-site tracking.
  • Collaboration between stakeholders is essential for successful implementation.
  • The goal is to balance privacy with functionality and sustainable business models.
