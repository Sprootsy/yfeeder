Gaming on Linux has never been more approachable

This article is a first-person account of switching from Windows to the CachyOS Linux distribution for gaming. The author details their initial hesitation and concerns about compatibility and performance, particularly with anti-cheat software in games like Destiny 2. They outline the steps taken to install CachyOS, highlighting its user-friendly installer and the inclusion of necessary drivers and software. The author then delves into the experience of gaming on CachyOS, noting that most games worked out-of-the-box thanks to Steam's Proton compatibility layer. They specifically mention successful gameplay in titles such as Cyberpunk 2077, Elden Ring, and Horizon: Zero Dawn. Performance comparisons are drawn, with the author reporting similar or even slightly improved frame rates in some games compared to their previous Windows setup. The author also discusses the troubleshooting process, including dealing with games that required specific Proton versions or launch options. One notable challenge was getting Destiny 2 to run, which required a workaround involving disabling anti-cheat measures. The author expresses satisfaction with the overall experience, noting the improved performance, smoother gameplay, and customizable nature of CachyOS. They acknowledge a few minor issues and the need for occasional tweaking, but ultimately conclude that CachyOS is a viable and enjoyable operating system for gaming, even for users who are not deeply familiar with Linux. The article serves as a practical guide and personal endorsement for those considering making the switch to Linux for gaming.

Key Points:

  • The author switched from Windows to CachyOS Linux for gaming.
  • Initial concerns included compatibility issues, especially with anti-cheat software.
  • CachyOS installation was straightforward due to its user-friendly installer.
