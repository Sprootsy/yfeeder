Meta appoints anti-LGBTQ+ conspiracy theorist Robby Starbuck as AI bias advisor

According to PinkNews, Meta has removed a deepfake video of trans activist Robby Starbuck from its platforms, Facebook and Instagram. The video was created using artificial intelligence (AI) and depicted Starbuck making false and defamatory statements. Starbuck, known for his anti-transgender activism, confirmed that the video was fake and that he never made the statements attributed to him in the AI-generated video.

Meta's decision to remove the video followed a review by its content moderation team, which determined that the video violated the company's policies against defamation and the use of AI to create misleading content. The company has been under increasing pressure to address the growing threat of AI-generated misinformation, particularly deepfakes, which can be used to spread false information and damage reputations.

Starbuck expressed gratitude towards Meta for its prompt action in removing the video. He also used the incident to highlight what he believes are the dangers of AI technology and the potential for it to be used to create malicious content. He called on social media platforms to implement stricter regulations and safeguards to prevent the spread of AI-generated deepfakes.

This incident raises concerns about the ethical implications of AI and the potential for its misuse. As AI technology becomes more sophisticated, it is becoming increasingly difficult to distinguish between real and fake content. This poses a significant challenge for social media platforms, which are tasked with identifying and removing harmful content while also protecting free speech.

Meta's removal of the deepfake video of Robby Starbuck demonstrates the company's commitment to combating AI-generated misinformation. However, it also highlights the need for continued vigilance and innovation in the fight against deepfakes and other forms of AI-generated disinformation.

Key points:

  • Meta removed a deepfake video of Robby Starbuck from Facebook and Instagram.
  • The video contained false and defamatory statements attributed to Starbuck.
  • Starbuck confirmed the video was AI-generated and fake.
  • Meta removed the video for violating its policies against defamation and misleading AI content.
  • The incident highlights the dangers of AI-generated misinformation and deepfakes.
