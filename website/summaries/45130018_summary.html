The thousands of atomic bombs exploded on Earth (2015)

Summary:

The article, written in 2005, discusses the extensive history of nuclear weapons testing conducted throughout the 20th century. It highlights the sheer number of atomic bombs that have been detonated on Earth, primarily by nations like the United States and the Soviet Union, as well as the United Kingdom, France, and China. The piece emphasizes the environmental impact of these tests, pointing out the significant amount of radioactive fallout released into the atmosphere and the long-term consequences for the planet. The article also links to resources such as the Nuclear Weapons Archive, which provides detailed information about nuclear weapons and their testing, and the Comprehensive Nuclear-Test-Ban Treaty Organization (CTBTO), which works to prevent future nuclear explosions. In essence, the article serves as a reminder of the scale of nuclear testing during the Cold War and the lasting legacy of these actions.

Key Points:

  • A large number of atomic bombs have been detonated on Earth.
  • The United States and the Soviet Union conducted the majority of these tests.
  • Other nuclear powers such as the United Kingdom, France, and China also contributed to the testing.
  • Nuclear testing released significant amounts of radioactive fallout into the atmosphere.
  • These tests had a lasting environmental impact.
  • The Nuclear Weapons Archive provides detailed information about nuclear weapons and testing.
  • The Comprehensive Nuclear-Test-Ban Treaty Organization (CTBTO) works to prevent future nuclear explosions.
