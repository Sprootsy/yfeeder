M8.2 solar flare, Strong G4 geomagnetic storm watch

Summary:

On May 29, 2024, at 13:14 UTC, solar active region 3697 produced a significant M8.2 (R2 - Moderate) solar flare. This flare was associated with a Type II radio emission with an estimated speed of 1477 km/s and a Type IV radio emission, both indicative of a coronal mass ejection (CME). The CME is anticipated to reach Earth, prompting NOAA's Space Weather Prediction Center (SWPC) to issue a G4 (Severe) geomagnetic storm watch for June 1-2, 2024. This geomagnetic storm could potentially affect satellite operations, power grids, and radio communications. The article details the event's timeline, characteristics, and expected impacts, emphasizing the importance of monitoring space weather conditions.

Key Points:

  • M8.2 Solar Flare: Active region 3697 produced an M8.2 class solar flare on May 29, 2024, at 13:14 UTC.
  • Radio Emissions: The flare was associated with Type II and Type IV radio emissions, indicating a CME.
  • CME Speed: The Type II radio emission suggests the CME has a speed of approximately 1477 km/s.
  • G4 Geomagnetic Storm Watch: NOAA's SWPC has issued a G4 (Severe) geomagnetic storm watch for June 1-2, 2024, due to the anticipated arrival of the CME.
  • Potential Impacts: The geomagnetic storm could affect satellite operations, power grids, and radio communications.
  • Active Region: The flare originated from solar active region 3697.
