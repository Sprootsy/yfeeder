Summary

A recent poll conducted by Navigator Research reveals that a significant portion of Americans believe political violence is likely in the near future. The poll explores attitudes towards political violence, its potential triggers, and the factors contributing to this sentiment.

The poll indicates that a considerable number of Americans think political violence is at least somewhat likely in the next few years. The survey delves into the reasons behind these concerns, exploring the role of political division, distrust in institutions, and the spread of misinformation.

The research also investigates potential triggers for political violence, such as contested election results. The poll examines how different demographics view the likelihood of violence and what factors might exacerbate or mitigate the risk. The findings highlight the deep-seated anxieties within the American population regarding the stability of the political system and the potential for conflict.

Key Points

  • A significant number of Americans believe political violence is likely in the near future.
