People kept working, became healthier while on basic income: report (2020)

The article reports on a study conducted by McMaster University that analyzes the potential impacts of a basic income program in Hamilton, Ontario. The research, led by Dr. Lloyd Reynolds, explores how a basic income could affect various aspects of the city, including poverty reduction, health outcomes, employment, and the local economy. The study uses a microsimulation model to predict outcomes based on different basic income scenarios, considering factors like benefit levels, eligibility criteria, and clawback rates.

The report suggests that a basic income program could significantly reduce poverty rates in Hamilton, potentially lifting thousands of families above the poverty line. This poverty reduction is expected to have positive ripple effects on health, as improved financial stability can lead to better access to healthcare, healthier food choices, and reduced stress. The study also examines the potential impact on employment, noting that while some individuals may choose to work fewer hours, others may be empowered to pursue education or entrepreneurship, ultimately contributing to the economy in different ways.

Researchers emphasized that the design of the basic income program is critical in determining its effectiveness. Factors such as the level of income provided, the criteria for eligibility, and how the basic income interacts with existing social assistance programs can all influence the program's success. The study considered different scenarios with varying benefit levels and clawback rates to assess the potential trade-offs.

The report was commissioned by the Hamilton Roundtable for Poverty Reduction, highlighting the city's interest in exploring innovative solutions to address poverty. The findings are intended to inform policy discussions and contribute to a broader understanding of the potential benefits and challenges of implementing a basic income program. The research adds to the growing body of evidence on basic income and its potential to address poverty and improve social and economic outcomes.
