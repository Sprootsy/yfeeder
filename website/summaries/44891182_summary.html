U.S. alcohol consumption drops to a 90-year low, new poll finds

A recent Gallup poll reveals a significant shift in Americans' drinking habits, with a notable increase in teetotalers and a decrease in those who consider themselves drinkers. The survey, conducted annually for over 80 years, shows that 62% of Americans report consuming alcohol, while 38% abstain, marking the highest percentage of non-drinkers since 2001. This trend is particularly pronounced among younger adults aged 18-34, with 52% identifying as non-drinkers. In contrast, older adults aged 55 and over are more likely to drink, with 76% reporting alcohol consumption.

Among those who drink, the average number of alcoholic beverages consumed per week remains relatively stable at around four. However, there's a slight decrease in the preference for beer, with wine and spirits gaining popularity, especially among younger drinkers. When asked about their preferred alcoholic beverage, 31% of drinkers chose beer, 29% wine, and 37% liquor.

The poll also explores the perceived health effects of alcohol. While some believe alcohol is detrimental to health, others view it as beneficial, and a significant portion believes it has no impact. Opinions on alcohol's health effects are generally divided, with 34% believing it's bad for one's health, 16% considering it beneficial, and 47% thinking it has no effect.

Several factors may contribute to these changing trends, including increased awareness of the potential health risks associated with alcohol consumption, the rise of non-alcoholic beverage options, and shifting social norms. Additionally, economic factors and changing lifestyles could also play a role in Americans' evolving relationship with alcohol. The poll suggests a growing moderation in alcohol consumption, with a greater emphasis on mindful drinking and a decline in heavy drinking habits.

  • A Gallup poll shows an increase in the percentage of Americans who do not drink alcohol, reaching 38%, the highest since 2001.
  • Alcohol consumption is more prevalent among older adults (55+) compared to younger adults (18-34).
  • Among drinkers, the average weekly consumption is about four alcoholic beverages.
  • Preference for beer has slightly decreased, while wine and liquor are gaining popularity, especially among younger drinkers.
  • Opinions on the health effects of alcohol are divided, with a significant portion believing it has no impact on health.
  • Possible factors driving these trends include increased awareness of health risks, availability of non-alcoholic alternatives, and shifting social norms.
