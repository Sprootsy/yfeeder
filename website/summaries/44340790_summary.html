The article discusses a memo written by Steve Jobs in 1985, shortly after he was ousted from Apple. The memo, titled "The Next Step," outlines Jobs' vision for his new company, NeXT. The author reflects on the memo's significance and insights into Jobs' mindset at the time.

The author begins by describing the memo's historical context. Jobs had just been forced out of Apple, the company he co-founded, after a power struggle with then-CEO John Sculley. The memo represents Jobs' attempt to chart a new course and define his goals for NeXT. The author notes that the memo reveals Jobs' ambition and his desire to prove his continued relevance in the technology industry.

The article then delves into the memo's content. The primary goal for NeXT was building high-performance workstations for the education market. Jobs emphasized the importance of creating innovative and user-friendly technology, but also stressed the need for profitability. The memo outlines the target market, product strategy, and marketing approach for NeXT.

The author highlights Jobs' focus on software as a key differentiator for NeXT. While hardware was important, Jobs believed that software would be crucial in setting NeXT apart from its competitors. This emphasis on software foreshadowed Apple's later success with macOS and iOS. The memo also reveals Jobs' attention to detail and his insistence on quality in all aspects of the company's operations.

The author also points out that the memo reflects Jobs' management style. He was known for being demanding and uncompromising, and the memo provides evidence of his high expectations for his employees. Jobs also emphasized the importance of teamwork and collaboration. The article concludes by examining the memo's legacy. NeXT ultimately failed to achieve its initial goals as a workstation vendor. However, Apple acquired NeXT in 1997. NeXT's technology became the foundation for macOS, which revitalized Apple and helped the company become one of the most valuable in the world. The author argues that the memo offers valuable lessons for entrepreneurs and business leaders. It demonstrates the importance of having a clear vision, focusing on innovation, and being willing to take risks.
