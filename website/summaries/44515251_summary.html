Why you should delete WhatsApp and install Signal

Summary:

The article is a personal recommendation to delete WhatsApp and switch to Signal for messaging. The author expresses concerns about WhatsApp's privacy practices, particularly its association with Facebook (Meta) and the company's history of data collection and utilization. The author emphasizes Signal's focus on privacy and security, highlighting features like end-to-end encryption, open-source code, and its operation as a non-profit organization. The author contrasts this with WhatsApp's data collection policies and perceived privacy risks.

The author acknowledges the challenge of convincing others to switch messaging platforms due to the network effect, where people tend to use the platform their contacts are already on. Despite this hurdle, the author argues that the privacy benefits of Signal outweigh the inconvenience of convincing others to switch. The article advocates for users to take control of their data and prioritize privacy by choosing a more secure messaging app like Signal. The author argues the importance of not just accepting what is convenient, but what is best to protect data and privacy from organizations that are likely to abuse it.
