Become unbannable from your email

The article "Become Unbannable From Your Email (Gmail)" discusses strategies to minimize the risk of being banned from email services, particularly Gmail, and to recover access if a ban occurs. It emphasizes diversifying email infrastructure and adopting practices that enhance email deliverability and sender reputation.

The author begins by highlighting the potential consequences of losing access to an email account, particularly for individuals who rely on it for various online services and communications. They stress that while complete unbannability is impossible, proactive measures can significantly reduce the likelihood of a ban.

The core of the article revolves around several key recommendations:

Diversify Email Providers: The author advises against relying solely on a single email provider like Gmail. They suggest using multiple providers (e.g., ProtonMail, iCloud Mail, self-hosted solutions) to distribute risk. This way, if one account is banned, the user still has access to others.

Use Custom Domains: Instead of exclusively using email addresses provided by Gmail or other providers (e.g., @gmail.com), the article recommends acquiring custom domains and setting up email addresses associated with them (e.g., @yourdomain.com). This provides more control and portability, as the user can switch email providers without changing their core email address.

Implement Email Forwarding: The author advises setting up forwarding from a custom domain email address to the Gmail address (or other provider address). This way, all emails sent to the custom domain address are forwarded to the Gmail address. If the Gmail account is banned, the user can switch the forwarding to a new email address.

Warm Up New Email Addresses: Warming up an email address involves gradually increasing the volume of emails sent from a new address to establish a positive sender reputation with email providers. This helps prevent the email address from being flagged as spam.

Maintain Good Email Hygiene: Practices like avoiding sending spam, honoring unsubscribe requests promptly, and authenticating emails using SPF, DKIM, and DMARC are crucial for maintaining a good sender reputation.

Utilize Aliases: Services like SimpleLogin or AnonAddy allow users to create aliases for their email addresses. This can help mask the primary email address and limit exposure to spam.

Backup Email Data: Regularly backing up important emails and data stored within the email account is essential to prevent data loss in the event of a ban.

Prepare a Recovery Plan: The author suggests having a plan in place for quickly switching to a backup email address and notifying contacts if a primary email account is banned.

Understand Gmail's Policies: Familiarizing oneself with Gmail's terms of service and acceptable use policies can help users avoid actions that could lead to a ban.

In conclusion, the article advocates for a layered approach to email management that includes diversification, custom domains, proper email handling practices, and proactive planning to mitigate the impact of potential email bans. The goal is to minimize the disruption caused by a ban and maintain control over one's email communication.

Key Points:

  • Diversify email providers (Gmail, ProtonMail, etc.) to avoid single point of failure.
  • Use custom domains to control your email identity and portability.
  • Set up email forwarding from custom domain to your primary email.
  • Warm up new email addresses to build a positive sender reputation.
  • Practice good email hygiene (avoid spam, honor unsubscribes).
  • Implement SPF, DKIM, and DMARC authentication.
  • Use email aliases to mask your primary email address.
  • Regularly backup email data.
  • Have a recovery plan for switching to a backup email.
  • Understand and adhere to Gmail's policies.
