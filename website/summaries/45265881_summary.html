Adios Chicos, 25 Years of KDE

The article is a retrospective on the KDE project, written on its 25th anniversary in 2025. It reflects on the project's history, evolution, and impact on the free and open-source software world. The author reminisces about the early days of KDE, highlighting the initial controversy surrounding its use of Qt, a non-free toolkit at the time, and how the project navigated those challenges. The piece also touches upon the evolution of the KDE desktop environment, from its early versions to the more modern Plasma desktop, emphasizing the constant pursuit of innovation and user-friendliness. The author notes the importance of the KDE community, underlining its global reach and the diverse contributions of its members. It describes how KDE applications expanded beyond the desktop, encompassing a wide range of software for various purposes, reflecting the community's commitment to providing comprehensive solutions for users. The article further explores the impact of KDE's technologies on other projects and platforms, emphasizing its role in advancing the broader free software ecosystem. It speaks about KDE's efforts to promote software freedom, open standards, and user privacy. The piece also addresses the challenges KDE faced and overcame over the years, including technological shifts, funding issues, and competition from other desktop environments. Looking to the future, the article expresses optimism about KDE's continued relevance and its potential to shape the future of desktop computing and beyond. It also emphasizes the importance of adapting to new technologies and continuing to prioritize user needs. The author ends with a celebratory note, acknowledging the achievements of the KDE community and expressing gratitude for the contributions of all those involved in the project's success.
