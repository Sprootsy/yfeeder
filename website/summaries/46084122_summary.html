The article discusses the "tricusp triangle," a shape formed by three overlapping circles. It focuses on the geometric properties and mathematical relationships associated with this shape. The author explores how the area of the tricusp triangle relates to the area of the triangle formed by the centers of the three circles. The article details a specific formula to determine the area of the tricusp triangle based on the side length of the triangle created by connecting the centers of the circles. It also points out the area is about 4.2% of the triangle formed by the centers of the circles.
