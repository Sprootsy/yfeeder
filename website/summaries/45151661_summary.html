Normalization of deviance (2015)

Summary

The article "WAT" by Dan Luu explores the inconsistencies and unexpected behaviors in various programming languages and systems. It delves into the quirks of different languages, highlighting situations where seemingly simple operations produce surprising or illogical results. The author showcases these "WAT" moments across a range of languages, including JavaScript, Python, PHP, C++, and others. The examples often involve type coercion, operator precedence, or language-specific rules that can lead to confusion and errors. For instance, the article points out the notorious behavior of JavaScript's addition and comparison operators, where the language's loose typing can result in unexpected outcomes when adding or comparing values of different types. Similarly, it discusses how PHP's string comparison can yield surprising results due to its type juggling. The author emphasizes that these inconsistencies are not necessarily flaws in the languages themselves but rather consequences of design choices, historical reasons, or compromises made to prioritize other aspects like ease of use or backward compatibility. However, these quirks can lead to bugs and make code harder to understand and maintain. The article also touches on the broader implications of these "WAT" moments, suggesting that they highlight the importance of understanding the underlying mechanisms of the languages and systems we use. It argues that developers should be aware of these potential pitfalls and take precautions to avoid them, such as using linters, type checking, and writing thorough tests. Ultimately, the article serves as a reminder that programming languages are complex tools with their own unique set of quirks and oddities. By understanding these "WAT" moments, developers can become more effective and avoid common mistakes.
