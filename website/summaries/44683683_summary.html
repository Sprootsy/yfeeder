I've Had It with Microsoft

Summary:

The author expresses strong dissatisfaction with Microsoft and its products, detailing a series of negative experiences and frustrations across various Microsoft offerings. The author begins by describing issues with Windows, including unwanted updates, telemetry, and a general feeling of being treated as a product rather than a customer. They recount instances of Windows updates causing problems and the difficulty in controlling the operating system's behavior.

The author then moves on to criticize Microsoft's software development practices, highlighting the perceived bloat and lack of polish in many of their applications. They mention specific issues with Microsoft Teams, such as its resource intensity and tendency to crash. They also voice concerns about the direction of Visual Studio Code (VS Code), an IDE they once admired, now feeling that it is becoming bloated with unnecessary features and integrations, moving away from its original focus on simplicity and performance. They feel the acquisition by Microsoft has ruined the application.

The author also criticizes Microsoft's cloud services, particularly Azure. They describe frustrating experiences with Azure's complexity, opaque pricing, and unexpected charges. They also express concern about Microsoft's handling of user data and privacy, citing instances where Microsoft appears to be collecting excessive amounts of information.

The overall tone of the article is one of disillusionment and frustration. The author feels that Microsoft has prioritized profit and data collection over user experience and product quality. They express a desire to move away from Microsoft products and services as much as possible, exploring alternative solutions that align better with their values and needs. They are tired of Microsoft's increasing intrusion into their personal computing experience.

Key Points:

  • Dissatisfaction with Windows due to unwanted updates, telemetry, and lack of user control.
  • Criticism of Microsoft's software development practices, citing bloat and lack of polish in applications like Microsoft Teams and Visual Studio Code.
  • Frustration with Azure's complexity, opaque pricing, and unexpected charges.
  • Concerns about Microsoft's handling of user data and privacy.
  • Overall feeling that Microsoft prioritizes profit and data collection over user experience.
  • Desire to move away from Microsoft products and services in favor of alternatives.
