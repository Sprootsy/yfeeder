Qantas is cutting executive bonuses after data breach

Summary:

Qantas has reduced the maximum potential pay for its top executives by 15% following a data breach that occurred in March. This decision, announced in the airline's remuneration report, reflects the company's accountability for the incident, which involved the personal information of employees. The change impacts both short-term and long-term incentives for the leadership team. The airline has stated that the data breach was a key factor in determining executive compensation adjustments.

Key Points:

  • Qantas has cut executive pay by 15% after a data breach.
  • The data breach involved the personal information of employees.
  • Both short-term and long-term incentives for executives are affected.
  • The decision reflects the company's accountability for the incident.
