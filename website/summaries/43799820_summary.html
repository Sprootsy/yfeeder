The article, titled "That Joke Isn't Funny Any More," explores the author's changing perspective on libertarianism, particularly in the context of contemporary political and social issues. However, the author argues that the landscape has shifted, and libertarianism, as a guiding philosophy, now seems inadequate and even harmful in addressing current challenges.

The author criticizes the libertarian tendency to downplay the importance of social justice issues and to prioritize individual liberty at the expense of collective well-being.

The author suggests that the libertarian faith in free markets often fails to account for the potential for corporations to exert undue influence on politics and society, leading to outcomes that are detrimental to individual freedom and social equity. They argue that a more nuanced approach is needed to address the challenges posed by corporate power and economic inequality.

Furthermore, the author reflects on the importance of community and social cohesion. They suggest that libertarianism's focus on individual autonomy can sometimes neglect the importance of social bonds and shared values in maintaining a healthy society. The author emphasizes the need for a sense of collective responsibility and a willingness to work together to address common problems.

Ultimately, the author concludes that while some aspects of libertarianism, such as its emphasis on individual freedom and skepticism of government power, remain valuable, the philosophy as a whole is no longer sufficient to guide political and social action in the present context.
