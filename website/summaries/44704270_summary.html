I hacked my washing machine

The article is a blog post by someone who details how they hacked their washing machine to gain more control over its functions and add custom features. Frustrated with the limitations of the pre-programmed cycles and the lack of specific options, the author decided to reverse engineer the machine's controller and modify its firmware.

The author begins by explaining their motivations for hacking the washing machine. They were dissatisfied with the pre-set wash cycles, finding them either too long, too short, or lacking specific options like extra rinse cycles or precise temperature control. They wanted the ability to create custom wash programs tailored to their specific needs and preferences.

The first step involved identifying the washing machine's microcontroller and accessing its firmware. The author disassembled the control panel and located the main circuit board. Using online resources and datasheets, they identified the microcontroller and found a way to connect to it using a serial interface. This allowed them to dump the original firmware for analysis.

Next, the author reverse engineered the firmware to understand how the washing machine's functions were controlled. They used disassemblers and decompilers to analyze the code, mapping out the relationships between different functions and identifying the variables that controlled parameters like water level, temperature, motor speed, and valve activation. This process was time-consuming and required a good understanding of embedded systems and assembly language.

With a solid understanding of the firmware, the author began modifying it to add their desired features. They created new wash cycles with customized parameters, added an extra rinse option, and implemented finer-grained temperature control. They also added a feature to display the remaining wash time more accurately.

After modifying the firmware, the author needed to flash it back onto the washing machine's microcontroller. They used a programmer to write the modified firmware to the chip, taking precautions to avoid bricking the device. The process was successful, and the washing machine booted up with the new firmware.

Finally, the author tested the modified washing machine extensively to ensure that all the new features worked as expected and that there were no unintended side effects. They ran various wash cycles, monitored the temperature and water levels, and verified that the machine was operating safely and reliably. After several weeks of testing, they were satisfied with the results.

In conclusion, the author successfully hacked their washing machine, gaining complete control over its functions and adding custom features. They emphasized that this project required a significant amount of technical knowledge and effort, but the results were worth it for them. They also cautioned readers to proceed with caution when attempting similar modifications, as there is a risk of damaging the washing machine or even causing harm.

Key Points:

  • The author was dissatisfied with the limited pre-set wash cycles of their washing machine.
  • They reverse engineered the washing machine's firmware to understand how it worked.
  • They modified the firmware to add custom wash cycles, an extra rinse option, and finer temperature control.
  • They flashed the modified firmware back onto the washing machine's microcontroller.
  • They thoroughly tested the modified washing machine to ensure it was working correctly and safely.
  • The project required significant technical knowledge and effort.
  • There is a risk of damaging the washing machine when attempting similar modifications.
