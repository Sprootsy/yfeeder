Do Things That Don't Scale (2013)

Paul Graham's essay "Do Things that Don't Scale" argues that startups should initially focus on providing exceptional, personalized service to a small number of users, even if it means doing things that are not scalable in the long run. This hands-on approach allows founders to deeply understand their users' needs, build a strong foundation of passionate customers, and refine their product before attempting to scale. Graham emphasizes that many successful startups started with non-scalable tactics, such as manually onboarding users, personally addressing their concerns, and even building parts of the product themselves. These efforts, while time-consuming, are crucial for gaining early traction and iterating on the product based on real-world feedback. By prioritizing quality over efficiency in the early stages, startups can create a loyal customer base and develop a product that truly resonates with their target audience. Once they have a solid foundation, they can then focus on scaling their operations and automating processes.

Key Points:

  • Focus on Exceptional Service: In the early stages, startups should prioritize providing exceptional, personalized service to a small group of users, even if it's not scalable.
  • Understand User Needs: Non-scalable tactics allow founders to deeply understand their users' needs and pain points.
  • Build a Strong Foundation: A hands-on approach helps build a strong foundation of passionate customers who are invested in the startup's success.
  • Iterate Based on Feedback: Early efforts should focus on refining the product based on real-world feedback from users.
  • Non-Scalable Tactics are Common: Many successful startups began with non-scalable tactics like manual onboarding and personalized support.
  • Quality over Efficiency: Prioritize quality and user satisfaction over efficiency in the initial stages.
  • Create a Loyal Customer Base: Exceptional service leads to a loyal customer base who will advocate for the product.
  • Scale Later: Once a solid foundation is established, focus on scaling operations and automating processes.
  • Examples of Non-Scalable Tactics: Manually onboarding users, personally addressing concerns, and building parts of the product themselves.
  • The Value of Doing Things Manually: Doing things manually can reveal insights and opportunities that might be missed when focusing solely on scalable solutions.
