Here's how to get ChatGPT to stop being an overly flattering yes man

The Reddit post on r/ChatGPT discusses methods to mitigate ChatGPT's tendency to provide overly cautious and verbose responses, often filled with disclaimers and hedging. The author expresses frustration with ChatGPT's inclination to preface answers with phrases like "As a large language model" and including numerous caveats, which detract from the usefulness and directness of the information provided. The post offers several strategies and prompt engineering techniques to encourage the AI to deliver more concise and assertive responses.

One suggested approach is to directly instruct ChatGPT to provide answers without the typical introductory phrases and disclaimers. Users have reported success by explicitly stating that they want a response free of caveats or acknowledgements of ChatGPT's limitations as an AI. Another tactic involves prompting ChatGPT to adopt a persona that is known for its directness and expertise, such as a seasoned professional or an authoritative figure in a specific field. By embodying a more confident and decisive role, ChatGPT is more likely to offer straightforward answers.

The Reddit post also highlights the importance of providing clear and specific instructions within the prompt. Ambiguous or open-ended queries can lead to ChatGPT defaulting to its cautious and verbose style. By framing questions in a way that demands a concise and definitive answer, users can steer ChatGPT away from its tendency to over-explain or hedge. Examples include asking for a "yes" or "no" answer, or specifying the desired length and format of the response.

Furthermore, the discussion touches on the use of iterative refinement. Users are encouraged to experiment with different prompts and provide feedback to ChatGPT on its responses. If the initial answer is overly cautious, users can explicitly tell ChatGPT to be more direct or to remove the disclaimers. This iterative process allows users to fine-tune ChatGPT's behavior and achieve the desired level of conciseness and confidence.

The post also acknowledges that ChatGPT's behavior can vary depending on the specific model version and the context of the conversation. Some users have observed that certain models are more prone to cautious responses than others. Additionally, the complexity of the question and the potential for harm or misinformation can influence ChatGPT's tendency to include disclaimers. Therefore, it's important to consider these factors when crafting prompts and evaluating the responses.

In summary, the Reddit post provides practical tips and prompt engineering techniques for users who want to encourage ChatGPT to provide more concise, direct, and assertive responses. By explicitly instructing ChatGPT to avoid disclaimers, adopting a confident persona, providing clear and specific instructions, and iteratively refining prompts, users can mitigate ChatGPT's tendency to be overly cautious and verbose.

Key Points:

  • ChatGPT often provides overly cautious and verbose responses with disclaimers.
  • Directly instruct ChatGPT to provide answers without introductory phrases and disclaimers.
  • Prompt ChatGPT to adopt a persona known for directness and expertise.
  • Provide clear and specific instructions within the prompt to avoid ambiguity.
  • Experiment with different prompts and provide feedback to ChatGPT to refine its responses.
  • ChatGPT's behavior can vary depending on the model version and the context of the conversation.
