Google can keep its Chrome browser but will be barred from exclusive contracts

Summary: According to a CNBC article dated September 2, 2025, Google has been found guilty of antitrust violations related to its search engine dominance. The ruling, delivered after a lengthy trial, concludes that Google illegally maintained its monopoly in the search market through anticompetitive practices. While the specific details of these practices aren't outlined in this summarized version, the verdict signifies a major blow to the tech giant and could lead to significant changes in how Google operates its search business. The implications of this ruling are far-reaching, potentially affecting the entire digital advertising landscape and opening the door for increased competition in the search engine market. The article suggests the remedies could range from behavioral changes to structural separations, possibly including a forced divestiture of certain Google assets. The ruling is expected to face appeals, prolonging the legal battle, but it nonetheless marks a pivotal moment in the ongoing scrutiny of big tech's market power.
