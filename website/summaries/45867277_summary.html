Python Software Foundation gets a donor surge after rejecting federal grant

Summary

The Python Software Foundation (PSF) experienced a significant surge in donations following its decision to reject a federal grant due to concerns about the grant's anti-Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion (DEI) stance. The grant, offered by the U.S. Agency for International Development (USAID) and managed by the U.S. Digital Response (USDR), aimed to enhance the security of Python's packaging ecosystem. However, the PSF found the grant's terms incompatible with its commitment to DEI, particularly a clause that would have restricted the organization's ability to prioritize DEI in its work.

The PSF's decision to decline the grant was rooted in its core values, which prioritize inclusivity and accessibility within the Python community. The organization believed that accepting the grant would compromise its ability to foster a welcoming and diverse environment for contributors and users. News of the PSF's decision spread rapidly within the tech community, sparking both support and criticism.

In the wake of this decision, the PSF received an outpouring of support from individual donors and corporate sponsors. Many praised the organization for standing by its principles and prioritizing DEI over financial gain. This influx of donations has provided the PSF with the resources it needs to continue its work on securing the Python packaging ecosystem while maintaining its commitment to DEI. The unexpected financial support has also validated the PSF's decision and demonstrated the importance of DEI within the open-source community.

The situation highlights the growing tension between government funding and DEI initiatives, as well as the increasing importance of DEI within the tech industry. The PSF's experience serves as an example of how organizations can prioritize their values, even when faced with financial incentives, and how the community can rally to support such decisions. This event is likely to influence future discussions about DEI in open-source and the role of government funding in shaping these initiatives.
