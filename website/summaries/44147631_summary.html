This article is a Mastodon post by Beka Valentine discussing the phenomenon of "shiny object syndrome" and its impact on personal projects and goals. Beka reflects on their own experiences with getting easily distracted by new and exciting ideas, leading to unfinished projects and a sense of stagnation. They emphasize the importance of recognizing this pattern and developing strategies to combat it, such as focusing on one project at a time, setting realistic goals, and celebrating small victories. The post also touches upon the role of external factors, like social media and the constant influx of information, in exacerbating shiny object syndrome. Beka encourages readers to be mindful of their attention and energy, and to prioritize consistency and completion over chasing every new idea that comes along. The post aims to help others identify and address similar challenges in their own lives, fostering a sense of self-awareness and promoting a more focused and fulfilling approach to personal pursuits.
