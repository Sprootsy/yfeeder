A 2k-year-old sun hat worn by a Roman soldier in Egypt

A 2,000-year-old sun hat, believed to have been worn by a Roman soldier in Egypt, is now on display after being stored for a century. The hat, made from woven reeds, was discovered in the early 20th century during excavations in Egypt. It is remarkably well-preserved, offering insights into the daily lives and practical needs of Roman soldiers stationed in the region. Its recent unveiling allows the public to view a rare artifact that highlights the resourcefulness and adaptation of Roman soldiers to the Egyptian climate.
