The US Department of Agriculture Bans Support for Renewables

Summary

According to an article published by Inside Climate News on August 19, 2025, the U.S. Department of Agriculture (USDA) has implemented a new policy that effectively bans the use of federal funds to support renewable energy projects on farms. This decision reverses previous USDA initiatives aimed at encouraging farmers to adopt sustainable energy practices.

The policy change, which was announced in a memo circulated internally within the USDA, prohibits the allocation of grants and loans from key USDA programs, such as the Rural Energy for America Program (REAP), towards renewable energy installations like solar panels, wind turbines, and biogas digesters. The rationale provided by the USDA cites concerns about the economic viability of renewable energy projects in the agricultural sector and their potential impact on farmland productivity.

Critics of the new policy argue that it undermines efforts to combat climate change and reduces opportunities for farmers to lower their energy costs and diversify their income streams. Renewable energy advocates express dismay at the USDA's decision, pointing to the demonstrated success of renewable energy technologies in reducing greenhouse gas emissions and enhancing the resilience of farming operations.

The article highlights the potential consequences of this policy shift, including a slowdown in the adoption of renewable energy technologies in rural communities and a setback for the Biden administration's goals of achieving net-zero emissions by 2050. Farmers who were planning to invest in renewable energy systems may now face significant financial barriers, as they lose access to crucial federal funding and incentives.

Furthermore, the article raises questions about the USDA's commitment to supporting sustainable agriculture and addressing the environmental challenges facing the farming industry. The decision to ban farm renewables support is seen by some as a sign of the agency's shifting priorities and its alignment with fossil fuel interests.

The Inside Climate News article concludes by emphasizing the urgent need for a comprehensive and science-based approach to agricultural policy that promotes both economic prosperity and environmental stewardship. It calls on policymakers to reconsider the USDA's decision and to prioritize investments in renewable energy and other sustainable practices that can help farmers thrive in a changing climate.
