Where are vacation homes located in the US?

The article explores the geographic distribution of vacation homes in the United States, focusing on understanding where these properties are concentrated. It examines data from the US Census Bureau and the American Community Survey (ACS) to identify counties with a high percentage of seasonal, recreational, or occasional use housing units. The analysis reveals that vacation homes are disproportionately located in specific regions, often characterized by attractive natural amenities such as coastlines, mountains, and lakes.

The article highlights the top counties for vacation homes, many of which are found in states like Maine, Florida, and Colorado. It discusses how the presence of vacation homes can significantly impact local economies and communities, both positively and negatively. On the one hand, vacation homes can boost local businesses and provide employment opportunities. On the other hand, they can contribute to rising housing costs, strain local infrastructure, and alter the character of communities.

The author also investigates the relationship between vacation home locations and factors such as proximity to water bodies, ski resorts, and national parks. The data suggests a strong correlation between these amenities and the prevalence of vacation homes. Furthermore, the article touches upon the demographic characteristics of vacation homeowners, noting that they tend to be wealthier and older than permanent residents.

In addition to identifying specific locations, the article discusses the implications of the concentration of vacation homes for housing affordability and community development. It points out that the conversion of properties into vacation homes can reduce the availability of housing for local workers and residents, potentially leading to displacement and other social issues. The author suggests that understanding the geography of vacation homes is crucial for policymakers and community leaders seeking to address these challenges and promote sustainable development.

  • Vacation homes are geographically concentrated in specific US counties.
