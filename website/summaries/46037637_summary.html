Claude Opus 4.5

The article announces the release of Anthropic's most advanced AI models: Claude 3 Opus, Claude 3 Sonnet, and Claude 3 Haiku. Opus is presented as the top-of-the-line model, excelling in complex tasks and pushing the boundaries of AI capabilities. Sonnet is described as a balance of intelligence and speed, offering strong performance at a lower cost. Haiku is highlighted for its speed and responsiveness, designed for near-instant interactions. The Claude 3 model family is characterized by its improved intelligence, near-perfect recall, and enhanced vision capabilities. They are also claimed to be less likely to refuse to answer prompts, and improvements have been made in accuracy. Anthropic emphasizes the models' ability to handle a wider range of prompts and their improved understanding of context, reducing the frequency of unnecessary refusals.

The models demonstrate significant advancements in reasoning, mathematics, and coding. Opus surpasses other models on benchmarks like MMLU, GPQA, and GSM8K. The article also highlights a "needle in a haystack" evaluation, where the models were tested on their ability to recall information from a large context window (specifically, one exceeding 200K tokens). Claude 3 models demonstrated a remarkable ability to retrieve the specific information, including subtle nuances, from within the extensive text. This highlights their exceptional recall capabilities, even in the face of potential distraction. Anthropic's models also show reduced hallucination rates. The article also discusses the models' sophisticated vision capabilities, which allow them to process a variety of visual formats, including photos, charts, graphs, and technical diagrams. These features enable use cases such as advanced image analysis and document processing. Anthropic emphasizes their commitment to responsible AI development and safety. They invested in advanced Constitutional AI techniques to guide the models' behavior, and implemented rigorous safety testing to mitigate potential risks. External experts also assessed the models, and the overall result is an improvement in trustworthiness. The models are designed to be more transparent in their decision-making processes, providing explanations and justifications for their outputs. This helps users understand and validate the AI's reasoning. The article concludes by stating that Opus and Sonnet are available now, and Haiku will be available soon. The models are accessible through the Anthropic API, Amazon Bedrock, and Google Cloud's Vertex AI. Anthropic is also increasing the context window for all Claude 3 models, with plans to offer a 1 million token context window to select customers.
