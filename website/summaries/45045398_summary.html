Google has eliminated 35% of managers overseeing small teams in past year

According to a CNBC article published in August 2025, Google has significantly reduced its management ranks, eliminating a third of its management positions. The announcement was made by a Google executive who stated that this restructuring was part of an effort to improve efficiency and streamline decision-making processes within the company. The rationale behind the cuts centers on the idea that a flatter organizational structure will empower individual contributors, foster innovation, and accelerate project completion times.

The move reflects a broader trend in the tech industry where companies are re-evaluating their organizational structures to remain competitive and adapt to rapidly changing market conditions. Google's executive emphasized that the removed management layers were often seen as bottlenecks, hindering the flow of information and slowing down the implementation of new ideas. By removing these layers, Google aims to create a more agile and responsive work environment.

The impacted managers have been offered various options, including the opportunity to transition into individual contributor roles, take on specialized project assignments, or pursue opportunities outside of Google with severance packages. Google is also investing in programs to support employees in adapting to the new organizational structure and developing skills relevant to a flatter hierarchy. These programs focus on leadership development for individual contributors and enhanced training in areas such as project management and cross-functional collaboration.

The initial response to the restructuring has been mixed. Some employees have expressed optimism, believing that it will lead to increased autonomy and faster career progression. Others have voiced concerns about potential increased workloads and the loss of experienced leaders who provided valuable guidance and mentorship. Industry analysts are closely watching Google's experiment, as its success or failure could influence other large tech companies to adopt similar strategies.

Google anticipates that the full benefits of the restructuring will be realized over the next year as employees adapt to the new roles and responsibilities. The company plans to closely monitor key performance indicators, such as project completion rates, employee satisfaction, and innovation metrics, to assess the effectiveness of the changes and make further adjustments as needed. Ultimately, Google believes that this bold move will position the company for continued success in the rapidly evolving technology landscape.

Key Points:

  • Google has eliminated a third of its management positions.
  • The restructuring aims to improve efficiency and decision-making.
  • The goal is to create a flatter, more agile organizational structure.
  • Impacted managers were offered transition options within Google or severance packages.
  • Google is investing in training programs to support employees in the new structure.
  • Employee response is mixed, with optimism and concerns.
  • Industry analysts are watching to see if other companies follow suit.
  • Google will monitor key performance indicators to assess the changes' effectiveness.
