I can't recommend Grafana anymore

The article is a critical review of the author's experiences with Grafana in a production environment, particularly focusing on the challenges and frustrations encountered while using it for monitoring and observability. The author begins by acknowledging Grafana's popularity and ubiquity in the monitoring space, but quickly transitions into detailing a series of issues that led to a negative overall experience. These issues range from seemingly minor user interface quirks to more serious problems with data accuracy and reliability.

A significant portion of the article is dedicated to problems with Grafana's alerting system. The author describes a situation where alerts were not firing as expected, despite being configured correctly. This led to missed incidents and a loss of confidence in the monitoring system. Further investigation revealed inconsistencies in how Grafana evaluated alert rules, particularly when dealing with missing data or edge cases. The author also points out the difficulty in debugging alert configurations and understanding why an alert did or did not fire.

Another area of concern is Grafana's data visualization capabilities. The author notes that while Grafana offers a wide range of visualization options, it can be challenging to create accurate and meaningful graphs, especially when dealing with complex data or multiple data sources. The author specifically mentions issues with data alignment, time zone handling, and the lack of clear error messages when something goes wrong. There are also complaints about the verbosity of the user interface and the difficulty in discovering features or configuring advanced options.

The article also touches on the operational aspects of running Grafana at scale. The author describes challenges with managing dashboards, particularly as the number of dashboards grows. The lack of proper organization and discoverability makes it difficult for users to find the information they need, leading to frustration and wasted time. The author also mentions problems with Grafana's performance, particularly when querying large datasets or rendering complex dashboards. This can lead to slow response times and a degraded user experience.

Furthermore, the author criticizes the overall complexity of Grafana. While acknowledging that Grafana is a powerful tool with many features, the author argues that it can be overwhelming for new users. The steep learning curve and the lack of clear documentation make it difficult to get started and to effectively use Grafana's more advanced features. The author suggests that Grafana could benefit from a more streamlined user interface and a more intuitive workflow.

In conclusion, the article presents a critical perspective on Grafana, highlighting a number of challenges and frustrations encountered while using it in a production environment. While acknowledging Grafana's strengths and popularity, the author argues that it suffers from issues with alerting, data visualization, operational complexity, and overall usability. The author suggests that Grafana needs to address these issues in order to remain a competitive and effective monitoring solution.
