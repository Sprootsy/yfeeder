Largest cargo sailboat completes first Atlantic crossing

Summary

The world's largest cargo sailboat, the Canopée, has successfully completed its first transatlantic voyage. The vessel departed from Saint-Nazaire, France, and arrived in Belém, Brazil, marking a significant milestone in the pursuit of sustainable shipping solutions. The Canopée is specifically designed to transport components of the Ariane 6 rocket for the European Space Agency (ESA). This initial voyage served to deliver parts necessary for the launch pad infrastructure at the Guiana Space Centre in French Guiana. The Canopée is a hybrid cargo ship that combines wind power with traditional diesel engines, aiming to reduce fuel consumption and greenhouse gas emissions. Its design incorporates four Oceanwings, automated solid sails developed by Ayro, providing a substantial contribution to propulsion. The vessel's route was carefully planned to leverage prevailing trade winds, optimizing the use of wind power. The journey wasn't without its challenges, including navigating through varied weather conditions and optimizing the performance of the Oceanwings. However, the successful completion of the voyage demonstrates the viability of large-scale wind-assisted propulsion for cargo transport. The Canopée's operations are expected to significantly decrease the carbon footprint associated with transporting Ariane 6 components compared to conventional shipping methods. This first transatlantic crossing represents a major step forward for sustainable maritime transport, showcasing the potential for wind-assisted technology to play a crucial role in decarbonizing the shipping industry. The Canopée is anticipated to make regular voyages between Europe and South America, transporting essential equipment for the Ariane 6 program while minimizing environmental impact.
