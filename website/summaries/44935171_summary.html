AI vs. Professional Authors Results

Summary

The article, a blog post dated August 18, 2025, details the results of a second round of judging in an AI vs. Authors competition. The premise of the competition is to have AI-generated stories judged against stories written by human authors, with the aim to evaluate the progress and capabilities of AI in creative writing.

In the first round, judges had successfully identified the AI-written stories "easily". For this round, the stakes were raised. The author, Mark Lawrence, implemented several changes. He used a "supercharged" AI model that can access the internet, which he fine-tuned on a dataset of the previous competition's human-authored stories to help the AI mimic human writing styles better. Also, the AI had the ability to rewrite the stories based on feedback. The human authors were also challenged to improve their stories, having seen the AI-generated content from the previous round. The number of stories in the competition was also increased.

The results of the second round proved far more interesting and challenging for the judges. While the judges correctly identified a majority of the AI-written stories, their success rate was significantly lower than in the first round. This suggests the AI's improvements were effective in blurring the lines between AI and human writing.

The judging criteria considered factors like originality, plot, character development, prose quality, emotional impact, and overall enjoyment. The judges noted that some AI stories exhibited technical proficiency but often lacked emotional depth, originality, or a sense of "humanity." However, some AI stories were praised for their clever plots and interesting ideas. The human-written stories, on the other hand, generally excelled in emotional resonance and nuanced character development, but occasionally suffered from weaker plots or less imaginative concepts.

Mark Lawrence reflects on the implications of these results, noting that AI writing is rapidly advancing and may soon reach a point where it is indistinguishable from human writing in many aspects. The results underscore the strengths and weaknesses of both AI and human authors. While AI can generate text quickly and efficiently, and even adapt to specific styles, it currently struggles to replicate the emotional complexity and originality that are hallmarks of human creativity. Lawrence suggests that the future of writing may involve collaboration between AI and human authors, leveraging the strengths of both to produce more compelling and innovative stories.

Key Points

  • The article discusses the results of a second AI vs. Authors competition.
  • The AI model in this round was "supercharged" with internet access and fine-tuned on human stories.
  • Human authors also improved their stories after seeing the previous AI attempts.
  • The judges found it more difficult to distinguish between AI and human writing in this round.
  • AI stories showed improvement, but often lacked emotional depth and originality.
  • Human stories excelled in emotional resonance and character development.
  • The author suggests that AI and humans may collaborate in the future of writing.
  • AI writing is rapidly improving and closing the gap with human writing.
