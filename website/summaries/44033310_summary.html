The forbidden railway: Vienna-Pyongyang (2008)

The article is a personal account of the author's journey into North Korean studies and his experiences in building relationships with North Korean diplomats and individuals involved in cultural exchange. It details his early interest in North Korea, sparked by curiosity and a desire to understand a country often portrayed negatively in Western media. Initially, his research was limited to secondary sources and academic materials. However, a turning point came when he began attending North Korean film screenings and cultural events in Vienna, Austria, which provided him with opportunities to meet and interact with North Korean officials.

The author recounts his efforts to learn the Korean language and his dedication to understanding North Korean perspectives on history, politics, and culture. He emphasizes the importance of building trust and rapport with North Korean counterparts, often through informal conversations and shared meals. These interactions allowed him to gain insights that went beyond official narratives and to develop a more nuanced understanding of North Korean society.

The article describes the challenges of navigating the complex political landscape surrounding North Korea and the importance of maintaining a balanced perspective. The author also discusses the difficulties of obtaining reliable information about North Korea and the limitations of relying solely on Western media sources. He stresses the need for critical thinking and a willingness to engage with diverse viewpoints in order to form a more complete picture of the country.

He details how, through persistent engagement and genuine interest, he was able to foster relationships with North Korean diplomats and cultural representatives. These relationships enabled him to access primary sources, attend cultural events, and conduct interviews that contributed to his research. He reflects on the cultural differences and the nuances of communication that he encountered in his interactions with North Koreans.

The author highlights the role of personal connections in facilitating understanding and breaking down stereotypes. He shares anecdotes about his conversations with North Koreans, revealing their perspectives on various issues and their aspirations for the future. He acknowledges the complexities and contradictions inherent in North Korean society but also emphasizes the importance of recognizing the humanity of the people who live there.

In essence, the article narrates the author's journey from a curious researcher to a seasoned observer of North Korean affairs, underscoring the value of direct engagement, cultural sensitivity, and a commitment to understanding different perspectives.
