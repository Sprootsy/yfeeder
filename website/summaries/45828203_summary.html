US may cut air traffic 10% by Friday without shutdown deal, sources say

Summary

According to sources, the U.S. Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) may cut air traffic by about 10% starting Friday if Congress doesn't reach a deal to prevent a partial government shutdown. This reduction aims to ensure safety with fewer staff available. The FAA is working on contingency plans as key funding for the agency is set to expire. If no agreement is reached, some agency employees will be furloughed.

Key Points

  • The FAA may cut air traffic by 10% if Congress doesn't reach a deal to avert a partial government shutdown.
  • The reduction is a safety measure due to potential staff shortages from furloughs.
  • Key FAA funding is set to expire, prompting contingency planning.
  • Some agency employees will be furloughed if no agreement is reached.
