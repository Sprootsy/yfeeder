Rams is a documentary portrait of Dieter Rams (2018)

"Rams" is a documentary film directed by Gary Hustwit that explores the life and design philosophy of Dieter Rams, one of the most influential product designers of the 20th century. The film offers an intimate portrait of Rams, now in his 80s, at his home in Kronberg, Germany, providing a rare glimpse into his personal spaces and his thoughts on consumerism, sustainability, and the future of design.

The documentary delves into Rams' career at Braun, where he served as the head of design for many years, and his role in shaping the company's iconic aesthetic. It showcases numerous examples of his groundbreaking product designs, from radios and record players to coffee makers and calculators, highlighting their minimalist form, functional excellence, and enduring appeal. The film emphasizes the "10 Principles of Good Design" formulated by Rams, which have become a touchstone for designers worldwide. These principles stress the importance of innovation, usefulness, aesthetics, understandability, unobtrusiveness, honesty, long-lasting quality, thoroughness, environmental friendliness, and minimal design.

Beyond showcasing Rams' past achievements, "Rams" also explores his concerns about the current state of consumer culture and its impact on the environment. He expresses his worries about the overabundance of poorly designed products, the throwaway culture, and the unsustainable practices that are prevalent in the modern world. The film reflects on Rams' call for a more conscious and responsible approach to design, advocating for products that are built to last, are repairable, and minimize their environmental footprint.

The documentary features interviews with Rams himself, as well as insights from other designers and design experts who have been influenced by his work. These interviews provide context and perspective on Rams' contributions to the field of design and his enduring legacy. The film also explores the influence of Rams' design principles on Apple's design aesthetic.

"Rams" is not just a biographical portrait of a design icon; it's a thought-provoking meditation on the role of design in shaping our lives and the importance of creating products that are both beautiful and sustainable.
