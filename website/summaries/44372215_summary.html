Berkshire Hathaway Now Pays 5% of All Corporate Income Taxes in America

Summary:

According to a recent interview with Becky Quick on CNBC, Warren Buffett stated that Berkshire Hathaway is now responsible for paying 5% of all corporate income taxes collected by the U.S. government. This figure underscores the significant contribution Berkshire Hathaway makes to the U.S. economy through its tax obligations. Buffett highlighted this contribution during a discussion about corporate taxes and their role in funding government services. The article emphasizes the magnitude of Berkshire Hathaway's tax payments, illustrating the company's substantial financial footprint and its importance as a major taxpayer in the United States.

Key Points:

  • Warren Buffett revealed that Berkshire Hathaway pays 5% of all U.S. corporate income taxes.
  • This substantial tax contribution highlights Berkshire Hathaway's significant financial role in the U.S. economy.
