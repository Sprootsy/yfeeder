A worker fell into a nuclear reactor pool

Summary

On October 15, 2025, a security event occurred at the Columbia Generating Station, a nuclear power plant operated by Energy Northwest. At 14:54 Pacific Daylight Time, a person was found to be potentially contaminated and required offsite transport.

The event was classified as a security event due to a potential security breakdown. Following established protocols, the individual was transported offsite for further medical evaluation and proper decontamination procedures. The NRC was notified of the event, and an investigation was initiated to determine the cause of the potential contamination and evaluate the effectiveness of existing safety protocols. The event's impact on plant operations and public safety was minimal, as the contamination was contained and the plant continued to operate safely.

Key Points

  • Location: Columbia Generating Station
  • Date/Time: October 15, 2025, at 14:54 PDT
  • Nature of Event: Transport of potentially contaminated person offsite.

  • Impact: Minimal impact on plant operations and public safety.
