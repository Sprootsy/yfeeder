Signs of autism could be encoded in the way you walk

The article reports on a study suggesting that subtle differences in gait, or the way a person walks, could potentially be an early indicator of autism spectrum disorder (ASD). Researchers used machine learning to analyze the walking patterns of children and found that those with ASD exhibited distinct gait characteristics compared to neurotypical children. The study highlights the possibility of using gait analysis as a tool for earlier diagnosis and intervention for autism.

Key Points:

  • Study suggests a link between gait (walking patterns) and autism spectrum disorder (ASD).
  • Researchers used machine learning to analyze the gait of children with and without ASD.
  • Children with ASD showed different gait characteristics compared to neurotypical children.
  • Gait analysis may potentially be used as a tool for earlier ASD diagnosis.
