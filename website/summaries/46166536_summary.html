Framework Sponsors CachyOS

The CachyOS team is seeking framework sponsorship to enhance the distribution and accelerate development. CachyOS is an Arch Linux-based distribution known for its performance optimizations, custom package builds, and user-friendly experience. Currently, the team primarily self-funds the project and relies on community contributions.

The sponsorship will be used to address several key areas:

  1. Infrastructure Costs: CachyOS requires robust infrastructure for package building, hosting repositories, and maintaining the website. Sponsorship would help cover these ongoing expenses, ensuring the stability and availability of the distribution.
  2. Development Resources: Funding would enable the team to dedicate more time to development tasks, such as implementing new features, optimizing performance, and addressing bug fixes promptly. This will lead to a more polished and feature-rich user experience.
  3. Community Engagement: The team aims to expand community engagement through initiatives like organizing events, creating tutorials, and providing better support channels. Sponsorship would facilitate these efforts, fostering a more vibrant and collaborative community around CachyOS.
  4. Hardware and Software: Access to better hardware and software licenses would significantly improve the development workflow. This includes dedicated build servers, testing environments, and professional development tools.

The team proposes several sponsorship tiers, each offering different levels of visibility and recognition for sponsors. These tiers may include logo placement on the website, shout-outs on social media, mentions in release notes, and even the opportunity to collaborate on specific features.

By securing framework sponsorship, CachyOS aims to solidify its position as a leading Arch-based distribution, providing a fast, stable, and user-friendly experience for its users while also fostering a strong and supportive community.

Key Points:

  • CachyOS is seeking framework sponsorship to improve the distribution.
  • Current funding is primarily self-funded and relies on the community.
  • Sponsorship funds will be used for infrastructure costs, development resources, community engagement, and hardware/software.
  • Proposed sponsorship tiers offer various levels of visibility for sponsors.
  • Goal is to enhance stability, features, user experience, and community.
