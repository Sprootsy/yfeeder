Japanese grandparents create life-size Totoro with bus stop for grandkids (2020)

Summary:

A life-sized sculpture of Totoro, the beloved character from Hayao Miyazaki's animated film "My Neighbor Totoro," has been erected at a bus stop in Taketa, Oita Prefecture, Japan. This charming sculpture was created by local artist Kenta Higashiyama as a tribute to the film and to bring joy to the community. Located near the Kaminaka bus stop, the Totoro sculpture has become a popular spot for locals and tourists alike to visit, take photos, and experience a touch of the whimsical world of Studio Ghibli. The sculpture is meticulously crafted and painted to resemble Totoro perfectly, capturing the character's gentle and friendly nature. It has quickly become a local landmark and a symbol of the connection between art, community, and the enduring appeal of Miyazaki's films.
