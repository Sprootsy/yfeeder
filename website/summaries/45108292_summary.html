U.S. Emissions Rise 4.2%, China's Fall 2.7%

According to an analysis by the Centre for Research on Energy and Clean Air (CREA) and Global Energy Monitor (GEM), U.S. greenhouse gas emissions increased in 2022 while China's emissions decreased, marking a significant shift between the world's two largest polluters. The United States saw a 0.8% increase in emissions, primarily due to increased coal power generation driven by high natural gas prices, and a rebound in air travel following the COVID-19 pandemic. In contrast, China experienced a 0.2% decrease in emissions, attributed to a decline in construction activity and pandemic-related disruptions. This marks the first time since 2018 that China's emissions have fallen while U.S. emissions have risen. Despite the slight decrease, China remains the world's largest emitter, with emissions exceeding those of the entire OECD combined.

The report highlights that the U.S. emissions increase was largely driven by the power sector, where coal generation rose due to high natural gas prices, making coal more economically attractive. Additionally, the aviation sector saw a significant rebound in emissions as air travel recovered from pandemic lows. On the other hand, China's emissions decrease was primarily due to a slowdown in construction, which reduced demand for steel and cement production (energy-intensive industries). China's strict COVID-19 lockdowns also played a role in curbing emissions by limiting industrial activity and transportation.

The analysis suggests that China's emissions decline may be temporary, with potential for a rebound as economic activity recovers post-lockdowns. The report also points out that both countries face significant challenges in meeting their climate goals. For the U.S., transitioning away from coal power and further decarbonizing the transportation sector are crucial. For China, managing its economic recovery in a sustainable manner and curbing emissions from its vast industrial sector will be key.

The report underscores the importance of sustained efforts by both the U.S. and China to reduce emissions and transition to cleaner energy sources. The shift in emissions trends highlights the complex interplay of economic factors, energy policies, and global events in shaping emissions trajectories. The findings serve as a reminder that achieving global climate goals requires continuous commitment and ambitious actions from all major emitters.
