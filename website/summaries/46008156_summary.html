Discontinuation of ARM Notebook with Snapdragon X Elite SoC

Summary

TUXEDO Computers has announced the discontinuation of their planned ARM notebooks featuring the Snapdragon X Elite SoC. The company cites significant performance deficits compared to initial Qualcomm promises and unsatisfactory support for Linux as the primary reasons for this decision. TUXEDO had invested considerable resources in developing a Linux distribution tailored for these devices and working closely with Qualcomm to address compatibility issues. However, the actual performance fell short of expectations, particularly in areas like GPU performance and AI capabilities, making the product uncompetitive in the current market. Furthermore, the level of support provided by Qualcomm for Linux integration and necessary kernel adaptations proved inadequate for TUXEDO's standards. The company concluded that proceeding with the ARM notebook project would not align with their commitment to delivering high-quality, performant Linux-based systems. They are now shifting their focus back to x86-based systems and exploring future ARM-based opportunities when the technology and support ecosystem mature sufficiently.

Key Points

  • TUXEDO Computers is discontinuing its ARM notebook project based on the Snapdragon X Elite SoC.
  • The decision is due to performance shortfalls compared to Qualcomm's initial claims.
  • Unsatisfactory Linux support from Qualcomm was also a major factor.
  • TUXEDO invested heavily in developing a custom Linux distribution for the ARM notebooks.
  • Actual GPU performance and AI capabilities were lower than expected.
  • Qualcomm's support for Linux integration and kernel adaptations was insufficient.
  • TUXEDO is refocusing on x86-based systems.
  • The company will consider ARM-based systems again in the future when technology and support improve.
