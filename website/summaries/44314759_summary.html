TI to invest $60B to manufacture foundational semiconductors in the U.S.

Texas Instruments (TI) has announced plans to invest more than $6 billion in potential new fabrication plants in the U.S. to increase production of foundational semiconductors. The company aims to meet the growing demand for semiconductors, particularly in the industrial and automotive sectors. These investments are part of TI's long-term strategy to enhance its manufacturing capabilities and ensure a reliable supply chain for its customers. The company emphasizes its commitment to manufacturing advancements that improve performance, reduce power consumption, and lower costs. The specific location and details of the new plants will be disclosed later as the projects progress through planning and permitting phases.

Key Points:

  • Texas Instruments plans to invest more than $6 billion in U.S. semiconductor manufacturing.
  • The investment will support the production of foundational semiconductors.
  • The expansion aims to meet increasing demand, especially from the industrial and automotive markets.
  • The investment is part of TI's long-term strategy to strengthen manufacturing capabilities.
  • Specific locations and details will be disclosed as projects progress.
