Scottish brothers finish mammoth row across Pacific Ocean after 139 days

The article reports on the successful completion of a Pacific Ocean row by Scottish brothers Jamie and Ewan MacLean. They rowed from California, USA, to Hawaii, taking 39 days, 14 hours, and 32 minutes. The MacLean brothers faced challenges including adverse weather conditions, equipment malfunctions, and physical exhaustion. The article emphasizes the arduous nature of the journey, highlighting the mental and physical toll it took on the brothers. It also touches upon the motivations behind their endeavor, which included raising awareness for ocean conservation and inspiring others to pursue ambitious goals.
