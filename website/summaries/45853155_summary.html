Older Adults Outnumber Children in 11 States

Summary:

The U.S. Census Bureau projects that by 2030, older adults will outnumber children for the first time in U.S. history. This demographic shift is driven by several factors, including the aging of the Baby Boom generation, increased life expectancy, and declining birth rates. The report highlights that all Baby Boomers will be age 65 and older by 2030, contributing significantly to the growth of the older adult population.

The Census Bureau uses various demographic measures to understand these population changes, including population size, age, sex, race, and ethnicity. These measures are crucial for policymakers, researchers, and businesses in planning and decision-making. As the population ages, there will be implications for healthcare, social security, housing, and the labor force. Understanding these demographic trends helps in preparing for the future needs of an aging society.

Key Points:

  • By 2030, older adults (age 65 and over) are projected to outnumber children (under age 18) in the U.S. for the first time in history.
  • The aging of the Baby Boom generation is a significant factor contributing to the growth of the older adult population.
  • Increased life expectancy and declining birth rates also contribute to this demographic shift.
  • The Census Bureau uses demographic measures to analyze population changes and provide data for planning and decision-making.
  • This demographic shift has implications for various sectors, including healthcare, social security, housing, and the labor force.
