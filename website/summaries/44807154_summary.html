Marines now have an official drone-fighting handbook

The Marine Corps has published an official handbook for countering drones, acknowledging the increasing threat posed by unmanned aerial systems (UAS) on the modern battlefield. This handbook, officially titled Marine Corps Warfighting Publication (MCWP) 3-25.8, "Counter-Unmanned Aircraft Systems," provides Marines with tactics, techniques, and procedures (TTPs) for detecting, identifying, and neutralizing enemy drones. The publication emphasizes the evolving nature of drone warfare and the need for adaptable strategies to defend against these threats.

The handbook covers a wide range of topics, starting with the basics of drone technology, including different types of drones, their capabilities, and common employment tactics used by adversaries. It details how drones can be used for reconnaissance, surveillance, electronic warfare, and even direct attacks using weaponized payloads. Understanding these capabilities is crucial for Marines to develop effective countermeasures.

A significant portion of the handbook focuses on detection and identification methods. It describes various sensors and technologies that can be used to spot drones, such as radar, electro-optical/infrared (EO/IR) systems, acoustic sensors, and radio frequency (RF) detectors. It also discusses the importance of visual observation and reporting by Marines on the ground. The handbook stresses the need for layered detection capabilities to increase the likelihood of spotting drones in different environments and under varying conditions.

Once a drone is detected, the handbook provides guidance on identifying its type, purpose, and origin. This involves analyzing the drone's flight path, size, shape, and RF signature. Knowing the type of drone helps Marines anticipate its capabilities and intentions, allowing them to choose the most appropriate countermeasures.

The handbook outlines various methods for neutralizing drones, ranging from kinetic to non-kinetic options. Kinetic methods involve physically destroying the drone using firearms, missiles, or other weapons. Non-kinetic methods include jamming the drone's communication signals, spoofing its GPS navigation, or using directed energy weapons to disrupt its electronics. The choice of method depends on factors such as the drone's proximity, the need to avoid collateral damage, and the available resources.

The handbook emphasizes the importance of integrating counter-drone measures into overall force protection plans. This includes establishing early warning systems, training Marines on counter-drone tactics, and coordinating with other units and agencies to share information and resources. The handbook also discusses the legal and ethical considerations associated with engaging drones, particularly in populated areas or when civilian drones are present.

The publication also highlights the importance of electronic warfare in countering drones. It explains how Marines can use electronic warfare techniques to disrupt drone communications, disable their sensors, and even take control of them. It emphasizes the need for continuous training and adaptation to stay ahead of evolving drone technology and tactics.

MCWP 3-25.8 is designed to be a living document, meaning it will be updated regularly to reflect changes in drone technology and tactics. The Marine Corps recognizes that the threat posed by drones is constantly evolving, and this handbook is intended to provide Marines with the knowledge and tools they need to stay ahead of the curve.
