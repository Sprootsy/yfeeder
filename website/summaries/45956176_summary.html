An official atlas of North Korea

Summary:

The article reviews the "Atlas of Korea," published in North Korea. The atlas provides a rare glimpse into the country's cartographic practices and ideological presentation of its geography. The atlas, published in 2018, is a comprehensive single-volume work containing detailed maps of North Korea, as well as maps of the world and other regions. The author emphasizes the atlas's role as a propaganda tool, highlighting how it reinforces the North Korean government's narratives and worldview. The review delves into specific examples within the atlas, such as the depiction of the Demilitarized Zone (DMZ) and the portrayal of South Korea. The atlas consistently presents North Korea as the legitimate and rightful Korea, often subtly undermining the South Korean state's legitimacy. The atlas's maps often include politically charged annotations and designations. Furthermore, the author notes that the atlas's production quality is high, demonstrating North Korea's capability in cartography. The atlas also reflects the North Korean regime's emphasis on self-reliance and its particular view of the world. The author points out the atlas's limitations regarding accessibility and its intended audience, primarily within North Korea's elite circles. Despite its propaganda elements, the atlas provides valuable insight into North Korea's geographical perspective and its strategic communication methods. The review concludes by underscoring the importance of analyzing such materials to understand North Korea's geopolitical outlook and its internal narratives.
