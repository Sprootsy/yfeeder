A new book about the origins of Effective Altruism

The article is a critical analysis of the effective altruism (EA) movement, exploring its origins, rise, recent controversies, and potential future. It traces EA's development from its roots in utilitarian philosophy and animal rights activism to its embrace by Silicon Valley and the broader philanthropic world. The author examines EA's core tenets, such as prioritizing the most impactful interventions based on data and reason, and its focus on global issues like poverty and existential risks.

The piece highlights the influence of key figures like Peter Singer and Will MacAskill, who popularized EA's principles and helped establish influential organizations. It discusses the movement's success in attracting significant funding and talent, particularly from the tech industry, and its impact on various charitable causes. However, the article also delves into the criticisms and controversies that have plagued EA in recent years.

The author examines concerns about EA's focus on quantifiable metrics, which can lead to neglecting qualitative factors and overlooking the complexities of social problems. The article also addresses the criticism that EA's emphasis on maximizing impact can inadvertently reinforce existing power structures and inequalities. Furthermore, the author discusses the ethical implications of EA's prioritization of long-term, existential risks over more immediate needs, as well as the potential for unintended consequences when implementing data-driven solutions in complex social systems.

The piece also examines the impact of the FTX scandal and the association of Sam Bankman-Fried with EA, which has significantly damaged the movement's reputation and raised questions about its judgment and oversight. The author explores the internal divisions within EA regarding its relationship with Bankman-Fried and the extent to which his actions reflect broader problems within the movement. The article concludes by considering the future of EA, suggesting that it needs to address its ethical shortcomings, improve its governance structures, and broaden its focus to be more inclusive and responsive to diverse perspectives. It remains to be seen whether EA can overcome its recent challenges and continue to be a force for good in the world.
