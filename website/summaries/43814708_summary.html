I just want to code (2023)

Summary:

Zach Bellay's article, "I Just Want to Code," reflects on the author's journey and evolving perspective on software development. Initially driven by a singular passion for coding, the author's experiences in the professional world brought to light the multifaceted nature of the job, which includes tasks beyond writing code. The author describes how, when starting out, coding felt like the ultimate goal. The process of transforming ideas into tangible software was inherently satisfying. However, as the author progressed in their career, they encountered the realities of the field, such as meetings, documentation, dealing with legacy systems, and navigating office politics. These elements often detracted from the pure coding experience they initially sought. The author acknowledges that while these tasks are crucial for project success and team collaboration, they sometimes create a sense of disconnect from the core activity they love: coding.

The article conveys a sentiment of longing for a development environment where coding is prioritized and streamlined. The author recognizes the importance of other essential software development aspects but emphasizes the desire to optimize processes to maximize the time spent coding. They also express a desire to work on projects where their coding skills are fully utilized and appreciated, and they can contribute meaningfully to the codebase. The author further reflects on the importance of striking a balance between coding and other responsibilities. While acknowledging that non-coding tasks are a necessary part of any software development role, they advocate for finding ways to minimize distractions and create more opportunities for focused coding time. This may involve improving team communication, streamlining project management processes, or advocating for changes within the organization.
