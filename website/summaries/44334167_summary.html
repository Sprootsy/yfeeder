Samsung Embeds IronSource Spyware App on Phones Across WANA

Summary:

SMEX, along with 17 other organizations, has sent an open letter to Samsung urging the company to stop pre-installing Israeli applications on devices sold in the West Asia and North Africa (WANA) region. The letter focuses on the ethical and legal issues surrounding the forced installation of apps, particularly those developed by Israeli companies, due to the ongoing human rights concerns related to the Israeli-Palestinian conflict. These apps, some of which have alleged ties to the Israeli military or surveillance activities, raise significant data privacy and security concerns for users in the WANA region. The letter argues that Samsung's actions may violate international human rights laws and corporate social responsibility principles. The organizations are calling on Samsung to respect the digital rights and privacy of its customers by ending the practice of forced app installations, ensuring transparency in its app selection process, and providing users with the freedom to choose which applications they want on their devices.

Key Points:

  • Open letter to Samsung from SMEX and 17 other organizations.
  • Demand to end forced pre-installation of Israeli apps in the WANA region.
  • Concerns about human rights violations and data privacy.
  • Allegations of some apps having ties to the Israeli military or surveillance activities.
  • Argument that Samsung's actions may violate international human rights laws and corporate social responsibility.
  • Call for transparency in app selection and user freedom to choose apps.
