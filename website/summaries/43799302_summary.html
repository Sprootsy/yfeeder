Martin argues that the WMF's focus has drifted away from its original mission of creating and maintaining a high-quality, freely available encyclopedia.

Martin suggests that the WMF is not adequately addressing these issues and that the community of editors feels increasingly alienated from the foundation's decision-making processes. He criticizes the WMF's spending on projects that he believes are not essential to the core mission, arguing that these resources could be better used to support editors and improve the encyclopedia's infrastructure.

Martin's letter has sparked discussion within the Wikipedia community, with some editors expressing support for his concerns and others defending the WMF's broader mission. The article highlights the tensions between the WMF's efforts to expand its reach and impact beyond the encyclopedia and the desire of some editors to focus primarily on improving the quality and accessibility of Wikipedia itself. The debate raises questions about the future of Wikipedia and the role of the WMF in shaping its direction.

The article also notes that the WMF has responded to Martin's letter, acknowledging the concerns raised and reiterating its commitment to supporting the editor community. However, the WMF defends its strategic priorities, arguing that initiatives like knowledge equity are essential to ensuring that Wikipedia reflects the diversity of human knowledge and that everyone has access to it. The WMF also emphasizes the importance of adapting to the changing information landscape and exploring new ways to deliver knowledge to users.

Overall, the article presents a critical perspective on the Wikimedia Foundation's strategic direction, highlighting the concerns of some Wikipedia editors who believe that the WMF is losing sight of its core mission.
