AI is a floor raiser, not a ceiling raiser

Summary

The article, written as a blog post from the future (July 29, 2025), discusses the observed impact of AI on various professions and creative fields. It argues against the common fear that AI will replace human workers and artists, positing instead that AI primarily acts as a "floor raiser" rather than a "ceiling raiser." This means AI tools are making it easier for individuals with less experience or skill to achieve a baseline level of competence, effectively raising the minimum standard of output across the board.

The author illustrates this point with examples from different sectors. In programming, AI code completion and debugging tools have reduced the time and expertise needed to produce functional code, allowing junior developers to contribute more quickly and reducing the burden on senior engineers. Similarly, in writing, AI-powered grammar and style checkers help improve the clarity and quality of written communication for everyone, regardless of their writing proficiency.

In creative fields like music and graphic design, AI tools are enabling individuals with limited artistic skills to generate decent-quality content, democratizing access to these fields. The author clarifies that while AI can produce acceptable results, it typically doesn't reach the level of true artistry or innovation achieved by highly skilled professionals. The focus is on enhancing the capabilities of the average person, rather than replacing exceptional talent.

The article also touches on the economic implications of this shift. By raising the "floor," AI increases overall productivity and reduces the cost of many services. This leads to a more competitive landscape, where simply being "good enough" is no longer sufficient to stand out. The author suggests that individuals need to focus on developing uniquely human skills such as creativity, critical thinking, and complex problem-solving to remain competitive in the AI-augmented workforce. They predict a future where the value lies not in basic competence (which AI can provide), but in the ability to leverage AI tools to achieve truly exceptional results.

The author emphasizes that AI is not about replacing humans, but about empowering them. It allows individuals to focus on higher-level tasks and creative endeavors by automating routine and tedious work. The challenge lies in adapting to this new reality and developing the skills necessary to thrive in an AI-driven world.

Key Points

  • AI acts as a "floor raiser," increasing the baseline level of competence across various fields.
  • AI tools empower individuals with less experience to achieve acceptable results more easily.
  • AI does not typically replace highly skilled professionals or lead to exceptional innovation.
  • The economic impact includes increased productivity and a more competitive landscape.
  • Individuals need to focus on developing uniquely human skills like creativity and critical thinking.
  • The value shifts from basic competence to the ability to leverage AI for exceptional results.
  • AI is about empowering humans to focus on higher-level tasks and creative endeavors.
