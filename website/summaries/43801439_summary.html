Australian who ordered radioactive materials walks away from court

Summary

An Australian man who ordered radioactive materials online has avoided conviction. The man, who was studying astrophysics, purchased americium-241, iodine-131, and barium-133 without proper authorization. The court acknowledged the man's remorse, his early guilty plea, and his cooperation with authorities. The judge also noted that the man posed a low risk of re-offending and had no intention to cause harm. The radioactive materials were detected by customs officials, leading to an investigation by the Australian Nuclear Science and Technology Organisation (ANSTO) and the Australian Federal Police.

Key Points

  • An Australian astrophysics student ordered radioactive materials online.
  • The materials included americium-241, iodine-131, and barium-133.
  • The man did not have authorization to purchase the radioactive substances.
  • Australian customs officials detected the radioactive materials.
  • ANSTO and the Australian Federal Police investigated the incident.
  • The court took into account the man's remorse, guilty plea, and cooperation.
  • The judge determined the man was a low risk and had no intention of causing harm.
  • The man avoided conviction.
