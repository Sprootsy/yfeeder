Tai Lopez charged by SEC in ponzi scheme

Summary:

The Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) announced charges against TradeStation Crypto, Inc. for failing to register as a securities exchange and broker. According to the SEC's order, from at least August 2019 to May 2022, TradeStation Crypto operated a crypto asset trading platform where it allowed customers to buy, sell, and trade crypto assets that were offered and sold as securities. The SEC stated that TradeStation Crypto did not register with the SEC as a national securities exchange or as a broker, which violated Sections 5(a) and 15(a) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934. Without admitting or denying the SEC's findings, TradeStation Crypto agreed to a cease-and-desist order, a censure, and to pay a $3 million penalty.

The SEC's order finds that TradeStation Crypto's platform provided functionalities of a securities exchange by providing a marketplace and facilities for bringing together orders for securities. It also acted as a broker by effecting transactions in securities for the accounts of others. As such, the SEC determined that TradeStation Crypto engaged in activities that required registration with the SEC, which it failed to do.

As a result of these findings, the SEC issued a cease-and-desist order against TradeStation Crypto, censured the company, and imposed a $3 million penalty. TradeStation Crypto consented to the order without admitting or denying the SEC's findings. The SEC considered TradeStation Crypto's cooperation in its investigation when determining the settlement terms.
