Workday suffered a data breach

Summary

According to an internal email viewed by Gizmodo, Workday, a major HR software provider, experienced a security breach. The email, sent by Workday's Chief Security Officer, revealed that an unauthorized party gained access to a subset of Workday's customer base. While the email states that the investigation is ongoing and there's no indication of widespread impact, it also acknowledges that the attacker used compromised credentials of Workday employees to gain access. The company believes the attacker primarily targeted compensation and personal information. Workday has notified law enforcement and is working to contain the incident. The breach is particularly concerning due to the sensitive nature of the data Workday handles, which includes employee salaries, addresses, and other personal details. The article emphasizes the potential risks associated with such a breach, including identity theft and financial fraud.

Key Points

  • Workday, a large HR software company, suffered a security breach.
  • The breach involved unauthorized access to a portion of Workday's customer data.
  • Compromised Workday employee credentials were used in the attack.
  • The attacker potentially targeted compensation and personal information.
  • Workday has notified law enforcement and is investigating the incident.
  • The breach has potentially serious implications due to the sensitivity of the data involved.
