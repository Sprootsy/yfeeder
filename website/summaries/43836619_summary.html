I can't pay rent because devs just don't care

This article is a critique of the tech industry's impact on housing affordability and societal well-being. The author argues that developers, often driven by profit and a narrow focus on technological advancement, contribute to rising housing costs and displacement. They accuse developers of lacking empathy and a broader understanding of the consequences of their work on communities. The article highlights the disconnect between the tech industry's purported goal of making the world a better place and its actual effects on affordability and social equity. The author specifically calls out the lack of attention paid to the needs of ordinary people struggling to afford housing, suggesting a moral failing within the development community.

Key Points:

  • Developers are accused of contributing to rising housing costs and displacement.
  • The author suggests that developers lack empathy and understanding of the consequences of their work.
  • There is a disconnect between the tech industry's stated goals and its actual impact on affordability.
  • The author highlights the lack of attention to the needs of people struggling to afford housing.
  • The article implies a moral failing within the development community regarding social responsibility.
