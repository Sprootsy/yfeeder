We've Issued Our First IP Address Certificate

The Let's Encrypt blog post announces that on July 1, 2025, they will issue their first certificate for an IP address. This marks a significant expansion of their services, which have historically focused on domain-validated certificates. The article explains the rationale behind this decision, highlighting the increasing need for secure communication with IP addresses directly, particularly in the context of IoT devices, private networks, and other scenarios where domain names are not practical or available.

The post addresses the challenges associated with issuing certificates for IP addresses, primarily concerning validation. Unlike domain names, IP addresses are not inherently tied to a specific entity in a globally verifiable way. Let's Encrypt outlines the validation methods they plan to implement, emphasizing a multi-faceted approach to ensure the legitimacy of certificate requests. This likely includes methods such as reverse DNS lookups, control of the network hosting the IP address, and potentially integrations with Regional Internet Registries (RIRs) or similar authorities.

The article also discusses the impact on the broader certificate ecosystem. Issuing IP address certificates requires careful consideration to prevent misuse, such as issuing certificates for IP addresses that are not under the control of the requesting party. Let's Encrypt emphasizes their commitment to responsible issuance practices and collaboration with other Certificate Authorities (CAs) to maintain the overall integrity of the Internet's security infrastructure.

The announcement also details the technical aspects of the new service. It explains the specific certificate extensions and formats that will be used for IP address certificates, ensuring compatibility with existing browsers and applications. Furthermore, it provides guidance for developers and system administrators on how to request and use these certificates effectively. The article also mentions the open-source nature of Let's Encrypt and invites community feedback and contributions to help refine the implementation.

Finally, the post reiterates Let's Encrypt's mission to make secure communication accessible to everyone. By extending their services to include IP address certificates, they aim to address a growing need in the industry and further contribute to a more secure and private Internet. The announcement includes a call for community participation and suggests ways for interested parties to get involved in the development and testing of the new service.

Key points:

  • Let's Encrypt will begin issuing certificates for IP addresses on July 1, 2025.
  • This expansion addresses the need for secure communication with IP addresses in IoT and private network scenarios.
  • Validation methods will be implemented to ensure the legitimacy of certificate requests, including reverse DNS lookups and control of the network.
  • Let's Encrypt is committed to responsible issuance practices and collaboration with other CAs.
  • The announcement includes technical details about certificate extensions and formats.
  • The initiative aligns with Let's Encrypt's mission to make secure communication accessible and encourages community involvement.
