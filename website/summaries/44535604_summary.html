Google nerfs Pixel 6a batteries following fire hazard

Summary

In July 2025, Ars Technica reported that Google had issued a software update that further reduced the maximum charging capacity of the Pixel 8 series phones, specifically targeting the Pixel 8 and 8 Pro. This update marks the second time in 2025 that Google has limited the battery charging capabilities of its Pixel phones via software. The initial battery capacity reduction was implemented earlier in the year to address concerns about battery health and longevity. However, the second update has sparked considerable user criticism due to the further reduced charging speeds and overall battery performance.

The article details how users have noticed a significant decrease in the rate at which their Pixel 8 phones charge. Before the update, the Pixel 8 series boasted fast charging capabilities, allowing users to quickly replenish their battery levels. After the update, however, many users reported that their devices took considerably longer to reach full charge. Independent testing and user feedback suggest that the maximum charging rate has been throttled even further, leading to frustration among Pixel 8 owners.

The rationale behind Google's decision to implement these changes is rooted in concerns about the long-term health of the lithium-ion batteries used in the Pixel 8 series. Over time, frequent fast charging and discharging cycles can degrade battery capacity, leading to shorter battery life and reduced overall performance. By limiting the maximum charging rate, Google aims to mitigate these effects and extend the lifespan of the battery. However, critics argue that the trade-off between battery longevity and user experience is not justified, particularly given that the Pixel 8 series is relatively new.

The Ars Technica report also highlights the broader context of Google's approach to battery management in its Pixel phones. Over the years, Google has employed various software tweaks and optimizations to improve battery life, sometimes at the expense of performance. While some of these efforts have been well-received, others have drawn criticism for being overly aggressive or for negatively impacting the user experience. The recent battery capacity reduction in the Pixel 8 series falls into the latter category, with many users expressing dissatisfaction with the reduced charging speeds and overall battery performance.

The article also mentions that Google has not provided a detailed explanation for the second battery capacity reduction. While the company has acknowledged the issue and stated that the changes are intended to improve battery health, it has not disclosed the specific technical reasons behind the decision or the extent to which the charging rate has been reduced. This lack of transparency has further fueled user frustration and speculation about the underlying causes of the problem.

In conclusion, the Ars Technica report paints a picture of Google grappling with battery management issues in its Pixel 8 series phones. While the company's intentions may be noble, the execution has been met with criticism from users who feel that their user experience has been negatively impacted. The lack of transparency from Google regarding the specific reasons for the battery capacity reduction has only exacerbated the situation, leaving many Pixel 8 owners feeling frustrated and disappointed.
