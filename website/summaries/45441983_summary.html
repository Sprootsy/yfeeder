ICE Is Buying a Tool to Track Phones, Without Warrants

This article discusses U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement's (ICE) planned acquisition of a new data analytics tool called "Hunchly," which raises significant privacy concerns. Hunchly is a tool designed to collect and archive online information, primarily used in criminal investigations. Its capabilities include automatically scraping and archiving data from websites, social media platforms, and the dark web. The tool is capable of monitoring hundreds of thousands of online sources, and archiving the information for extended periods. This raises the possibility of mass surveillance and tracking of individuals' online activities, even if they are not suspected of any crime. The tool also has features that include real-time alerts and the ability to generate reports based on collected data.

Key Points:

  • ICE plans to acquire Hunchly, a data analytics tool designed to collect and archive online information.
  • Hunchly can scrape and archive data from websites, social media, and the dark web, potentially monitoring hundreds of thousands of online sources.
  • The tool's capabilities raise concerns about mass surveillance and the tracking of individuals' online activities, regardless of criminal suspicion.
  • Hunchly's features include real-time alerts and report generation based on collected data, increasing its surveillance potential.
