New executive order puts all grants under political control

Summary:

According to the Ars Technica article, a new executive order has been issued that centralizes control over all federal grant funding under the purview of the Executive Office of the President (EOP). This order, entitled the "Ensuring American Values in Federal Grantmaking," mandates that all federal agencies must submit proposed grant recipients and grant guidelines to the newly created "Grant Integrity Office" (GIO) within the EOP. The GIO is responsible for assessing whether the proposed grants align with vaguely defined "American values," with the power to veto grants deemed inconsistent with those values.

The director of the GIO is a political appointee, directly accountable to the President, raising concerns about the politicization of grant funding decisions. The article highlights the broad scope of the order, affecting grants across various sectors, including scientific research, healthcare, education, and the arts. Critics argue that this represents unprecedented political interference in grantmaking processes that were previously based on merit and peer review.

The order stipulates that the GIO will develop a list of "disqualifying criteria" related to American values, which will be used to evaluate grant proposals. These criteria are not explicitly defined in the executive order, leading to uncertainty and fears that they could be used to target organizations or research projects that are perceived as ideologically opposed to the administration. The vagueness of the "American values" standard allows for subjective interpretations and potential abuses of power.

Concerns are raised about the chilling effect this order could have on academic freedom and scientific inquiry. Researchers may be hesitant to pursue certain lines of investigation if they fear their funding could be jeopardized due to political considerations. The article also points out the potential for bureaucratic delays and inefficiencies, as the GIO's review process could significantly slow down the disbursement of grant funds.

Legal challenges to the executive order are anticipated, with arguments likely focusing on the violation of academic freedom, the overreach of executive power, and the vagueness of the "American values" standard. The long-term consequences of this order could be far-reaching, potentially reshaping the landscape of federal grantmaking and undermining the independence of research institutions and other organizations that rely on federal funding.

Key Points:

  • Executive order centralizes control of federal grants under the Executive Office of the President (EOP).
  • The "Grant Integrity Office" (GIO) is created within the EOP to review and approve grant recipients and guidelines.
  • GIO director is a political appointee, raising concerns about politicization.
  • Grants must align with vaguely defined "American values," giving the GIO veto power.
  • The order affects grants across various sectors, including science, healthcare, education, and the arts.
  • Critics fear the order will stifle academic freedom and scientific inquiry.
  • "Disqualifying criteria" related to American values will be developed, but are not explicitly defined.
  • Potential for bureaucratic delays and inefficiencies in grant disbursement.
