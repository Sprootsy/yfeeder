Computer fraud laws used to prosecute leaking air crash footage to CNN

According to the article, investigators used the Computer Fraud and Abuse Act (CFAA) to prosecute individuals who leaked footage of an airplane crash to CNN. The article critiques the use of the CFAA in this context, arguing that it's a misapplication of the law. The CFAA, designed to combat hacking and unauthorized access to computer systems, is being used to punish those who accessed a system with authorization but then misused the information obtained.

The article highlights the ongoing debate over the scope and application of the CFAA. Critics argue that the law is overly broad and can be used to target individuals for actions that don't constitute actual hacking or malicious intrusion. In this case, the employees had legitimate access to the system containing the crash footage, but their decision to leak the footage to CNN was deemed a violation of the CFAA.

The author expresses concern that this interpretation of the CFAA could have a chilling effect on whistleblowers and journalists. If individuals fear prosecution for accessing and sharing information, even when they have authorized access, it could discourage them from exposing wrongdoing or providing information to the public. The article suggests that this broad interpretation of the CFAA could be used to punish any employee who violates a company's terms of service or confidentiality agreements, even if the violation doesn't involve malicious intent or harm to a computer system.

The author argues that while leaking the footage may have been unethical or a violation of company policy, it doesn't necessarily warrant criminal prosecution under the CFAA. They suggest that there are other legal and ethical frameworks that could be used to address the situation, such as employment law or breach of contract.

The article implies that the investigators are using the CFAA as a convenient tool to punish individuals for leaking sensitive information, even if their actions don't meet the traditional definition of computer fraud or abuse. It questions whether the law is being used appropriately in this case and raises concerns about the potential for abuse in the future.

In conclusion, the article is critical of the use of the CFAA to prosecute individuals who leaked air crash footage to CNN, arguing that it's an overreach of the law and could have negative consequences for whistleblowers and journalists.
