Writing a good Claude.md

Summary

The article provides guidance on writing effective prompts for Anthropic's Claude, a large language model. It emphasizes that Claude excels when given clear, detailed instructions and relevant context. The article details how Claude, unlike some other models, benefits from longer, more descriptive prompts. The key to good prompting is to think of Claude as a knowledgeable but somewhat unguided assistant who needs detailed instructions to perform optimally. The author advocates for providing background information, specific task descriptions, and desired output formats to achieve the best results. Furthermore, the article outlines a structured approach to prompt engineering for Claude, focusing on elements like defining the role, providing context, specifying the task, setting the tone, and defining the output format. It illustrates these principles with examples and highlights common mistakes to avoid, such as vague instructions or insufficient context. The author also discusses techniques for iterative prompt refinement, where you analyze Claude's responses and adjust your prompts to improve performance. Lastly, the article briefly touches on advanced prompting strategies, including few-shot learning and chain-of-thought prompting, to handle more complex tasks.

Key Points

  • Clarity is Key: Claude benefits from explicit and detailed instructions. Vague prompts lead to suboptimal results.
  • Provide Context: Give Claude sufficient background information to understand the task.
  • Define the Task Precisely: Clearly state what you want Claude to do.
  • Specify Output Format: Tell Claude how you want the response to be formatted (e.g., bullet points, paragraphs, code).
  • Iterative Refinement: Adjust your prompts based on Claude's responses to improve performance over time.
  • Consider Tone: Specify the desired tone or style of the response.
  • Role Definition: Define a role for Claude to help it better understand the context of the request.
  • Avoid Ambiguity: Ensure your prompts are unambiguous and leave no room for misinterpretation.
  • Few-Shot Learning: Provide a few examples of the desired input-output relationship.
  • Chain-of-Thought Prompting: Encourage Claude to explain its reasoning process step-by-step.
