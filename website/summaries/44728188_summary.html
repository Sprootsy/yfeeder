RIP Shunsaku Tamiya, the man who made plastic model kits a global obsession

Shunsaku Tamiya, the president of Tamiya, Inc., passed away at the age of 95. He was instrumental in transforming the company from a lumber supplier into a world-renowned plastic model kit manufacturer. He joined the family business in 1946 and took over as president in 1948 when his father died. He led the company through significant changes, recognizing the potential in plastic model kits. He initially focused on creating accurate model kits of warships, motivated by the inaccuracies he observed in existing wooden models.

Tamiya's early success came from the meticulous recreation of the Japanese battleship Yamato as a plastic model. This attention to detail and accuracy became a hallmark of Tamiya products. Shunsaku Tamiya also recognized the importance of producing models of vehicles that were popular overseas, particularly those from the United States and Germany. This strategy allowed Tamiya to gain international recognition and market share.

A key aspect of Tamiya's success was Shunsaku Tamiya's commitment to quality and accuracy. He prioritized realism and precision in the design and manufacturing of their models, which appealed to serious hobbyists and collectors. Tamiya also pioneered the use of high-quality packaging and clear instructions, which further enhanced the consumer experience.

Beyond model kits, Tamiya also became known for its radio-controlled cars. Shunsaku Tamiya recognized the potential of this market and introduced a line of RC cars that quickly gained popularity. This diversification helped the company to expand its reach and appeal to a broader audience.

Shunsaku Tamiya's leadership and vision were critical to Tamiya's global success. His focus on accuracy, quality, and innovation transformed the company into a leading manufacturer of plastic model kits and RC cars. His legacy continues to influence the hobby industry today.
