US attack on renewables will lead to power crunch that spikes electricity prices

Summary

According to a CNBC article dated August 24, 2025, the renewable energy sector, particularly solar and wind, has become dominant in the United States, leading to significantly lower electricity prices. This shift is attributed to several factors, including technological advancements, the extension of tax credits, and supportive government policies.

The article highlights that the Inflation Reduction Act, passed in 2022, played a crucial role by extending the Investment Tax Credit (ITC) and Production Tax Credit (PTC) for renewable energy projects. These incentives made solar and wind energy more economically competitive compared to traditional fossil fuels. Furthermore, state-level policies, such as renewable portfolio standards and carbon pricing mechanisms, have further accelerated the adoption of renewable energy.

The drop in electricity prices is benefiting consumers and businesses alike. Lower energy costs have boosted household disposable income and made American industries more competitive in the global market. The article also mentions the growth of energy storage technologies, such as batteries, which have helped to address the intermittency issues associated with solar and wind power, making them more reliable sources of electricity.

The impact of the Trump-era tariffs on imported solar panels is also discussed. While these tariffs initially slowed down the growth of the solar industry, domestic manufacturing capacity has since increased, and the overall cost of solar energy has continued to decline. The article notes that the tariffs had a mixed effect, protecting some domestic manufacturers but also raising costs for consumers and project developers.

Looking ahead, the article suggests that renewable energy will continue to grow in importance, driven by further technological innovation, supportive government policies, and increasing concerns about climate change. While challenges remain, such as the need to upgrade the electric grid and address land-use conflicts, the overall outlook for renewable energy is positive. The article concludes that the transition to a clean energy economy is well underway and will likely continue to accelerate in the years to come.

Key Points

  • Renewable energy sources, especially solar and wind, have become dominant in the US electricity market.
  • Electricity prices have significantly decreased due to the rise of renewables.
  • The Inflation Reduction Act, which extended tax credits for renewable energy projects, played a crucial role.
  • Lower energy costs have benefited consumers and businesses.
  • Energy storage technologies have helped address the intermittency of renewable energy.
  • Trump-era tariffs on imported solar panels had mixed effects, slowing growth initially but also spurring domestic manufacturing.
  • The renewable energy sector is expected to continue growing due to innovation, policy support, and climate change concerns.
