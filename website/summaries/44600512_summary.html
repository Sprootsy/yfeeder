USB-C hubs and my slow descent into madness (2021)

Summary

The article is a deep dive into the author's frustrating experience trying to find a reliable USB-C hub for their specific needs, highlighting the inconsistencies and deceptive marketing practices prevalent in the USB-C hub market. The author needed a hub that could reliably support two 4K 60Hz monitors via DisplayPort, along with USB 3.0 ports for peripherals and Power Delivery (PD) for charging their laptop. They purchased and tested numerous hubs from various brands, including Anker, Cable Matters, and others, only to find that many failed to meet the advertised specifications or suffered from various issues like flickering displays, USB disconnects, or insufficient power delivery.

The core problem lies in the complexity of the USB-C standard and the lack of clear labeling and certification for hubs. USB-C can carry different protocols (USB data, DisplayPort, Power Delivery) over the same connector, and the capabilities of a hub depend on how these protocols are handled internally. Many hubs advertise support for features like "4K 60Hz" without specifying crucial details such as whether it's supported over both ports simultaneously or only one, or whether it will share bandwidth with USB data. The author found that many hubs internally use DisplayPort Multi-Stream Transport (MST) to drive multiple displays, which can cause compatibility issues and limitations compared to DisplayPort Single-Stream Transport (SST). Some hubs also falsely advertise USB 3.0 speeds when they are actually using USB 2.0 internally.

The article details the author's testing methodology, which involved using a high-resolution oscilloscope to verify USB data transfer speeds and observing the behavior of connected displays and peripherals under different load conditions. They found significant discrepancies between the advertised and actual performance of many hubs, and identified common failure modes like insufficient bandwidth allocation or poor power management.

The author emphasizes the importance of understanding the underlying technology and not solely relying on manufacturer specifications. They also suggest that consumers should demand more transparency and accurate information from hub manufacturers, and call for better certification and labeling standards to reduce confusion and prevent deceptive marketing. The author concludes that finding a reliable USB-C hub for demanding use cases requires careful research, testing, and a healthy dose of skepticism.

Key Points

  • The USB-C hub market is plagued by inconsistent performance and misleading advertising.
  • Many hubs fail to reliably support dual 4K 60Hz displays, USB 3.0 speeds, and adequate power delivery simultaneously.
  • The complexity of the USB-C standard and the lack of clear specifications contribute to the problem.
  • DisplayPort Multi-Stream Transport (MST) is often used internally for dual displays, leading to compatibility issues.
  • Many hubs falsely advertise USB 3.0 speeds, but internally use USB 2.0.
  • Thorough testing is crucial to verify the advertised performance of a USB-C hub.
  • Consumers should demand more transparency and better certification standards for USB-C hubs.
