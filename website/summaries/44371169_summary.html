National Archives at College Park, MD, will become a restricted federal facility

This article is about the National Archives at College Park, Maryland, which is one of the main facilities of the National Archives and Records Administration (NARA). It highlights the facility's function as a repository for a vast collection of archival materials, including textual records, photographs, maps, films, and electronic records. These holdings document a wide array of topics, such as the activities of the U.S. Federal Government, military history, and genealogical research.

The article describes the types of records available at College Park, giving examples such as records from the Continental Congress, the U.S. Senate and House of Representatives, various federal agencies, and military branches. It notes the facility's importance for researchers, historians, genealogists, and the general public seeking to access these primary sources.

Beyond its role as an archive, the article emphasizes that the College Park facility offers research support services, including reference assistance, finding aids, and online resources. It provides information on how to access the archives, including details on visiting the facility, conducting research remotely, and ordering copies of records.
