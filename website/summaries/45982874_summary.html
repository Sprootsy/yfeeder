Netherlands returns control of Nexperia to Chinese owner

The Dutch government has ordered Nexperia, a Chinese-owned chipmaker, to unwind its 2021 acquisition of Nowiport, citing national security concerns. This decision reverses a previous ruling and highlights growing worries in Europe about China's influence in critical industries. Nexperia has been instructed to sell Nowiport within a specified timeframe. The Dutch government's intervention reflects a broader trend of increased scrutiny of foreign investments, particularly those from China, in sectors deemed strategic. This move underscores the Netherlands' commitment to protecting its technological sovereignty amid escalating geopolitical tensions.


Key points:

  • The Dutch government is forcing Nexperia to sell Nowiport.
  • The decision is based on national security concerns.
  • The move reverses a previous ruling allowing the acquisition.
  • It reflects growing European unease about Chinese influence in key industries.
  • The Netherlands is demonstrating a commitment to protecting its technological sovereignty.
