AOL closes its dial up internet service

Summary:

After 34 years, AOL has officially closed its dial-up internet service. Once a dominant force in the early days of the internet, AOL's dial-up service provided millions with their first access to the online world. The company's iconic CDs, offering free trial periods, became a ubiquitous symbol of the internet's rise in the 1990s.

However, with the advent of broadband technologies like DSL and cable, AOL's dial-up service gradually declined in popularity. Faster connection speeds and always-on access made broadband a more attractive option for consumers. Despite the shift, a small but dedicated user base continued to rely on AOL's dial-up service, either due to its affordability or the lack of broadband options in their area.

AOL was acquired by Verizon in 2015, and later sold to Apollo Global Management in 2021, with the company being renamed Yahoo. The closure of the dial-up service marks the end of an era for a company that played a pivotal role in shaping the early internet experience. While AOL's dial-up service is now a thing of the past, the company's legacy as a pioneer in online communication and content remains. The closure represents the final curtain for a technology that once connected millions but was ultimately superseded by faster and more efficient alternatives.
