Stephen Miller's Quota Likely Drove Korean Arrests in Immigration Raid

The Forbes article, published on September 16, 2025, discusses the potential motivations behind a recent immigration raid that led to the arrest of several Korean individuals. The author, Stuart Anderson, suggests that a quota system implemented by Stephen Miller, likely influenced the arrests. The article posits that Miller, known for his hard-line immigration policies during his time in the Trump administration, may have implemented a quota or target for immigration arrests, specifically focusing on Korean immigrants to fulfill certain objectives. The author suggests that the arrests may have been driven by a desire to meet pre-set numerical targets rather than solely based on violations of immigration laws. The article examines the possibility that the arrests are a consequence of policies that prioritize numerical outcomes over individual circumstances and due process.

Key Points:

  • The article examines a recent immigration raid that resulted in the arrest of several Korean individuals.
  • It suggests that Stephen Miller's quota system might have influenced the arrests.
  • Stephen Miller previously implemented hard-line immigration policies during the Trump administration.
  • The arrests of Korean immigrants may be driven by meeting numerical targets rather than based on actual violations of immigration laws.
  • The article raises concerns about prioritizing numerical outcomes over individual circumstances and due process in immigration enforcement.
