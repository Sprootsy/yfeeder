Eastern Baltic cod grow much smaller than they did due to overfishing

The article from Smithsonian Magazine discusses the phenomenon of shrinking cod populations in the North Sea. For decades, scientists have observed a significant decrease in the size of these fish, and the study sheds light on the primary driver behind this change: genetics and fishing practices. The study's conclusion states that the size of the cod is decreasing due to genetics and fishing practices.

Key Points:

  • Cod in the North Sea have been shrinking in size for decades.
  • Scientists have identified genetics and fishing practices as the main cause.
