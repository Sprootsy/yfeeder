What “working” means in the era of AI apps

Summary

The Andreessen Horowitz (a16z) article "Revenue Benchmarks for AI Apps" analyzes key revenue metrics for AI-powered applications. The report focuses on understanding what constitutes good performance for AI apps, which often differ from traditional SaaS models due to varying cost structures and user behaviors. It aims to provide benchmarks and insights for founders, investors, and operators in the AI application space.

The report categorizes AI apps into different segments based on their primary function and business model, such as those focused on productivity enhancement, content creation, or specific industry verticals. It highlights that AI apps can generate revenue through various mechanisms, including subscription models (monthly or annual recurring revenue), usage-based pricing (pay-as-you-go), and outcome-based pricing (where payment is tied to the value delivered by the AI). The document underscores the importance of selecting a pricing model that aligns with the app's value proposition and the customer's needs.

Key performance indicators (KPIs) discussed in the article include annual recurring revenue (ARR), customer acquisition cost (CAC), customer lifetime value (LTV), and gross margin. The authors emphasize that traditional SaaS benchmarks for CAC and LTV may not directly apply to AI apps due to the potential for viral growth, lower marginal costs, and different customer acquisition strategies. For example, AI apps often leverage product-led growth (PLG) strategies, where the product itself drives user acquisition and expansion.

The report also delves into the impact of AI infrastructure costs (such as compute and data storage) on gross margins. It points out that these costs can be significant for AI apps, especially those involving large language models (LLMs) or extensive data processing. Therefore, optimizing infrastructure costs and improving model efficiency are critical for achieving healthy gross margins. The authors stress that as AI technology evolves, the infrastructure costs could decrease, potentially improving the profitability of AI apps.

Furthermore, the article examines the importance of user engagement and retention for AI apps. High user engagement, indicated by metrics like daily/monthly active users (DAU/MAU) and session duration, is crucial for demonstrating the value of the AI app and driving long-term customer retention. The authors also mention the significance of continuous model improvement through feedback loops and retraining, which can enhance the app's performance and user experience over time.

The report provides guidance on how to approach revenue forecasting and financial planning for AI apps, taking into account the unique characteristics of the AI business model. This includes considering the potential for non-linear growth, the impact of AI infrastructure costs, and the importance of continuous model improvement. By establishing appropriate revenue benchmarks and KPIs, AI app developers can better understand their performance, make informed decisions, and attract investment.

Key Points

  • The article provides revenue benchmarks specifically for AI-powered applications, acknowledging differences from traditional SaaS.
  • AI apps can utilize subscription, usage-based, or outcome-based pricing models.
  • Traditional SaaS benchmarks for CAC and LTV may not be directly applicable; AI apps often leverage product-led growth.
  • AI infrastructure costs (compute, data storage) significantly impact gross margins. Optimizing these costs is crucial.
  • User engagement (DAU/MAU, session duration) is critical for demonstrating value and driving retention.
  • Continuous model improvement through feedback loops enhances app performance and user experience.
  • The report emphasizes the importance of establishing appropriate revenue benchmarks and KPIs for financial planning and investment attraction.
