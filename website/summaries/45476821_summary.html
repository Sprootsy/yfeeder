Summary

The article describes a novel method for generating high-quality, full-length cDNA libraries from limited biological samples, particularly focusing on laser capture microdissected (LCM) tissue. The technique, called Matrix-Cores cDNA synthesis, addresses the challenges posed by working with minute quantities of RNA, such as incomplete cDNA synthesis, bias in representation, and loss of rare transcripts. The method leverages a solid support, streptavidin-coated magnetic beads, to immobilize mRNA molecules. This immobilization facilitates efficient enzymatic reactions and minimizes sample loss during purification steps. The key steps involve: (1) mRNA capture using biotinylated oligo(dT) primers attached to the magnetic beads; (2) first-strand cDNA synthesis directly on the beads; (3) RNase H digestion to remove the RNA template; (4) second-strand cDNA synthesis using a novel "core" primer, which contains a defined sequence that allows for subsequent amplification and library construction; (5) release of the double-stranded cDNA from the beads; and (6) amplification using PCR with primers specific to the core sequence. The Matrix-Cores cDNA synthesis method offers several advantages over traditional methods: * **Improved cDNA yield:** By performing enzymatic reactions on a solid support, the method minimizes sample loss during purification and ensures efficient conversion of mRNA into cDNA. * **Full-length cDNA synthesis:** The optimized protocol and reaction conditions promote the synthesis of full-length cDNA molecules, which are essential for accurate gene expression analysis and functional studies. * **Reduced bias:** The method reduces bias in cDNA representation by employing optimized enzyme concentrations and reaction conditions. * **Simplified workflow:** The protocol involves fewer purification steps compared to traditional methods, making it less labor-intensive and more amenable to automation. * **Compatibility with various downstream applications:** The generated cDNA libraries are compatible with various downstream applications, such as quantitative PCR (qPCR), microarray analysis, and next-generation sequencing (NGS). The article provides a detailed protocol for the Matrix-Cores cDNA synthesis method, including a list of required materials, step-by-step instructions, and optimization tips. It also presents experimental data demonstrating the performance of the method, showing that it produces high-quality, full-length cDNA libraries from limited amounts of RNA. The method is validated using LCM samples. The results demonstrate the method's ability to generate representative cDNA libraries suitable for various downstream applications.
