The article reports on the aftermath of Typhoon Haikui, which struck Taiwan, particularly focusing on the extensive damage and recovery efforts in Taitung County. The typhoon brought record-breaking rainfall and strong winds, causing widespread flooding, power outages, and damage to infrastructure and agriculture.

Taitung County was hit the hardest, with numerous areas experiencing severe flooding and landslides. Many roads were blocked, isolating communities and hindering rescue operations. The typhoon caused significant damage to homes, businesses, and public facilities, leaving many residents without electricity and water. The agricultural sector also suffered heavy losses, with crops destroyed by the storm. The Council of Agriculture estimated agricultural losses to be NT$185 million, as well as forestry and fisheries losses.

In response to the disaster, the Taiwanese government mobilized resources and personnel to assist with rescue and recovery efforts. Military personnel were deployed to help clear debris, repair infrastructure, and deliver essential supplies to affected areas. Emergency shelters were set up to provide temporary housing for those displaced by the typhoon. The government also allocated funds for disaster relief and reconstruction.

Despite the challenges, residents and local authorities worked tirelessly to restore normalcy to their communities. Volunteers assisted with clean-up efforts, and utility companies worked to restore power and water services. The government and private organizations provided assistance to farmers and businesses to help them recover from their losses.

The article also highlights the resilience and solidarity of the Taiwanese people in the face of natural disasters. Despite the devastation caused by Typhoon Haikui, communities came together to support each other and rebuild their lives. The experience served as a reminder of the importance of disaster preparedness and the need for continued investment in infrastructure and resilience measures.
