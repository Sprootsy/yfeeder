System 7 natively boots on the Mac mini G4

The article is a forum post on the MacOS9Lives forums discussing the possibility of running Mac OS 9 on modern hardware, specifically Apple Silicon Macs, through emulation or virtualization. The original poster, "nick77uk," is enthusiastic about the idea and inquires about the progress of any such projects, mentioning previous attempts like SheepShaver and discussions around QEMU. They express a desire to use classic Mac software on their new M1 Mac. Other users chime in, providing a mix of technical insights, encouragement, and cautionary notes. Some users discuss the challenges involved, such as the differences in architecture between older PowerPC Macs and modern ARM-based Macs, as well as the complexities of emulating or virtualizing older operating systems. The performance limitations of emulation are also mentioned. Basilisk II and SheepShaver are discussed, along with their known limitations. One user, "adespoton," points out the theoretical possibility of using the Rosetta translation layer on Apple Silicon to aid in the process but notes that it would require significant reverse engineering and development effort. Some users also discuss the legal aspects of running Mac OS 9 on non-Apple hardware, referencing Apple's software license agreements. Alternative solutions like using older Mac hardware or virtualizing Mac OS X Snow Leopard (which can run PowerPC applications through Rosetta) are also suggested as more practical approaches. The general consensus seems to be that while running Mac OS 9 on Apple Silicon is technically challenging and faces legal hurdles, there is ongoing interest and some theoretical possibilities, although a fully functional and practical solution is not yet readily available.
