Paris had a moving sidewalk in 1900, and a Thomas Edison film captured it (2020)

Summary:

The article discusses the moving sidewalk, also known as the “rue roulante,” that was a feature of the 1900 Exposition Universelle in Paris. This innovative transportation system allowed visitors to traverse a significant portion of the fairgrounds with ease. The moving sidewalk consisted of three elevated platforms: a stationary platform, a platform moving at a moderate speed, and a faster-moving platform. Passengers could step onto the slower platform and then transfer to the faster one to quickly cover ground. The article highlights the novelty and popularity of this attraction during the Exposition, noting it as an early example of mechanized public transportation. It also touches upon the broader context of technological advancements and urban planning that characterized the era.

Key Points:

  • The 1900 Exposition Universelle in Paris featured a moving sidewalk called the “rue roulante.”
  • The moving sidewalk had three platforms: stationary, slow-moving, and fast-moving.
  • It allowed visitors to quickly traverse the fairgrounds.
  • The moving sidewalk was a popular attraction at the Exposition.
  • The sidewalk represents an early example of mechanized public transportation.
