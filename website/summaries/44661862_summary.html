A diverse cast of rocky worlds around a small star revealed by astronomers

Summary

A team of researchers at Université de Montréal (UdeM), led by doctoral student Étienne Artigau, has confirmed the existence of a fifth planet orbiting the red dwarf star L 98-59, located 35 light-years away. This exoplanetary system is notable because it is the closest one discovered to date that resembles our own solar system.

The initial discovery of three planets in this system was made by NASA's Transiting Exoplanet Survey Satellite (TESS). The UdeM team, using the Near-Infrared Spectrograph (NIRPS) installed on the European Southern Observatory's (ESO) telescope in Chile, has now confirmed the existence of a fourth and fifth planet. This is the first time a planet has been detected in this system using the radial velocity method, which measures the wobble of a star caused by the gravitational pull of its orbiting planets.

The newly confirmed planets include L 98-59 e, which is only about 2.9 times the mass of Earth, and L 98-59 f, which resides in the habitable zone of the star. The habitable zone is the region around a star where temperatures could allow liquid water to exist on a planet's surface, making it potentially habitable for life as we know it. L 98-59 f is not the only planet residing in the habitable zone, as L 98-59 d was also found to be located in it.

The L 98-59 system is of particular interest to astronomers because the planets are relatively close to their star, allowing for detailed study of their atmospheres (if they have any). The UdeM team's work has also provided valuable data on the planets' masses and orbital periods, allowing for a better understanding of the system's architecture. Future observations, particularly with instruments like the James Webb Space Telescope, could potentially reveal more about the atmospheric composition of these planets and whether they could potentially host life.

The confirmation of these planets, especially the potentially habitable ones, makes the L 98-59 system a prime target for future exoplanet research. This research highlights the ongoing efforts to discover and characterize exoplanets and the potential for finding other habitable worlds beyond our solar system.

Key Points

  • A UdeM team confirmed the existence of a fifth planet around the red dwarf L 98-59.
  • The L 98-59 system is located 35 light-years away and is the closest system resembling our solar system.
  • The Near-Infrared Spectrograph (NIRPS) was used to detect the planets using the radial velocity method.
  • L 98-59 e is about 2.9 times the mass of Earth, and L 98-59 f is located in the habitable zone.
  • L 98-59 d is the other planet located in the habitable zone.
