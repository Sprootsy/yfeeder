How to title your blog post or whatever

This article is a humorous exploration of various types of titles people use, both official and unofficial, and how they reflect status, expertise, or personality. It categorizes titles into several sections, including legal titles, professional titles, earned but potentially inflated titles, silly titles, and titles indicating experience. The author playfully examines the nuances of each category, highlighting how titles can be used to impress, amuse, or even mislead. The piece is written in a lighthearted and engaging style, making it an entertaining read for anyone interested in the social dynamics and psychology behind the use of titles.

Key Points:

  • Legal Titles: These are legally recognized titles like "Mr.", "Ms.", "Dr." (for medical doctors), and titles of nobility. Their use is generally straightforward and indicates a recognized status.
  • Professional Titles: These titles reflect a person's profession or role within an organization, such as "Engineer," "Manager," or "CEO." Their value depends on the context and the legitimacy of the organization.
  • Earned (Maybe) Titles: This category includes titles earned through education or experience but might be inflated or used in contexts where they don't fully apply. Examples include academic titles or certifications.
  • Silly Titles: These are informal, humorous titles people adopt or are given, such as "Chief Troublemaker" or "Supreme Overlord." They're often used for fun and to express personality.
  • Titles of Experience: These titles reflect a person's level of experience or seniority, like "Senior Developer" or "Master Craftsman." They indicate a certain level of skill and expertise.
