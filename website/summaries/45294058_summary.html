U.S. already has the critical minerals it needs, according to new analysis

According to a new analysis, the U.S. already possesses significant quantities of the critical minerals required for clean energy technologies and other essential applications. However, instead of being utilized, these valuable resources are currently being discarded as waste. The research highlights the potential to recover critical minerals from various waste streams, including mine tailings, coal ash, and electronic waste, which could significantly reduce the nation's reliance on foreign sources and bolster domestic supply chains.

Key Points:

  • The U.S. has domestic sources of critical minerals.
  • These minerals are currently being wasted.
  • Recovery from waste streams could reduce reliance on foreign sources.
