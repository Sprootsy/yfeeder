The Onion brought back its print edition and the gamble is paying off

Summary

The Onion, known for its satirical news, has seen unexpected success with its print edition, gaining over 14,000 subscribers since launching in select markets. This growth bucks the trend of declining print media and highlights a demand for tangible, humorous content.

Originally conceived as a way to experiment and generate content ideas, the print edition has become a profitable venture. It offers a curated selection of articles from The Onion's website and archives, alongside exclusive content created specifically for print. The print edition is visually appealing, designed with a retro aesthetic.

The subscribers are a mix of longtime fans and new readers drawn to the novelty of a print newspaper. This success has led The Onion to expand its distribution and consider new print-related projects, such as limited-edition items.

Key Points

  • The Onion's print edition has gained over 14,000 subscribers.
  • The print edition was initially launched as an experiment and source of content ideas.
  • The print edition includes a curated selection of articles from The Onion's website and archives, as well as exclusive content.
  • The print edition has a retro aesthetic design.
  • The Onion is considering expanding its print ventures.
