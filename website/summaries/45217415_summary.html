Float Exposed

The provided URL leads to the website Float.exposed, which is a photography project by visual artist, Cody Cobb. The project documents the otherworldly landscapes of the American West, captured through long-exposure photography at night. The images showcase the unique beauty of the desert environment, emphasizing the starkness, stillness, and ethereal qualities of these remote locations.

Cobb's work focuses primarily on the desert regions of the American West. He captures images using long exposure techniques at night. This process allows him to reveal details and light patterns that are invisible to the naked eye. The photographs often feature desolate landscapes, geological formations, and subtle gradations of light and shadow. His website serves as a portfolio and blog, containing entries about his journeys, equipment, and artistic philosophy.

The project's aim is to immerse viewers in the alien beauty of the desert at night. The images portray a sense of solitude and silence, offering a fresh perspective on familiar landscapes. By highlighting the interplay of light and darkness, Cobb creates a dreamlike and surreal atmosphere in his photographs.

Float.exposed has been featured in various online publications, gallery shows, and print magazines. Cobb's photographic style emphasizes minimalism, composition, and the use of natural light, thereby evoking a sense of wonder and contemplation. His photographs reveal hidden beauty in the seemingly barren landscapes of the American West, encouraging viewers to appreciate the serenity and isolation that these places offer.
