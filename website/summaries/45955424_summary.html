Israeli-founded app preloaded on Samsung phones is attracting controversy

Summary: The article discusses the controversy surrounding AppCloud, an Israeli app pre-installed on Samsung phones in certain regions, particularly in the Middle East and Africa. AppCloud is a third-party app store that allows users to download apps, but it has raised concerns due to its potential security risks, unwanted ads, and the fact that it cannot be easily uninstalled or disabled. While Samsung has stated that AppCloud is included to provide localized app recommendations and is subject to security checks, users and security experts have expressed reservations about its presence on devices. Some have also pointed to potential privacy implications due to the data AppCloud collects.

Key Points:

  • AppCloud is a third-party app store pre-installed on some Samsung phones.
  • It is mainly found on devices in the Middle East and Africa.
  • AppCloud offers localized app recommendations.
  • Concerns exist regarding potential security risks associated with the app store.
  • Users have reported unwanted advertisements through AppCloud.
  • The app is difficult to uninstall or disable.
  • Samsung claims that AppCloud undergoes security checks.
  • Privacy concerns exist regarding the data collected by AppCloud.
  • The app's presence has sparked controversy among users and security experts.
