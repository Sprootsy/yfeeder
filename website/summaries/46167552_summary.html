Leaving Intel

Brendan Gregg announces his departure from Intel at the end of 2025. He reflects on his time at the company, which began in January 2020 after Intel's acquisition of Netflix performance tools team. He joined as a Principal Engineer, initially focusing on cloud performance engineering.

He highlights key projects and contributions during his tenure. These include developing performance analysis tools, with focus on extending the Linux perf tool with advanced profiling capabilities to better understand Intel CPUs. He also worked on improving cloud performance analysis methodologies and infrastructure within Intel. Gregg mentions challenges related to integrating into a large organization and aligning priorities, while also underscoring the learning experiences and opportunities he gained.

The decision to leave Intel was influenced by multiple factors. Gregg acknowledges a shift in his career interests and a desire to explore new challenges outside of the specific domain of CPU-focused performance engineering. He expresses a longing to return to a broader scope of performance analysis, potentially involving diverse hardware and software systems.

Gregg emphasizes his gratitude for the experiences and relationships he cultivated at Intel. He thanks his colleagues, managers, and the company as a whole for the opportunities he was given. He also touches on the supportive environment he found within certain teams.

Looking forward, Gregg doesn't explicitly state his next career move, but conveys his intention to pursue new opportunities aligned with his evolving interests in broader performance analysis and potentially different domains. He anticipates sharing updates on his future endeavors in the future.
