Seagate achieves 6.9TB storage capacity per platter

Seagate has achieved a significant breakthrough in hard drive technology, reaching a storage density of 6.9 TB per platter in its laboratory. This advancement paves the way for the potential development of hard drives with capacities ranging from 55 TB to 69 TB. The achievement was made possible by combining two key technologies: heat-assisted magnetic recording (HAMR) and superlattice platinum-alloy media. HAMR enables writing data on smaller areas of the disk, while the new media facilitates higher density storage. While there is not an ETA when these drives might become commercially available, this milestone signals continued innovation in HDD technology, which remains crucial for meeting the ever-increasing demands for data storage, especially in hyperscale data centers and other environments where high capacity and cost-effectiveness are paramount. The increase in per-platter capacity directly translates to higher drive capacities without increasing the number of platters or the drive's physical size. This advancement could lead to lower costs per terabyte and reduced energy consumption per stored bit, addressing key concerns in large-scale data storage.
