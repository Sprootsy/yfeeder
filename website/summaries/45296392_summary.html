ICE unit signs new $3M contract for phone-hacking tech

The U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) has signed a new \$3 million contract for phone hacking technology. This contract renews ICE's access to tools that can extract data from mobile devices, including contacts, call logs, messages, and location information. Privacy advocates have raised concerns about the use of such technology, citing potential for abuse and violations of civil liberties. They argue that the broad scope of data extraction could disproportionately affect immigrant communities and others who may be targeted by ICE.

Key Points:

  • ICE has signed a \$3 million contract for phone hacking technology.
  • The technology allows ICE to extract data from mobile devices.
  • Extracted data includes contacts, call logs, messages, and location information.
  • Privacy advocates are concerned about potential abuse and civil liberties violations.
  • Concerns include the disproportionate impact on immigrant communities.
