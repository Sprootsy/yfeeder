A day at Hetzner Online in the Falkenstein data center

The article provides a detailed look inside Hetzner Online's data center in Falkenstein, Germany. It covers various aspects of the company's operations, including server technology, cooling methods, production processes, returns management (RMA), and sustainability efforts. The article highlights Hetzner's focus on efficiency, cost-effectiveness, and environmental responsibility.

Summary

The visit to Hetzner's Falkenstein data center reveals a highly optimized and vertically integrated operation. Hetzner designs and assembles its own servers, allowing for customization and cost control. The server hardware utilizes standard components, carefully chosen for performance and reliability. The production process involves automated steps and manual quality checks. Cooling is achieved through free cooling, adiabatic cooling, and heat recovery systems, minimizing energy consumption. Hetzner also emphasizes sustainability, using renewable energy sources and optimizing resource utilization. The company handles returns and repairs in-house, further streamlining operations. The article also touches upon security measures implemented within the data center.

Key Points

  • Server Design and Assembly: Hetzner designs and assembles its own servers, offering customization and cost efficiency.
  • Standard Components: Servers utilize carefully selected standard components for performance and reliability.
  • Production Process: The production process uses automation and manual quality control.
  • Cooling Systems: Hetzner uses free cooling, adiabatic cooling, and heat recovery to minimize energy consumption.
  • Sustainability: Hetzner is committed to sustainability through renewable energy and efficient resource use.
  • Returns Management (RMA): Returns and repairs are managed internally for streamlined operations.
  • Security: Security measures are implemented within the data center.
