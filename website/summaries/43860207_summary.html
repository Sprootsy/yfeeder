Arizona laptop farmer pleads guilty for funneling $17M to Kim Jong Un

An Arizona woman, Thanh Nguyen, has pleaded guilty to running a laptop farm involved in a large-scale fraud scheme. Nguyen and her accomplices purchased over 1,000 laptops and used them to file fraudulent applications for COVID-19 pandemic unemployment assistance (PUA) and other benefits. The scheme involved using stolen identities, including those of deceased individuals and homeless people, to submit the applications. Nguyen's role involved managing the laptops, processing the applications, and receiving and disbursing the fraudulently obtained funds.

Key Points:

  • Thanh Nguyen pleaded guilty to operating a laptop farm for fraud.
  • The scheme involved over 1,000 laptops used to file fraudulent unemployment benefit applications.
  • Stolen identities, including those of deceased and homeless individuals, were used.
  • Nguyen managed the laptops, processed applications, and handled the fraudulent funds.
