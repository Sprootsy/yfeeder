“Secret Mall Apartment,” a Protest for Place

Summary

The article, "Secret Mall Apartment: A Protest for Place," explores the story of Michael Townsend and his group of artists who secretly built and lived in an apartment inside the Providence Place Mall in Rhode Island for approximately four years. The narrative delves into the motivations behind this audacious act, which stemmed from a desire to critique consumerism, question the use of public space, and explore the concept of home in an increasingly commercialized world.

Townsend, an artist with a background in architecture, noticed an unutilized space within the mall – a cavity behind a blank wall. Inspired by the possibilities, he and his collaborators meticulously planned and constructed a small apartment complete with basic amenities like a PlayStation, kitchenette, and living area. They accessed the apartment through an unlocked service door and blended in with mall employees to avoid detection.

The apartment became their secret sanctuary, a place where they could escape the constant barrage of advertising and consumer culture. They used it as a space for creative projects, contemplation, and social gatherings. The act of living in the mall was a form of protest, a way to reclaim a small piece of public space that had been privatized and commodified.

However, their secret existence eventually came to an end when Townsend was caught on mall security cameras. He was arrested and faced trespassing charges. The discovery of the apartment sparked media attention and ignited a debate about the ownership and use of public spaces, the impact of consumerism on society, and the role of art as a form of social commentary.

The article analyzes the deeper implications of Townsend's actions, positioning the mall apartment as a symbolic act of resistance against the homogenizing forces of corporate culture. It highlights the tension between private property rights and the public's right to access and utilize spaces within their communities. The story also raises questions about the nature of home in a society where traditional notions of dwelling are increasingly challenged by economic pressures and changing lifestyles.

Ultimately, the "Secret Mall Apartment" serves as a provocative example of how individuals can challenge the status quo and spark important conversations about the spaces we inhabit and the values that shape our society. It is a reminder that art can be a powerful tool for social critique and that even the most seemingly ordinary places can become sites of resistance and reimagination.
