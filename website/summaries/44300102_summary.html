Summary:

Honda held a briefing on its electrification strategy, outlining key initiatives and future plans. The company aims to achieve 100% electric vehicle (EV) sales globally by 2040. To achieve this goal, Honda is adopting a comprehensive strategy focused on hardware and software development, strategic partnerships, and infrastructure development.

Regarding hardware, Honda is developing a new EV architecture called "Honda 0 Series," emphasizing design, automated driving, connected technologies, and performance. They are also focusing on battery technology, with plans for next-generation batteries, including all-solid-state batteries, aiming for mass production in the latter half of the 2020s. They are working to secure battery procurement through strategic partnerships globally.

In software, Honda is developing its own operating system to enhance the user experience and enable advanced features like over-the-air (OTA) updates, personalized services, and connectivity. They are also working on advanced driver-assistance systems (ADAS) and automated driving technologies to improve safety and convenience.

Honda is also strengthening its partnerships. They are collaborating with companies worldwide to develop and produce EVs, batteries, and other related technologies. These partnerships aim to accelerate development, reduce costs, and expand Honda's global reach.

Infrastructure development is another crucial aspect of Honda's strategy. The company is investing in charging infrastructure, including partnerships to expand the availability of charging stations and develop innovative charging solutions. They are also exploring energy management systems to optimize energy use and support the grid.

Honda aims to introduce a series of new EV models globally, catering to various customer needs and preferences. These EVs will incorporate Honda's latest technologies and design philosophies, aiming to provide a unique and compelling ownership experience.

Key Points:

  • Goal: Achieve 100% EV sales globally by 2040.
  • Honda 0 Series: New EV architecture focusing on design, automated driving, connectivity, and performance.
  • Battery Technology: Development of next-generation batteries, including all-solid-state batteries, aiming for mass production in the late 2020s. Secure battery procurement through strategic partnerships.
  • Software Development: Developing a proprietary operating system for enhanced user experience, OTA updates, and advanced features.
  • Strategic Partnerships: Collaborating with companies globally for EV development, battery production, and related technologies.
  • Infrastructure Development: Investing in charging infrastructure and exploring energy management systems.
  • New EV Models: Introducing a range of new EV models globally to meet diverse customer needs.
