Pentagon orders states' national guards to form 'quick reaction forces'

Summary:

A leaked Pentagon memo details the planned deployment of quick reaction forces across the United States in anticipation of potential civil unrest following the 2024 presidential election. The memo, dated September 2025 but reflecting plans developed earlier, outlines preparations to address scenarios where state National Guard units are insufficient or unwilling to quell disturbances. These quick reaction forces, composed of active-duty military personnel, would be stationed at various military bases around the country, ready to be deployed within hours. The plan sparks controversy and raises concerns about the militarization of domestic law enforcement and the potential violation of the Posse Comitatus Act, which generally prohibits the use of the U.S. military for domestic law enforcement purposes. The memo describes different levels of alert and deployment criteria, emphasizing the need for readiness and coordination with civilian authorities. It also addresses potential legal challenges and outlines rules of engagement designed to minimize the risk of civilian casualties. Critics argue the plan reflects a deeply troubling lack of faith in the democratic process and a willingness to use military force against American citizens engaged in political protest. Supporters argue that such preparations are necessary to ensure public safety and maintain order in the event of widespread violence or insurrection. The existence of the memo itself fuels further division and distrust, with some seeing it as evidence of a potential authoritarian power grab, while others view it as a prudent measure to safeguard the nation against internal threats.
