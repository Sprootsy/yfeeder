FTC bans hidden fees for live events and short-term rentals, effective May 12

The Federal Trade Commission (FTC) has announced a new rule banning hidden and deceptive fees across all industries, with a specific focus on live events and short-term rentals. Formally known as the "Rule on Unfair or Deceptive Fees," this regulation mandates that companies disclose all mandatory fees upfront, ensuring consumers know the total cost of a product or service before committing to a purchase. The rule is set to take effect on May 12, 2025.

The FTC estimates that hidden fees cost Americans tens of billions of dollars annually. These fees, often added late in the purchasing process, make it difficult for consumers to compare prices and can lead to significant overspending. The new rule aims to combat these practices by requiring businesses to clearly and conspicuously display the total price, including all mandatory fees, from the outset. This includes fees for things like processing, service, or any other mandatory charges.

Specifically, for live events (such as concerts, sporting events, and theater performances) and short-term rentals (like vacation homes and apartments), the rule will require that the initial price displayed include all unavoidable fees. This means that consumers will see the full price, inclusive of all mandatory charges, before selecting their seats or booking their stay. The FTC believes this will bring transparency and fairness to these markets, where hidden fees are particularly prevalent.

The FTC's move has been praised by consumer advocacy groups, who argue that it will empower consumers to make more informed decisions and promote competition among businesses. By eliminating hidden fees, companies will be forced to compete on the true cost of their products and services, rather than relying on deceptive pricing practices. The rule is expected to face legal challenges from industry groups who argue that it will increase compliance costs and reduce flexibility in pricing. However, the FTC maintains that the benefits to consumers outweigh these concerns.

The rule applies to all businesses operating in the United States, regardless of size. The FTC has indicated that it will actively monitor compliance and pursue enforcement actions against companies that violate the rule. Penalties for non-compliance could include fines and other legal remedies. The FTC is providing guidance to businesses to help them understand and comply with the new requirements. This includes examples of compliant and non-compliant pricing displays, as well as resources for implementing the necessary changes to their pricing systems.

The implementation of this rule marks a significant step towards greater transparency and fairness in the marketplace. Consumers can expect to see more straightforward pricing practices across various industries, particularly in live events and short-term rentals, starting May 12, 2025.

Key points:

  • The FTC has issued a rule banning hidden and deceptive fees, effective May 12, 2025.
  • The rule requires businesses to disclose all mandatory fees upfront, showing the total price inclusive of all fees.
  • It specifically targets live events and short-term rentals, where hidden fees are common.
  • The aim is to provide pricing transparency and promote fair competition.
  • The FTC will monitor compliance and enforce the rule, with penalties for violations.
