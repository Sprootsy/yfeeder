The story behind Caesar salad

Summary of "The Story Behind Caesar Salad"

The National Geographic article "The Story Behind Caesar Salad" explores the origins of the popular salad, tracing its creation back to Caesar Cardini, an Italian immigrant who ran a restaurant in Tijuana, Mexico. The commonly accepted story is that in 1924, a busy weekend depleted the kitchen's supplies, and Cardini improvised a salad using what was available: romaine lettuce, croutons, Parmesan cheese, eggs, Worcestershire sauce, garlic, olive oil, and lime juice.

The article highlights that while the exact details are debated, the Cardini family maintains that Caesar himself created the original recipe. His daughter, Rosa Cardini, is credited with preserving and promoting the salad's legacy. Other accounts suggest that one of Cardini's employees, Livio Santini, or his brother, Alex Cardini, may have played a role in the salad's invention or popularization.

The salad quickly gained popularity among Hollywood celebrities who frequented Tijuana during Prohibition. Its fame spread throughout the United States and eventually worldwide. The article also discusses the evolution of the recipe over time, noting that anchovies, while often included today, were not part of the original salad; the Worcestershire sauce provided the necessary umami flavor.

The article further delves into the various claims and counterclaims surrounding the salad's true origins, acknowledging the difficulty in definitively proving any single version of events. It emphasizes the importance of the Cardini family's role in popularizing the salad and establishing its association with their name. Ultimately, the Caesar salad's enduring appeal lies in its simplicity, flavor, and the captivating story of its creation on a busy night in Tijuana. The salad's history is a mix of culinary ingenuity, family legacy, and the allure of Hollywood glamour.

Key Points

  • Caesar salad was likely created by Caesar Cardini in Tijuana, Mexico, around 1924.
  • The original recipe included romaine lettuce, croutons, Parmesan cheese, eggs, Worcestershire sauce, garlic, olive oil, and lime juice.
  • The Cardini family, especially Caesar's daughter Rosa, played a crucial role in preserving and promoting the salad's legacy.
  • There are competing claims regarding the salad's exact origins, including suggestions that Cardini's employees or brother contributed to its creation or popularization.
  • The salad gained popularity due to its appeal to Hollywood celebrities visiting Tijuana during Prohibition.
  • Anchovies were not part of the original recipe; the umami flavor came from Worcestershire sauce.
  • The Caesar salad's enduring appeal is due to its simple ingredients, delicious flavor, and the fascinating story of its creation.
