LinkedIn is loud, and corporate is hell

The article "LinkedIn is Loud" by ramones.dev discusses the author's perception of the current state of LinkedIn as being overly noisy and filled with content that lacks substance. The author expresses frustration with the platform's shift towards becoming a generic social media platform, filled with motivational content, humblebrags, and irrelevant discussions, rather than a professional networking site focused on job opportunities and industry-specific discussions. The author feels that the signal-to-noise ratio on LinkedIn has decreased significantly, making it difficult to find valuable and relevant content.

Key Points:

  • The author believes that LinkedIn has become overly noisy and filled with irrelevant content.
  • The platform's focus has shifted from professional networking to generic social media content.
  • The author finds much of the content on LinkedIn to be shallow, including motivational posts, humblebrags, and engagement-baiting questions.
  • The signal-to-noise ratio on LinkedIn has decreased, making it difficult to find valuable content.
  • The author expresses frustration with the platform's current state.
