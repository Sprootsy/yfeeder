This article discusses the digitization and online availability of a vast collection of occult books. The Ritman Library in Amsterdam, also known as the Bibliotheca Philosophica Hermetica, has digitized a significant portion of its collection, making over 2100 books freely accessible online. These texts delve into esoteric subjects such as alchemy, astrology, magic, mysticism, Rosicrucianism, and Hermeticism. The digitization project allows scholars, researchers, and anyone interested in these topics to explore a wide range of historical documents that were previously difficult to access. The collection includes illuminated manuscripts, printed books, and other rare materials spanning several centuries.

The article highlights the importance of this project in preserving and democratizing access to knowledge about Western esotericism. By making these texts available online, the Ritman Library is contributing to a greater understanding of the history of ideas and the influence of occult traditions on Western culture. The digitized collection can be accessed through the Ritman Library's website and other online platforms, enabling users to search, browse, and study these texts from anywhere in the world.
