Occult books digitized and put online by Amsterdam’s Ritman Library

Summary

Open Culture reports that over 2,178 occult books have been digitized and made available online, primarily through the Ritman Library’s Bibliotheca Philosophica Hermetica collection. These texts cover a wide array of esoteric topics, including alchemy, astrology, Kabbalah, Hermeticism, Rosicrucianism, Freemasonry, and related fields. The digitization effort provides researchers, enthusiasts, and the curious with access to rare and historically significant materials that were previously difficult to access. The collection includes manuscripts and printed books, some dating back centuries, offering insight into the history of Western esotericism and its influence on culture, science, and philosophy. The project underscores the growing trend of making cultural heritage accessible through digital archives, allowing for broader engagement with these sometimes obscure and marginalized areas of intellectual history.

Key Points

  • Over 2,178 occult books have been digitized.
  • The books are primarily from the Ritman Library’s Bibliotheca Philosophica Hermetica collection.
  • The collection includes texts on alchemy, astrology, Kabbalah, Hermeticism, Rosicrucianism, and Freemasonry.
  • The digitization provides access to rare and historically significant materials.
  • The books include both manuscripts and printed works, some dating back centuries.
  • The project enhances access to the history of Western esotericism.
  • The digital archive facilitates broader engagement with esoteric intellectual history.
