The article is a blog post announcing the public release of 90s.dev, a project that aims to recreate the experience of browsing the web in the 1990s. The author details the motivations behind the project, the technical challenges involved, and the features that have been implemented to achieve this goal.

The author begins by reminiscing about the early days of the internet, highlighting the unique aesthetic and user experience that characterized websites of that era. They express a desire to revisit and preserve this aspect of internet history, which they feel has been lost in the modern web's focus on minimalism and streamlined design.

The core idea behind 90s.dev is to provide a platform where users can create and share websites that embody the design principles and technologies of the 1990s. This includes features such as:

  • Retro design elements: The platform encourages the use of animated GIFs, tiled backgrounds, marquees, and other visual elements that were common in 90s web design.
  • HTML 3.2 and early CSS: 90s.dev focuses on using the web technologies that were prevalent during the 1990s.
  • Guestbooks and counters: These elements, which were common on early websites, are included as a way to foster interaction and a sense of community.
  • Low-resolution images: The platform embraces the use of smaller, more pixelated images, in line with the limitations of the technology at the time.

The author acknowledges the technical challenges involved in recreating this experience. Modern browsers and web technologies have evolved significantly since the 1990s, and it was necessary to find ways to emulate the behavior of older browsers and design elements while still ensuring that the websites function correctly on modern devices.

The blog post also discusses the importance of community involvement in the project. The author encourages users to contribute their own designs, code, and ideas to help expand and improve the platform. They envision 90s.dev as a collaborative effort to preserve and celebrate the history of the web.
