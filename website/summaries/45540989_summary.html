Regarding the Compact

Summary

In a letter addressed to the MIT community, MIT President Sally Kornbluth discusses the MIT Compact, an evolving financial and operational model designed to support the institute's mission in education, research, and innovation. The Compact aims to balance ambitious goals with financial sustainability amid increasing costs and a changing higher education landscape.

The letter highlights the challenges MIT faces, including rising expenses in areas like research, infrastructure, and financial aid. These rising costs necessitate a strategic approach to resource allocation to ensure that MIT can continue to offer a transformative education, conduct groundbreaking research, and contribute to solving global challenges. The Compact is presented as MIT's way of addressing these financial realities while upholding its core values and commitment to excellence.

President Kornbluth emphasizes the importance of community involvement in shaping the Compact's evolution. The letter encourages open dialogue and feedback from faculty, students, and staff to ensure that the Compact reflects the diverse needs and priorities of the MIT community. The goal is to create a shared understanding of the financial constraints and opportunities facing the institute and to work collaboratively to find sustainable solutions.

The communication underscores that the MIT Compact is not a static document but an ongoing process of adaptation and improvement. It acknowledges that difficult choices may be necessary to ensure the long-term financial health of the institution but stresses that these decisions will be made with careful consideration of their impact on MIT's mission and values. The letter conveys a sense of optimism and determination, expressing confidence that the MIT community can work together to navigate the challenges ahead and emerge stronger and more resilient.

President Kornbluth frames the MIT Compact as a critical tool for preserving MIT's unique character and ensuring its continued leadership in science and technology. The goal is to enable MIT to continue attracting top talent, supporting cutting-edge research, and making a positive impact on the world.

Key Points

  • The MIT Compact is an evolving financial and operational model.
  • It is designed to balance ambitious goals with financial sustainability.
  • Rising expenses in research, infrastructure, and financial aid are key challenges.

  • The Compact aims to preserve MIT's unique character and leadership in science and technology.
