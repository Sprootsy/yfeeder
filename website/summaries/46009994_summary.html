California DMV approves map increase in Waymo driverless operations

The California DMV has approved Waymo's application for driverless testing and deployment in specific areas. Waymo is authorized to operate autonomous vehicles without a driver behind the wheel within defined regions of Los Angeles County, Orange County, San Francisco, San Mateo County, and Santa Clara County.

The approved areas of operation exclude certain conditions. Waymo vehicles are not permitted to operate during heavy fog or heavy rain. The authorization also prohibits operations on specified roads and highways, including but not limited to:

  • Any area within the City and County of San Francisco
  • Specific sections of State Route 1 (SR-1) in San Mateo County, such as areas near Devil's Slide
  • Interstate 280 (I-280) in San Mateo and Santa Clara Counties
  • Interstate 880 (I-880) in Alameda and Santa Clara Counties
  • State Route 85 (SR-85) in Santa Clara County
  • US Highway 101 (US-101) in San Mateo and Santa Clara Counties
  • Interstate 680 (I-680) in Contra Costa and Alameda Counties
  • State Route 24 (SR-24) in Contra Costa and Alameda Counties

The detailed maps outlining the approved areas of operation are available for each county, providing precise boundaries for Waymo's driverless activities. These maps are accessible as PDF documents through the California DMV website, with each county having its own specific map detailing the permissible operational zones.

Key Points:

  • Waymo has received approval from the California DMV for driverless testing and deployment.
  • Approved areas are located in Los Angeles County, Orange County, San Francisco, San Mateo County, and Santa Clara County.
  • Operations are restricted during heavy fog and heavy rain.
  • Operations are prohibited on specified roads and highways in various counties.
  • Detailed maps outlining the approved areas are available on the DMV website.
