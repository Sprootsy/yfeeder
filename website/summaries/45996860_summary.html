The data collected by these devices is stored in a central database that can be accessed by DHS officials and other law enforcement agencies. The system is designed to track the movement of people and vehicles across the border, but it also captures data on people who are not suspected of any wrongdoing.

DHS officials defend the program, saying that it is necessary to secure the border and to protect national security.
