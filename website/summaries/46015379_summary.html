'The French people want to save us': help pours in for glassmaker Duralex

Summary:

The article reports on the outpouring of support for Duralex, the iconic French glass manufacturer, after it faced financial difficulties. In 2020, Duralex was placed in receivership, leading to concerns about the future of the brand and its historic factory in La Chapelle-Saint-Mesmin. The article highlights how the French public rallied to save the company, driven by nostalgia, a sense of national pride, and a commitment to preserving French industrial heritage. This support manifested in increased sales, with people actively seeking out Duralex products in stores. The article also discusses the role of Antoine de Navacelle, who took over as CEO in January 2021, in revitalizing the company. De Navacelle implemented a new business plan focused on modernizing production, reducing energy consumption, and expanding into new markets while maintaining the brand's core identity. The commitment of Duralex employees, many of whom have worked at the factory for generations, is also emphasized as a crucial factor in the company's survival. The article suggests that Duralex's story reflects a broader desire within France to protect its industrial base and promote sustainable production.

Key Points:

  • Duralex, the iconic French glass manufacturer, faced financial difficulties and was placed in receivership in 2020.
  • The French public demonstrated strong support for Duralex, driven by nostalgia and a desire to preserve French industrial heritage.
  • This support translated into increased sales of Duralex products.
  • Antoine de Navacelle became CEO in January 2021 and implemented a plan to revitalize the company.
  • The revitalization plan included modernizing production, reducing energy consumption, and expanding into new markets.
  • The dedication of Duralex employees, many with long family histories at the factory, was vital to the company's survival.
  • Duralex's story reflects a broader French desire to protect its industrial base and promote sustainable production.
