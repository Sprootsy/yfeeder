What will become of the CIA?

The article is a review of Tim Weiner's book, "The Mission: The CIA in the 21st Century." The review discusses the CIA's activities and transformations in the post-9/11 era, particularly focusing on its role in counterterrorism, intelligence gathering, and covert operations. It examines the agency's successes and failures, highlighting controversies and debates surrounding its methods and effectiveness. The review analyzes how the CIA has adapted to new threats and challenges, including those posed by rogue states, terrorist organizations, and cyber warfare. It also delves into the internal dynamics of the CIA, exploring the impact of bureaucratic structures, political pressures, and ethical dilemmas on its operations.

The review emphasizes the CIA's increased reliance on drone warfare and targeted killings, as well as the ethical and legal implications of these practices. It scrutinizes the agency's intelligence gathering capabilities, including its surveillance programs and its efforts to penetrate foreign governments and organizations. The review also addresses the CIA's relationship with other intelligence agencies and its role in shaping U.S. foreign policy. It assesses the agency's performance in predicting and preventing terrorist attacks, as well as its involvement in regime change operations and other covert activities. The review concludes by offering a critical perspective on the CIA's legacy in the 21st century and its future prospects in an increasingly complex and dangerous world.
