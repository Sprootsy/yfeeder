ICC Judge Guillou loses all US IT accounts after MAGA sanctions [FR]

The France Info article "Quand les sanctions internationales émises par Washington imposent une vie déconnectée à un magistrat français" discusses the impact of US sanctions on a French magistrate, focusing on the unintended consequences of these measures.

The article centers on Alain Juillet, a former senior intelligence official and magistrate in France, who has found himself caught in the web of US sanctions due to his professional activities and associations. These sanctions, primarily economic, are designed to target individuals and entities involved in activities deemed contrary to US foreign policy objectives, such as terrorism, nuclear proliferation, or human rights abuses.

However, the broad reach of US sanctions, coupled with the extensive influence of the US financial system, means that even individuals and entities not directly targeted can experience significant disruptions in their daily lives. Juillet's case exemplifies this phenomenon.

The core issue is Juillet's inclusion on a US sanctions list, which the article suggests stems from his professional activities and his perceived connections to sanctioned entities or individuals. While the specific reasons for his blacklisting aren't exhaustively detailed, the consequences are vividly portrayed. He faces significant constraints on his ability to conduct international financial transactions. Banks and financial institutions, fearing repercussions from the US government, often refuse to process transactions involving sanctioned individuals, even if those transactions are perfectly legal under French or international law. This has led to a "disconnected life" for Juillet, limiting his ability to engage in international commerce, travel, and even manage his personal finances effectively.

The article highlights the extraterritorial reach of US law and the complexities it creates for individuals and businesses in other countries. It raises questions about the fairness and proportionality of these sanctions, particularly when they impact individuals who may not be directly involved in the activities the sanctions are intended to deter.

Furthermore, the article touches upon the challenges faced by European countries in navigating the tension between complying with US sanctions and protecting the interests of their own citizens and businesses. It suggests that there is growing concern in Europe about the overreach of US sanctions and the potential for these measures to undermine European sovereignty and economic interests.

In essence, the article uses Alain Juillet's experience to illustrate the far-reaching and often unintended consequences of US sanctions, highlighting the challenges they pose for individuals and businesses operating outside the United States.
