Danish department determined to dump Microsoft

The Danish Agency for Digital Government is reportedly planning to discontinue its use of Microsoft Office in favor of open-source alternatives across the country's public sector. This decision stems from concerns regarding data security and vendor lock-in associated with Microsoft's cloud-based services. A risk assessment apparently highlighted issues relating to data privacy and compliance with EU regulations, particularly regarding the transfer of data to the United States. The Danish government aims to promote greater digital sovereignty and reduce dependence on proprietary software by adopting open-source solutions.

Key Points:

  • The Danish Agency for Digital Government intends to replace Microsoft Office with open-source software in the public sector.
  • Data security and vendor lock-in are the primary drivers behind the decision.
  • A risk assessment revealed concerns about data privacy and compliance with EU regulations related to data transfer to the US.
  • The move aims to increase digital sovereignty and reduce reliance on proprietary software.
