Plex Security Incident

The article discusses Plex's announcement that it is ending support for plugins, including the Unsupported App Store (UAS), effective November 30, 2023. Plex cites low usage and the maintenance burden as the primary reasons for this decision. The announcement also covers the end of support for "plugins" which, within Plex's ecosystem, are different from "integrations." The article explains the distinction between plugins and integrations: plugins are older technology, while integrations are more modern. Plex states that integrations are the future and that they will continue to support them.

The impact of this decision will be felt by users who rely on plugins for accessing content or enhancing their Plex experience. While Plex acknowledges the inconvenience, they emphasize that only a small percentage of users actively use plugins. The article implies that Plex is focusing its resources on developing and supporting integrations, which offer a more streamlined and efficient way to connect with various services and content providers.

The article suggests that users should explore available integrations as alternatives to the plugins they currently use. It highlights Plex's commitment to evolving its platform and focusing on features that benefit the majority of its users. The removal of plugins is portrayed as a strategic move to simplify the platform and prioritize more modern technologies.

Key Points:

  • Plex is ending support for plugins, including the Unsupported App Store (UAS), on November 30, 2023.
  • Low usage and high maintenance costs are the reasons behind the decision.
  • Plex differentiates between plugins (older technology) and integrations (newer technology).
  • Plex will continue to support integrations.
  • The decision impacts users who rely on plugins for accessing content or enhancing their Plex experience.
  • Plex is focusing on integrations as the future of its platform.
  • Users are encouraged to explore available integrations as alternatives to plugins.
