The un-celebrity president: Jimmy Carter shuns riches, lives modestly (2018)

The article profiles Jimmy Carter's post-presidency life in Plains, Georgia, focusing on his modest lifestyle and dedication to service. Despite having opportunities to amass wealth through book deals and speaking engagements like other former presidents, Carter has chosen to live a simple life in the same house he built before becoming president. He continues to teach Sunday school, build houses with Habitat for Humanity, and monitor elections around the world.

Carter's financial choices are contrasted with those of other presidents, highlighting his commitment to a life of purpose over profit. The article emphasizes his hands-on approach to philanthropy and his deep connection to his hometown. It suggests that Carter's decision to live modestly and dedicate his life to service reflects his values and provides him with a different kind of fulfillment than accumulating wealth.

The article also touches on the town of Plains, Georgia, and how Carter's presence has affected the community. It describes the economic challenges faced by the town and the ways in which Carter's activities have brought attention and tourism to the area.

The article paints a picture of a former president who has deliberately chosen a path of service and simplicity, eschewing the trappings of wealth and celebrity that often come with the office.
