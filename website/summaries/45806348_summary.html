When stick figures fought

Summary:

The article, titled "When Stick Figures Fought," delves into the history and impact of stick figure animation on the internet, primarily focusing on its rise in the early 2000s and its connection to online creativity and animation culture. It highlights the accessibility and simplicity of stick figure animation as key factors in its widespread adoption, allowing amateur animators to create and share their work easily using basic software like Macromedia Flash (later Adobe Flash).

The piece traces the origins of stick figure animation to pre-internet sources, noting examples in educational materials and early computer games, but emphasizes that its true explosion occurred online. It credits sites like Newgrounds as crucial platforms for showcasing and popularizing stick figure animations, fostering a community of creators and viewers. One of the most important aspects of Newgrounds was that it allowed anyone to upload content, leading to many different kinds of animations, games and movies.

The article discusses the evolution of stick figure animation from simple, comedic shorts to more elaborate and action-oriented projects, particularly focusing on the genre of stick figure fighting animations. It mentions early influential animations such as those by Protojman and the emergence of series like "Xiao Xiao" by Zhu Zhiqiang (credited as CrazyFrog), which were renowned for their fluid animation and intense fight choreography, setting a new standard for the medium.

It explores the techniques used by stick figure animators to achieve dynamic and visually appealing action sequences, including the use of keyframes, tweening, and creative camera angles within the limitations of the format. Furthermore, the text touches upon the software used, emphasizing the importance of Flash and its intuitive tools for frame-by-frame animation.

The article also examines the cultural impact of stick figure animation, noting its influence on subsequent animation styles and its role in democratizing animation production. It emphasizes that many professional animators and artists began their careers creating stick figure animations, using it as a training ground to learn fundamental animation principles. It shows that the simplicity and open nature of stick figure animation lowered the bar for entry, and fostered the talent of many future artists.

Finally, it suggests that while stick figure animation's mainstream popularity may have waned over time, its legacy continues to be felt in online animation communities, webcomics, and the broader animation landscape. The article indicates that it remains a viable and accessible form of animation for beginners and hobbyists. The article shows that stick figure animation was an important step in the history of internet animation.

Key Points:

  • Stick figure animation gained immense popularity in the early 2000s due to its accessibility and simplicity.
  • Newgrounds was a key platform for showcasing and popularizing stick figure animations.
  • Animations like "Xiao Xiao" raised the bar for stick figure fighting animations, demonstrating complex fight choreography.
  • Macromedia Flash (later Adobe Flash) was the primary software used for creating stick figure animations.
  • Stick figure animation served as a training ground for many professional animators and influenced later animation styles.
  • While its mainstream popularity has decreased, stick figure animation continues to be a relevant and accessible form of animation online.
