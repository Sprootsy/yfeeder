Weighting an average to minimize variance

The article discusses how to find the minimum variance unbiased estimator (MVUE) of a parameter. It explains that if you have an unbiased estimator, you can sometimes reduce its variance by conditioning on a sufficient statistic. The Rao-Blackwell theorem states that if you have an unbiased estimator and a sufficient statistic, conditioning the estimator on the sufficient statistic will produce another estimator that is also unbiased but has a variance no greater than the original estimator.

The article provides an example using the exponential distribution. If you have a sample from an exponential distribution and you want to estimate the parameter λ, the reciprocal of the sample mean is an unbiased estimator. However, the sum of the samples is a sufficient statistic for λ. By conditioning the initial estimator (reciprocal of the first sample) on the sufficient statistic (sum of all samples), a new estimator is derived. This new estimator, which is (n-1) divided by the sum of the samples, is the MVUE for λ. It is unbiased, and no other unbiased estimator has a smaller variance.

The article mentions that finding a sufficient statistic is key to improving an estimator. It also notes that if a sufficient statistic is also complete, then any estimator based on it is the unique MVUE.
