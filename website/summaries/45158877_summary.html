Nepal Bans 26 Social Media Platforms, Including Facebook and YouTube

The article reports that Nepal has banned major social media platforms, including Facebook, Instagram, X, YouTube, TikTok, and others. The ban, enacted in September 2025, is framed by the Nepali government as a necessary step to maintain social harmony, curb misinformation, and regulate content deemed harmful or disruptive.

The government cites concerns over the proliferation of fake news, hate speech, and content that threatens national security as primary reasons for the ban. They claim that these platforms have been used to spread disinformation and incite violence, leading to social unrest and instability. Additionally, the government expresses frustration with the platforms' alleged failure to adequately address these issues despite repeated requests for cooperation.

The ban has sparked widespread controversy and debate within Nepal. Supporters of the ban, including some politicians and conservative groups, argue that it is essential for preserving cultural values and preventing social disorder. They believe that the platforms have become breeding grounds for negativity and that the ban will help restore a sense of community and stability.

However, critics of the ban, including human rights organizations, journalists, and many Nepali citizens, denounce it as a severe infringement on freedom of expression and access to information. They argue that the ban will stifle dissent, limit political discourse, and isolate Nepal from the global community. They also raise concerns about the lack of transparency and due process in the decision-making process, as well as the potential for abuse of power by the government.

The ban has significant implications for various sectors in Nepal. Businesses that rely on social media for marketing and communication are facing challenges in reaching their customers. Journalists and media outlets are struggling to disseminate information and engage with their audiences. Activists and human rights defenders are finding it more difficult to organize and advocate for their causes.

The article also notes that the ban is part of a growing trend among some governments around the world to restrict access to social media platforms in the name of national security or social order. This trend raises concerns about the future of internet freedom and the role of social media in democratic societies.

The Nepali government's action has drawn international criticism from human rights organizations and digital rights advocates, who are urging the government to reconsider the ban and uphold its commitments to freedom of expression and access to information. The situation in Nepal is being closely monitored as a test case for the balance between government regulation and digital freedoms in the age of social media.
