Google admits anti-competitive conduct involving Google Search in Australia

Summary:

The Australian Competition and Consumer Commission (ACCC) has announced that Google has admitted to engaging in anti-competitive conduct concerning Google Search in Australia. The admission relates to Google's conduct regarding updates to its search algorithm that impacted the visibility of certain comparison shopping websites. Specifically, the ACCC alleged that Google altered its algorithm in a way that demoted or suppressed the rankings of comparison shopping services (CSS) in its organic search results, while simultaneously favoring its own Google Shopping service.

The ACCC's investigation focused on Google's actions from 2017 to 2020. The core issue was that Google allegedly used its dominance in the general search market to give its own comparison shopping service an unfair advantage, thereby hindering the ability of other CSS providers to compete on merit. This conduct potentially harmed consumers by limiting their access to a diverse range of product options and potentially leading to higher prices.

Google's admission acknowledges that it engaged in conduct that contravened Australian competition law. While the specifics of the algorithm changes are complex, the fundamental concern is that Google leveraged its market power to prioritize its own commercial interests over providing consumers with the most relevant and unbiased search results. This is a significant development as it marks a formal acknowledgment from Google regarding anti-competitive behavior in the Australian market.

The ACCC and Google are now expected to engage in discussions regarding the appropriate remedies for this anti-competitive conduct. These remedies could include financial penalties, changes to Google's search algorithm, and measures to ensure fair competition in the comparison shopping market. The outcome of these discussions will likely have implications for how Google operates its search engine in Australia and potentially set a precedent for other jurisdictions where similar concerns have been raised.

The ACCC's action against Google underscores the importance of maintaining a level playing field in digital markets. It highlights the potential for dominant platforms to engage in self-preferencing behavior that can harm competition and ultimately disadvantage consumers.

  • The ACCC and Google will now discuss remedies, which could include financial penalties and changes to Google's algorithm.
