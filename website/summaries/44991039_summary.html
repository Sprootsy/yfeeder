Mail Carriers Pause US Deliveries as Tariff Shift Sows Confusion

Several global mail services, including Royal Mail, Deutsche Post, and Canada Post, have announced they will halt deliveries to the United States in anticipation of the end of the de minimis exception. This exception allowed goods valued under $800 to enter the U.S. duty-free and with minimal inspection. The decision to suspend services stems from concerns regarding increased costs and potential delays associated with stricter import regulations once the de minimis threshold is eliminated.

The de minimis rule has facilitated the rapid growth of e-commerce by reducing the burden of customs duties and taxes on low-value shipments. However, it has also faced criticism for allegedly enabling the entry of counterfeit goods, products that violate intellectual property rights, and items that pose security risks. Lawmakers and domestic industries have argued for its repeal or modification to level the playing field and protect American businesses.

The suspension of services by these major postal operators is expected to significantly impact cross-border e-commerce, particularly for small businesses and consumers who rely on affordable international shipping options. The changes could lead to higher prices for imported goods, longer delivery times, and more complex customs procedures. Alternative shipping methods, such as using private courier services, may be more expensive.

The U.S. government has not yet officially announced the end of the de minimis exception, but the mail services are preparing for what they believe is an inevitable change in policy. The exact date for the elimination of the de minimis rule is not specified in the article.
