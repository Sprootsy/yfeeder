Self-hosting email like it's 1984

Summary:

The article, written as a blog post from October 2025 by Max Adamski, reflects on the changing role of email in personal and professional communication. Adamski observes that email, once a dominant form of digital correspondence, has significantly declined in importance due to the rise of more efficient and immediate communication methods. These alternatives include advanced messaging platforms, collaborative workspaces, and AI-powered communication tools.

Adamski begins by reminiscing about the early days of email, when it was a novel and revolutionary way to communicate asynchronously. He acknowledges its initial usefulness for formal communication, document sharing, and record-keeping. However, he argues that email's inherent inefficiencies, such as the constant influx of spam, the time-consuming process of sorting and responding to messages, and the lack of real-time interaction, have gradually rendered it obsolete for many purposes.

The blog post highlights the emergence of new communication technologies that have surpassed email in various aspects. Messaging apps like Slack and Microsoft Teams are presented as superior alternatives for team collaboration and internal communication, offering features like instant messaging, file sharing, and integrated project management tools. These platforms enable faster and more organized communication, reducing the need for lengthy email threads.

Adamski also discusses the impact of AI on communication. He mentions AI-powered tools that can automatically summarize emails, filter out irrelevant information, and even generate responses. While these tools can alleviate some of the burdens associated with email, Adamski suggests that they also highlight email's fundamental shortcomings. He suggests that the need for AI to manage email underscores the fact that email itself is an inefficient communication medium.

Furthermore, the article touches upon the changing expectations of communication speed and responsiveness. In a fast-paced world, people increasingly expect immediate responses, a need that email, with its asynchronous nature, struggles to fulfill. Messaging apps and real-time collaboration tools are better suited to meet these demands.

Adamski concludes by suggesting that while email may not disappear entirely, its role will continue to diminish. He predicts that it will primarily be relegated to specific use cases, such as formal legal correspondence or communication with organizations that have not yet adopted more modern communication methods. For the majority of everyday communication, email will be replaced by more efficient, immediate, and intelligent alternatives. The article ultimately presents a nostalgic yet forward-looking perspective on the evolution of digital communication, acknowledging email's historical significance while recognizing its decline in the face of technological advancements.

Key Points:

  • Email is becoming obsolete due to the rise of more efficient communication methods.
  • Messaging apps and collaborative workspaces offer faster and more organized communication.
  • AI-powered tools can help manage emails, but also highlight email's inefficiencies.
  • Expectations for communication speed have increased, making email less suitable.
  • Email's role will diminish to specific use cases like legal correspondence.
  • New communication tools are more immediate, efficient, and intelligent than email.
