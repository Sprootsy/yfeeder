Millihertz 5 Mechanical Computer (2022)

The article discusses the design and development of a five-fingered robotic hand, named MHZ5, intended for versatile grasping and manipulation tasks. It focuses on the hand's mechanical structure, control system, and software interface. The MHZ5 hand incorporates under-actuated mechanisms, meaning fewer actuators than degrees of freedom, to achieve adaptive grasping, where the fingers conform to the shape of the object being grasped. Each finger has multiple joints and sensors to provide feedback on contact forces and joint positions. The hand is designed to be lightweight yet strong, using materials like aluminum alloy and carbon fiber. The control system employs a microcontroller to manage the actuators and process sensor data. A software interface allows users to program and control the hand's movements and grasping strategies.

The design emphasizes adaptability, dexterity, and ease of use. The under-actuated design allows the fingers to wrap around objects of various shapes without precise pre-programming of each joint. The multiple degrees of freedom enable a wide range of grasping postures and in-hand manipulation capabilities. The integrated sensors provide valuable feedback for force control and object recognition. The article highlights the potential applications of the MHZ5 hand in areas such as manufacturing, healthcare, and robotics research.
