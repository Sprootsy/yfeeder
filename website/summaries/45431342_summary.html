U.S. Army confirms Tesla Cybertruck can't be imported in Europe

Summary:

According to a report, the U.S. Army has confirmed that the Tesla Cybertruck cannot be imported into Europe. The article references a video showcasing the Cybertruck at the Central Region UAS Integration Complex (CRUIC) at Fort Custer, Michigan, where soldiers were evaluating the vehicle. The discussion in the video apparently included the U.S. Army stating that the Cybertruck does not meet European regulations and, therefore, cannot be imported there.

Key Points:

  • The U.S. Army has confirmed that the Tesla Cybertruck cannot be imported into Europe.
  • The confirmation was reportedly made during the evaluation of the Cybertruck at Fort Custer, Michigan.
  • The reason for the import ban is due to the Cybertruck not meeting European regulations.
