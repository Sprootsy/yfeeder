Microsoft doubles down on small modular reactors and fusion energy

Microsoft has joined the World Nuclear Association as it increases its focus on small modular reactors (SMRs) and fusion energy. This move signifies Microsoft's commitment to exploring nuclear energy as a viable solution to meet its growing energy demands, particularly for powering data centers and advancing its AI capabilities. The company aims to leverage nuclear energy to achieve its goal of being carbon negative by 2030. Microsoft's interest in nuclear power is driven by its need for reliable, carbon-free energy sources to support its expanding cloud infrastructure and AI development, which require substantial amounts of electricity. By joining the WNA, Microsoft gains access to industry expertise and a platform for collaboration in the nuclear sector.

Key Points

  • Microsoft joins the World Nuclear Association (WNA).
  • Microsoft is focusing on small modular reactors (SMRs) and fusion energy.
  • The move supports Microsoft's goal of being carbon negative by 2030.
  • Microsoft needs reliable, carbon-free energy for data centers and AI development.
  • Joining the WNA provides access to industry expertise and collaboration opportunities.
