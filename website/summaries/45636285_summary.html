Airliner hit by possible space debris

Summary:
A United Airlines Boeing 737 MAX 8, operating as flight UA433 from Sarasota, Florida, to Chicago O'Hare, experienced an unusual incident at 36,000 feet. While en route, the aircraft was struck by an unidentified falling object. The impact resulted in damage to a leading edge slat on the left wing. Despite the damage, the pilots continued the flight and safely landed the aircraft at Chicago O'Hare. Upon arrival, a post-flight inspection revealed the damage to the slat. The aircraft was subsequently taken out of service for repair. The origin and nature of the object that struck the plane remain unknown, and the incident is under investigation.
