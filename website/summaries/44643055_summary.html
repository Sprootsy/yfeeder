Workers at Snopes.com win voluntary recognition

The NewsGuild-CWA announced that workers at Snopes.com, a fact-checking website, have won voluntary union recognition. This means that Snopes management has agreed to recognize the Snopes Union without requiring a formal election process overseen by the National Labor Relations Board (NLRB). The Snopes Union is composed of approximately 20 employees across various departments, including editorial, product, and tech.

According to the NewsGuild, the workers at Snopes decided to form a union to address issues such as improving workplace conditions, promoting transparency, and securing better benefits. The union aims to have a collective voice in decisions affecting their employment and the overall direction of Snopes.

The voluntary recognition by Snopes management is viewed as a positive step, indicating a willingness to collaborate with the union. The NewsGuild-CWA emphasizes the importance of this collaborative approach, stating that it will facilitate a more constructive dialogue between the employees and management as they move forward to negotiate a collective bargaining agreement. This agreement will outline the terms and conditions of employment for the union members.

The NewsGuild-CWA represents thousands of journalists and other media professionals across the United States and Canada.
