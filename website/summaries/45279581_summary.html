When Computer Magazines Were Everywhere

Summary:

The article is a nostalgic look back at the golden age of computer magazines, particularly during the 1980s and 1990s. It describes a time when these magazines were a primary source of information, software, and community for computer enthusiasts, before the widespread availability of the internet. The author reminisces about the excitement of visiting newsstands and bookstores to find the latest issues, each packed with reviews, tutorials, type-in programs, and advertisements for hardware and software. The magazines served not only as a way to learn about new technologies but also as a form of entertainment and a connection to a broader community of like-minded individuals.

The author highlights the importance of cover disks, which were often included with the magazines and contained demo software, utilities, and even full games. These disks were a major draw for readers, offering a tangible and affordable way to experience new software. The article also touches on the role of computer magazines in shaping the early gaming culture, providing reviews, previews, and cheat codes that were eagerly consumed by gamers. The sense of anticipation and discovery associated with these magazines is contrasted with the instant access to information available today through the internet.

Furthermore, the article explores the diverse range of magazines catering to different computer platforms and interests, from general-purpose computing to specific systems like the Commodore 64, Amiga, and Atari. Each magazine had its own unique style and focus, fostering a sense of loyalty among its readership. The author also reflects on the eventual decline of computer magazines as the internet became more prevalent, offering a more convenient and comprehensive source of information. Despite their diminished role today, the article argues that these magazines played a crucial role in popularizing personal computing and fostering a vibrant community of enthusiasts. The author also acknowledges some magazines that are still being published to this day.

Key Points:

  • Computer magazines were a primary source of information and software before the internet.
  • Visiting newsstands to find the latest issues was exciting.
  • Magazines contained reviews, tutorials, type-in programs, and advertisements.
  • Cover disks with demo software and games were a major draw.
  • Magazines played a key role in shaping early gaming culture.
  • There was a wide variety of magazines catering to different platforms and interests.
  • The internet led to the decline of computer magazines.
  • Computer magazines helped popularize personal computing and fostered a community.
  • Some magazines are still published today.
