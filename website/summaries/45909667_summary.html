My dad could still be alive, but he's not

Summary:

The article is a personal narrative about the author's experience with her father's declining health and eventual death, which she believes could have been prevented with better medical care. The author recounts how her father, a veteran, began experiencing various health issues, including shortness of breath and fatigue, which were initially misdiagnosed or dismissed by medical professionals. She details the challenges she faced in navigating the healthcare system, particularly the Veterans Affairs (VA) system, and the frustration of feeling like her concerns about her father's condition were not taken seriously. She describes instances where she felt the medical staff was condescending and failed to properly investigate her father's symptoms, leading to delayed diagnoses and inadequate treatment. The author emphasizes the importance of advocating for loved ones within the healthcare system and highlights the systemic issues within the VA that may have contributed to her father's premature death. She expresses deep regret and anger over the perceived medical negligence and the lack of empathy shown by some of the healthcare providers involved in her father's care. Ultimately, the article is a poignant reflection on grief, the importance of healthcare advocacy, and the need for systemic improvements within the VA to ensure veterans receive the quality care they deserve.
