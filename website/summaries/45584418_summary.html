U.S. Sanctions Cambodian Conglomerate, Citing Role in 'Pig-Butchering' Scams

Summary

The U.S. government has imposed sanctions on a Cambodian conglomerate, Mong Reththy Group, and its owner, Mong Reththy, citing their involvement in pig butchering scams. These scams, a form of online fraud, involve building trust with victims through online relationships before defrauding them of money. The U.S. Treasury Department's Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC) stated that Mong Reththy Group provided material support, including land, to entities involved in these scams in Cambodia. The sanctions aim to disrupt the operations of these criminal organizations and protect potential victims. As a result of the sanctions, all property and interests in property of Mong Reththy Group and Mong Reththy that are in the United States or in the possession or control of U.S. persons are blocked and must be reported to OFAC.

Key Points

  • The U.S. sanctioned Mong Reththy Group, a Cambodian conglomerate, and its owner, Mong Reththy.
  • The sanctions are due to the company's role in supporting pig butchering scams.
  • Pig butchering scams are online frauds where perpetrators build relationships with victims before defrauding them.
  • Mong Reththy Group provided material support, including land, to entities involved in these scams.
  • The U.S. Treasury Department's OFAC imposed the sanctions.
  • The sanctions block all U.S.-based property and interests of Mong Reththy Group and Mong Reththy.
