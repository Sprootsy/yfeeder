Tesla's "Mad Max" mode is now under federal scrutiny

Summary:

The National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA) is investigating Tesla's "Mad Max" suspension mode. This mode, discovered in updated versions of the Tesla Model S and Model X, allows drivers to adjust the vehicle's suspension settings beyond the factory-defined limits, potentially affecting handling and stability. The investigation was prompted by a petition highlighting concerns that these modifications could increase the risk of accidents.

The probe will assess the safety implications of allowing users to alter suspension parameters. NHTSA is seeking information from Tesla regarding the design, functionality, and potential safety consequences of the Mad Max mode. The investigation aims to determine whether this feature poses an unreasonable risk to vehicle safety.

Key Points:

  • NHTSA is investigating Tesla's "Mad Max" suspension mode.
  • The mode allows drivers to adjust suspension settings beyond factory limits on Model S and Model X.
  • The investigation was initiated due to concerns about potential safety risks.
  • NHTSA wants to know about the design, function, and safety consequences of the mode.
  • The probe will determine if the mode poses an unreasonable risk to vehicle safety.
