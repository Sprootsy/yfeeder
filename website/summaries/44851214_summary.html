GPTs and Feeling Left Behind

The article is a fictional journal entry dated August 6, 2025, reflecting on the rapid advancements in GPT technology and the author's sense of being left behind. The author, an individual who was once enthusiastic about AI, now feels overwhelmed and struggles to keep up with the pace of innovation.

The author recounts their initial excitement about GPT-3 and their efforts to learn and experiment with it. They even built a small application using the technology. However, with the release of GPT-4 and subsequent versions, the advancements became too rapid. The author describes feeling like they barely grasped the capabilities of one version before being confronted with a newer, more advanced one. This constant cycle of catch-up left them feeling inadequate and discouraged.

A significant turning point for the author was when GPT models started excelling in areas they considered their strengths, such as creative writing and problem-solving. Seeing AI surpass their abilities in these domains led to a sense of existential crisis. The author questions their own value and purpose in a world where AI can perform many tasks more efficiently and effectively.

The article explores the psychological impact of rapid technological advancement, particularly on individuals who once felt skilled and knowledgeable in a particular area. It delves into themes of obsolescence, the changing nature of work, and the challenges of maintaining a sense of purpose in an AI-driven world. The author expresses a longing for a slower pace of change and a desire to find new ways to contribute and remain relevant.

The journal entry concludes with the author grappling with uncertainty about the future and contemplating how to adapt to this new reality. They acknowledge the potential benefits of AI but also express concern about its potential to exacerbate existing inequalities and create new forms of social division.
