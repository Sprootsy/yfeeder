A conspiracy to kill IE6 (2019)

The article "A Conspiracy to Kill IE6" by Chris Zacharias recounts the author's experience leading a campaign at YouTube to deprecate Internet Explorer 6 (IE6), a web browser released in 2001. The author explains the technical challenges that IE6 posed for web developers due to its lack of support for modern web standards and its buggy rendering engine. Maintaining compatibility with IE6 required significant extra effort and resources, diverting focus from implementing new features and improving the user experience for users on modern browsers.

The author describes how the YouTube team initially tried to address the IE6 problem by educating users and encouraging them to upgrade to newer browsers. However, these efforts had limited success. They then considered completely dropping support for IE6, but this was deemed too risky due to the significant number of YouTube users still using the browser, particularly in China.

The turning point came when the author conceived the idea of displaying a small banner to IE6 users suggesting that they upgrade their browser. The banner would not block access to YouTube but would serve as a persistent reminder of the benefits of upgrading. The team carefully designed the banner to be unobtrusive and user-friendly, and they tested it extensively to ensure that it would not negatively impact the user experience.

After launching the banner, the YouTube team saw a significant drop in IE6 traffic. Other websites, including Digg, followed suit, implementing similar banners. This collective effort created a network effect, accelerating the decline of IE6 usage across the web. The author emphasizes that the campaign was not a top-down initiative but rather a grassroots effort driven by individual developers who were frustrated with the technical challenges of supporting IE6.

The author acknowledges that some people viewed the campaign as a conspiracy against IE6, but he argues that it was a necessary step to move the web forward. By reducing the burden of supporting IE6, web developers could focus on building better and more innovative web experiences for everyone. The article concludes by reflecting on the impact of the campaign and its role in shaping the modern web.

  • IE6 posed significant technical challenges for web developers due to its lack of support for modern web standards.
