U.S. Lost 32,000 Private-Sector Jobs in September, Says Payroll Processor

Summary:

According to a report by payroll processor ADP, the U.S. private sector experienced a loss of 32,000 jobs in September. This is a notable shift from the revised 180,000 jobs added in August and marks the weakest performance since the beginning of the year. The decline was primarily driven by a significant decrease in jobs within the manufacturing sector, which shed 30,000 positions.

The report also highlighted variations across different business sizes. Large companies, those with 500 or more employees, reduced their workforce by 134,000 jobs. Medium-sized businesses, employing between 50 and 499 workers, saw a modest increase of 14,000 jobs. Small businesses, with fewer than 50 employees, added 114,000 jobs.

In terms of wages, the report indicated that annual pay for job-stayers increased by 5.9% in September, a slowdown from the previous month. For those who changed jobs, the annual pay increase was 9%, also a decrease from August.

This ADP report precedes the Labor Department's official jobs report, and while it's not always perfectly aligned, it offers insights into the labor market's direction. The unexpected job losses in September suggest a potential cooling of the labor market, though economists caution against drawing definitive conclusions based on a single month's data. They emphasize the need to consider broader economic trends and the Labor Department's report for a more comprehensive understanding of the employment situation.

Key Points:

  • U.S. private sector lost 32,000 jobs in September, according to ADP.
  • Manufacturing sector experienced a significant loss of 30,000 jobs.
  • Large companies cut 134,000 jobs, while small businesses added 114,000.
  • Annual pay increase for job-stayers slowed to 5.9%.
  • Annual pay increase for job-changers also slowed to 9%.
  • The report precedes the Labor Department's official jobs report and offers insights into the labor market.
