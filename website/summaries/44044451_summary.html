Gemma 3n preview: Mobile-first AI

Summary

The Google Developers Blog post introduces Gemma 3N, a new family of open models from Google, built upon the same research and technology used to create the Gemini models. Gemma 3N is available in two sizes: Gemma 2B and Gemma 7B, each with pre-trained and instruction-tuned variants. The models are designed to be lightweight and efficient, making them suitable for a variety of hardware platforms, including laptops, desktops, and mobile devices. Gemma 3N prioritizes responsible AI development, with comprehensive safety evaluations and mitigations implemented during its creation. The models are released with tools and resources to help developers build responsibly with them. Google emphasizes the ease of use and integration of Gemma 3N with popular AI development frameworks like JAX, PyTorch, and TensorFlow, along with tools like Colab and Kaggle. Optimized versions are also available for NVIDIA GPUs. The release aims to foster innovation and collaboration within the AI community by providing access to state-of-the-art models. The blog post details the performance of Gemma 3N, highlighting its strong performance relative to other open models of similar sizes. It provides links to resources for getting started with Gemma 3N, including model cards, documentation, and tutorials. The release underscores Google's commitment to open innovation in AI and its support for developers building the next generation of AI applications.
