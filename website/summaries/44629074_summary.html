EU commissioner shocked by dangers of some goods sold by Shein and Temu

Summary:

An EU commissioner has expressed shock over the prevalence of dangerous and non-compliant goods being sold on online marketplaces Shein and Temu. The commissioner was surprised after personally ordering items from both platforms and discovering significant safety issues, including products that posed potential health risks. These issues included toys with small parts that could be choking hazards and electrical goods with inadequate safety features. The commissioner highlighted that the scale of the problem was alarming, suggesting that a substantial portion of products on these platforms failed to meet EU safety standards. The commissioner stressed the need for stricter enforcement of regulations and greater accountability for online marketplaces to protect consumers from potentially harmful products.

Key Points:

  • EU commissioner "shocked" by dangerous goods on Shein and Temu.
  • Personal orders revealed significant safety issues.
  • Toys with small parts posed choking hazards.
  • Electrical goods had inadequate safety features.
  • A large portion of products failed to meet EU safety standards.
  • The commissioner called for stricter enforcement and greater marketplace accountability.
