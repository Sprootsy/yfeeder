Summary

The haveibeenflocked.com website provides a service to check if your email address has been registered for a "flocking" prank. Flocking is described as a practical joke where a large number of plastic pink flamingos are placed on someone's lawn, typically as a fundraising effort by a local organization. The website allows users to enter their email address to determine if someone has signed them up to be flocked. It emphasizes that the website is independent and not affiliated with any flocking organizations. The site assures users that email addresses are only used for checking against the flocking list and are not stored, shared, or used for any other purpose. It explains the flocking process and offers an opt-out option.

Key Points

  • The website checks if your email is on a list to be "flocked" (receiving a lawn full of plastic pink flamingos).
  • Flocking is a fundraising prank involving plastic flamingos.
  • The website is independent and not associated with any specific flocking organizations.
  • Email addresses are only used to check against the flocking list.
  • Email addresses are not stored, shared, or used for other purposes.
