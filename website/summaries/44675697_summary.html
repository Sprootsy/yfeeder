Games: No sex, please. we're credit card companies

According to an Ars Technica article published in July 2025, Itch.io, a platform known for hosting a wide variety of indie games including adult-oriented content, has begun removing explicitly sexual titles. This marks a shift for the platform, which has historically been more permissive than other storefronts like Steam. The decision to remove these titles has sparked controversy among developers and users, with concerns raised about censorship and the potential impact on independent creators who rely on the platform's openness. The article explores the reasons behind this change, the reaction from the Itch.io community, and the broader implications for the distribution of adult games.

Reportedly, the primary driver for this change is increased pressure from payment processors and advertisers. These companies are becoming more risk-averse regarding adult content, leading them to impose stricter terms of service. Itch.io, like many online platforms, relies on these third-party services for financial transactions and advertising revenue. To maintain access to these essential services, Itch.io has been forced to re-evaluate its content policies.

The removal of explicit titles has been met with mixed reactions. Some users express understanding, acknowledging the difficult position Itch.io faces. They recognize that the platform needs to balance its commitment to indie developers with the practical realities of operating a business. However, many developers and users feel betrayed, arguing that Itch.io's open and inclusive approach was a key differentiator. They worry that this change signals a move towards a more sanitized and corporate environment, potentially stifling creativity and innovation.

Several developers have voiced concerns about the lack of transparency and communication from Itch.io regarding the specific criteria for content removal. Some claim their games were removed without clear explanation, leaving them uncertain about how to comply with the new policies. This lack of clarity has fueled mistrust and resentment within the community.

The article also discusses the potential alternatives for developers affected by the content removal. Some are considering migrating to other platforms with more permissive policies, while others are exploring alternative funding models, such as direct patronage or cryptocurrency-based transactions. However, these alternatives often come with their own challenges, such as smaller audiences or increased technical complexity.

Ultimately, the situation highlights the ongoing tension between artistic expression, platform responsibility, and the influence of third-party service providers in the digital age. The long-term consequences of Itch.io's decision remain to be seen, but it undoubtedly marks a significant turning point for the platform and the wider indie game community.
