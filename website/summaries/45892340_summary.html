Microplastics: No longer a "maybe"

This article from the Insolvency and Bankruptcy Board of India (IBBI) provides information about the Model Bye-Laws for registered valuers organizations (RVOs). It defines the term RVO and explains that RVOs are companies licensed under Section 8 of the Companies Act, 2013, to enroll and regulate registered valuers as their members. These organizations play a crucial role in developing the profession of valuers. The article emphasizes the importance of adhering to the Model Bye-Laws to maintain standards and ethics within the valuation profession.

The Model Bye-Laws are designed to guide RVOs in their operations and governance. They cover various aspects, including membership criteria, rights and obligations of members, the establishment of committees, disciplinary procedures, grievance redressal mechanisms, and the overall management of the RVO. They prescribe eligibility criteria for individuals seeking membership, such as educational qualifications, experience requirements, and the passing of valuation examinations conducted by IBBI. The bye-laws also specify the process for admission, renewal, and termination of membership.

The article highlights the responsibilities of RVO members, which include upholding the code of conduct, adhering to valuation standards, participating in continuing professional education, and cooperating with the RVO in its regulatory functions. The Model Bye-Laws outline the rights of members, such as the right to vote, participate in general meetings, and access information about the RVO's activities.

Furthermore, the Model Bye-Laws address the governance structure of RVOs, stipulating the composition and functions of the governing board and various committees. These committees may include membership committees, disciplinary committees, and ethics committees. The bye-laws outline the procedures for conducting meetings, making decisions, and maintaining records.

A significant portion of the Model Bye-Laws is dedicated to disciplinary proceedings. They describe the process for handling complaints against members, conducting investigations, and imposing penalties for misconduct. The bye-laws ensure that disciplinary actions are fair, transparent, and consistent with the principles of natural justice. Members have the right to appeal against disciplinary decisions.

The article also discusses the importance of having a robust grievance redressal mechanism within the RVO. This mechanism provides a forum for members to raise concerns and seek resolution of disputes. The Model Bye-Laws outline the procedures for filing grievances, conducting hearings, and making decisions.

In conclusion, the article underscores the significance of the Model Bye-Laws in promoting professionalism, ethical conduct, and effective regulation within the valuation profession. By adhering to these bye-laws, RVOs can ensure the quality and integrity of valuation services and contribute to the development of a robust valuation ecosystem.

Key Points:

  • RVOs are companies licensed under Section 8 of the Companies Act, 2013, to enroll and regulate registered valuers.
  • The Model Bye-Laws provide a framework for the governance and operation of RVOs.
  • Membership criteria include educational qualifications, experience, and passing the valuation examination.
  • Members must adhere to a code of conduct and valuation standards.
  • RVOs have committees for membership, discipline, and ethics.
  • Disciplinary proceedings must be fair and transparent.
  • RVOs must have a grievance redressal mechanism.
  • The Model Bye-Laws promote professionalism and ethical conduct in the valuation profession.
