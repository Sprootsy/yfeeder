This assumption permeates various aspects of life, from media representation and cultural norms to personal interactions and expectations.

The author reflects on how compulsory heterosexuality shaped her own understanding of relationships and attractions from a young age. This conditioning manifested in internal confusion, feelings of inadequacy, and a sense of being different or abnormal.

This sheds light on the constant pressure lesbians face to validate or justify their identity.

Ultimately, the article advocates for greater awareness and understanding of compulsory heterosexuality and its impact on individuals, particularly those who identify as lesbian. It calls for a more inclusive and accepting society that embraces diverse sexual orientations and challenges the heteronormative assumptions that perpetuate inequality and discrimination.
