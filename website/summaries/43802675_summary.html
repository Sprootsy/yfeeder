Backblaze: Mounting Losses, Lawsuits, Sham Accounting, Insider Selling

Summary:

The provided URL leads to a Backblaze Drive Stats report by Morpheus Research. The report analyzes the hard drive failure rates and reliability based on data published by Backblaze, a cloud storage provider. Backblaze regularly publishes statistics on the hard drives they use in their data centers, providing valuable insights into the performance and longevity of different drive models from various manufacturers like HGST (now Western Digital), Seagate, and Toshiba.

The core of the report revolves around examining the Annualized Failure Rates (AFR) of different hard drive models. AFR represents the percentage of drives of a specific model that are expected to fail within a year. By tracking AFR over time, the report identifies trends, such as which drives are consistently reliable, which ones have higher failure rates, and how failure rates change as drives age. The analysis often breaks down the data by drive size (e.g., 4TB, 8TB, 12TB, 16TB) to see if there's a correlation between drive capacity and reliability.

The report also considers factors like the drive's usage hours (drive days) when calculating AFR. A larger number of drive days for a specific drive model gives more statistical significance to the AFR. The data also takes into account the total number of drives of each model. Some models may have a low AFR, but if the sample size is small, the results might not be as reliable as those from a model with a large number of drives tested.

The reports typically cover the lifespan of the drives, from initial installation to eventual retirement. The data can also be used to compare the performance of different manufacturers. For example, one brand might consistently have lower AFRs across multiple drive sizes than another. These insights are valuable for anyone making decisions about purchasing hard drives for data storage purposes, whether for personal use, small businesses, or large enterprises.

Backblaze's reports and, by extension, Morpheus Research's analysis, provide a unique look into real-world hard drive performance within a demanding data center environment. The data isn't a controlled lab test, but rather reflects the actual experience of drives operating 24/7. This makes the information particularly useful for understanding long-term reliability.

Finally, it's important to note that while the Backblaze data is insightful, it is not the definitive source for hard drive reliability. The operational conditions within Backblaze's data centers and the workloads placed on the drives are specific to their use case. Other users might experience different failure rates based on their environment and usage patterns.

Key Points:

  • The report analyzes Backblaze's hard drive statistics to determine failure rates and reliability.
  • Annualized Failure Rate (AFR) is a primary metric used to evaluate drive reliability.
  • The analysis compares AFRs across different drive models, manufacturers (Seagate, Western Digital/HGST, Toshiba), and drive sizes.
  • The data considers the number of drive days to ensure statistical significance.
  • The reports cover the entire lifespan of the drives, from installation to retirement.
  • Real-world data is based on Backblaze's data center environment and workloads.
  • The data can be useful for making informed decisions about hard drive purchases.
