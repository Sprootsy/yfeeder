Algorithms for Optimization [pdf]

The article is a comprehensive introduction to optimization techniques, focusing on both unconstrained and constrained optimization problems. It begins by defining optimization as the process of finding the best solution from all feasible solutions, clarifying key terms like objective function, decision variables, and constraints. The text then explores unconstrained optimization, detailing methods such as line search (including bisection and Newton's method) and gradient descent. It emphasizes the importance of step size selection and convergence criteria in these iterative approaches.

The discussion transitions to constrained optimization, where the feasible region is limited by equality and inequality constraints. It introduces concepts like Lagrange multipliers and the Karush-Kuhn-Tucker (KKT) conditions, which provide necessary conditions for optimality in constrained problems. The article examines linear programming (LP), a specific type of constrained optimization with linear objective functions and constraints, and discusses the simplex method for solving LP problems.

Further topics covered include quadratic programming (QP), where the objective function is quadratic and constraints are linear, and methods for solving QP problems. The text also delves into nonlinear programming (NLP), which encompasses more general nonlinear objective functions and constraints, and introduces techniques like sequential quadratic programming (SQP) for solving NLP problems. The article highlights the challenges associated with NLP, such as non-convexity and the potential for local optima.

Throughout the document, the concepts are illustrated with examples, and the mathematical formulations are presented with clarity. The article also touches upon various practical considerations, such as dealing with non-differentiable functions and handling constraints effectively. It provides a solid foundation in optimization theory and techniques, suitable for readers with a basic understanding of calculus and linear algebra.

Key Points:

  • Definition of Optimization: Finding the best solution from a set of feasible solutions by minimizing or maximizing an objective function.
  • Unconstrained Optimization: Techniques for finding optima without constraints, including line search methods (bisection, Newton's) and gradient descent.
  • Constrained Optimization: Optimization problems with equality and inequality constraints.
  • Lagrange Multipliers and KKT Conditions: Necessary conditions for optimality in constrained optimization problems.
  • Linear Programming (LP): Optimization with linear objective functions and constraints; solved using the simplex method.
  • Quadratic Programming (QP): Optimization with quadratic objective functions and linear constraints.
  • Nonlinear Programming (NLP): General optimization problems with nonlinear objective functions and constraints; solved using SQP and other methods.
  • Practical Considerations: Handling non-differentiable functions, constraint management, and the challenges of non-convexity.
