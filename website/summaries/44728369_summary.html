Microsoft bans LibreOffice developer's account without warning, rejects appeal

Summary

Microsoft has banned an account belonging to a LibreOffice developer without warning and rejected the developer's appeal. The developer, involved in distributing LibreOffice via the Microsoft Store, received an email stating their Microsoft Partner account was closed due to a violation of the Microsoft Store Policies, specifically related to section 11.2. The email indicated the account was closed because the developer engaged in deceptive, misleading, malicious, or otherwise abusive or unacceptable practices. No specific details or examples of the violations were provided, and the developer was informed that the decision was final and not subject to further review.

The developer says their account closure prevents them from updating the LibreOffice app in the Microsoft Store, effectively blocking users from receiving updates and security patches. The developer expressed frustration over the lack of specific information regarding the reason for the ban and the inability to appeal the decision effectively.

The article highlights the broader implications of such actions, especially for open-source developers who rely on the Microsoft Store to distribute their software. It raises questions about Microsoft's policies and enforcement mechanisms, particularly the lack of transparency and due process in account closures. The sudden ban and the lack of detailed explanations could potentially deter other open-source developers from using the Microsoft Store as a distribution platform, impacting the availability of open-source software to Windows users.

Key Points

  • Microsoft banned a LibreOffice developer's Microsoft Partner account without warning.
  • The stated reason for the ban was a violation of Microsoft Store Policies, specifically section 11.2, relating to deceptive, misleading, malicious, or abusive practices.
  • No specific details or examples of the violations were provided to the developer.
  • The developer's appeal was rejected, and the decision was deemed final with no further review possible.
  • The account closure prevents the developer from updating the LibreOffice app in the Microsoft Store.
  • The incident raises concerns about Microsoft's treatment of open-source developers and the transparency of its store policies.
