The cost of interrupted work (2023)

Summary

The article is a personal reflection on the author's experience of running the Berlin Marathon 2023. It meticulously details the author's preparation, including training regimes, nutrition, and mental strategies employed in the months leading up to the race. The author discusses the challenges faced during training, such as injuries and the mental fortitude required to maintain consistency.

The article then transitions to the race day experience. The author vividly describes the atmosphere at the starting line, the initial excitement, and the gradual settling into a rhythm. A significant portion is dedicated to narrating the physical and mental challenges encountered throughout the 42.195 kilometers. The author describes the strategies used to cope with pain, fatigue, and moments of doubt, including breaking the race into smaller, more manageable segments and focusing on maintaining a consistent pace.

A turning point in the narrative occurs around the 30km mark, where the author experiences a significant drop in energy and an increase in physical discomfort. The article delves into the mental battle that ensues, highlighting the importance of self-belief and drawing strength from the support of the crowd and fellow runners.

The final kilometers are portrayed as a grueling test of endurance, with the author pushing through intense pain and exhaustion. The emotional impact of approaching the finish line is palpable, culminating in a sense of overwhelming relief and accomplishment upon crossing it.

The author reflects on the overall experience, emphasizing the profound sense of personal growth and the lessons learned about perseverance, mental toughness, and the power of community. The article concludes with a sense of gratitude and a renewed appreciation for the journey, both physical and mental, that the marathon represents.
