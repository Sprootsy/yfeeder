Washington, DC police put under federal control, National Guard deployed

Summary

According to an article published on CNBC.com in August 2025, former President Donald Trump, now a candidate for the 2028 presidential election, is campaigning on a platform focused on restoring law and order in Washington, D.C. He describes the city as being in a state of decay and lawlessness. Trump's plan involves deploying the National Guard to combat crime and address the homelessness crisis in the city. He criticizes the current city leadership, including the mayor, for failing to maintain safety and order.

Trump is advocating for a more aggressive approach to crime, promising to crack down on what he describes as rampant lawlessness. His rhetoric echoes his previous "law and order" campaigns, emphasizing the need for strong action to restore public safety. The article suggests that this stance is a key element of his strategy to resonate with voters concerned about crime rates and urban decay.

The proposal to deploy the National Guard has sparked controversy, with critics questioning the legality and necessity of such a move. They argue that it could potentially violate the city's autonomy and raise concerns about the militarization of law enforcement. Supporters, however, claim it is a necessary step to address the pressing issues of crime and homelessness.

The article also touches upon the homelessness issue in D.C., highlighting Trump's intention to address it with a similar assertive approach. While specific details of his plans remain unclear, the article implies that his strategy will likely involve removing homeless encampments and providing alternative housing solutions. This aspect of his platform has also drawn criticism, with some arguing that it lacks compassion and fails to address the root causes of homelessness.

Overall, the article portrays Trump's 2028 campaign as heavily focused on restoring law and order in Washington, D.C., using tactics such as deploying the National Guard and taking a firm stance on homelessness. This strategy is presented as a way for Trump to appeal to voters concerned about the city's perceived decline and to contrast himself with his political opponents.
