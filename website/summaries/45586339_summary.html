FSF announces Librephone project

Summary:

The Free Software Foundation (FSF) announced its endorsement of the Librephone project. The Librephone project aims to create a fully free and open source smartphone. This endorsement signals the FSF's belief that the project is committed to the four essential freedoms of free software, providing users with control over their devices and data. The project is working to build a phone where users can trust that the software running on it respects their rights to use, study, share, and improve it. The Librephone initiative encompasses not only the operating system but also the hardware components and the processes involved in manufacturing and distribution, aiming for maximum transparency and user control.

Key points:

  • The Free Software Foundation (FSF) has endorsed the Librephone project.
  • Librephone aims to create a smartphone that is entirely free and open source.
  • The project is aligned with the FSF's four essential freedoms of free software.
  • The goal is to provide users with control over their devices and data.
  • The initiative encompasses the operating system, hardware, manufacturing, and distribution.
