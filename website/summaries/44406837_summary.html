Use Plaintext Email (2019)

The article "useplaintext.email" advocates for the use of plain text emails over HTML emails, highlighting several benefits related to security, accessibility, readability, and respect for the recipient's preferences. It argues that while HTML emails offer more formatting and visual options, they come with significant drawbacks that make plain text a superior choice for most email communication.

The primary arguments in favor of plain text emails are:

  • Security: HTML emails can contain tracking pixels, scripts, and other potentially malicious content that can compromise the recipient's privacy and security. Plain text emails, devoid of these features, eliminate these risks.
  • Accessibility: Plain text emails are inherently more accessible to people with disabilities, particularly those who use screen readers or have other assistive technologies. HTML emails can be complex and difficult to parse for these tools, leading to a degraded user experience.
  • Readability: Plain text emails are rendered consistently across all email clients and devices, ensuring that the recipient sees the message as intended. HTML emails, on the other hand, can be displayed differently depending on the email client, leading to formatting issues and readability problems.
  • Size and Bandwidth: Plain text emails are significantly smaller in size than HTML emails, which can be especially important for recipients with limited bandwidth or data plans.
  • Respect for Recipients: Sending plain text emails demonstrates respect for the recipient's preferences and choices. It allows them to view the message in the way that best suits their needs and constraints, without imposing unnecessary formatting or tracking.
  • Reduced Clutter and Distraction: The simplicity of plain text encourages focused communication, eliminating distractions caused by images, animations, and other visual elements.
  • Future-Proofing: Plain text is a timeless format that will remain readable regardless of future technological changes. HTML, on the other hand, is subject to evolving standards and compatibility issues.
  • Easier Archiving and Searching: Plain text emails are easier to archive and search because they lack the complex structure and formatting of HTML emails.

The article acknowledges that HTML emails can be useful in certain situations, such as for marketing campaigns or newsletters where visual appeal is paramount. However, it argues that for the vast majority of email communication, plain text is the more responsible, secure, and user-friendly option.

It concludes by encouraging readers to adopt plain text as their default email format and to advocate for its wider adoption.

Key Points:

  • Plain text emails enhance security by eliminating tracking pixels and malicious scripts.
  • They improve accessibility for users with disabilities.
  • Plain text ensures consistent readability across all email clients.
  • They consume less bandwidth.
  • Sending plain text shows respect for the recipient's preferences.
  • Plain text reduces distractions and promotes focused communication.
  • It's a future-proof format.
  • Plain text emails are easier to archive and search.
  • HTML emails, while visually appealing, have security and accessibility drawbacks.
  • The article advocates for plain text as the default email format for most communications.
