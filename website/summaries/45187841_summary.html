Apple barely talked about AI at its big iPhone 17 event

Apple's September event showcased a range of new products and features, with a significant emphasis on AI integration, now branded as "Apple Intelligence." The event highlighted advancements across various devices, including the iPhone 15 lineup, Apple Watch Series 9, and the next generation of AirPods Pro. A key theme was the seamless integration of hardware and software to deliver enhanced user experiences, with AI playing a crucial role in personalization, automation, and overall device functionality.

The iPhone 15 and 15 Pro models feature camera improvements, a new A17 Bionic chip (for the Pro models), and a transition to USB-C charging. Apple emphasized the camera's low-light performance and enhanced zoom capabilities. The A17 Bionic chip enables improved gaming performance and more efficient processing for AI-related tasks. The USB-C port replaces the Lightning connector, aligning with industry standards and offering faster data transfer speeds.

The Apple Watch Series 9 introduces a new S9 SiP (System in Package) that enables features like Double Tap, allowing users to control the watch with one hand by tapping their index finger and thumb together. This new gesture can answer calls, end calls, stop timers, and control music. The display is also significantly brighter, and on-device Siri processing enhances speed and privacy. Apple also announced advancements in its environmental initiatives, with a focus on carbon neutrality for select Apple Watch models.

The next-generation AirPods Pro boast improved audio quality, enhanced Active Noise Cancellation, and Personalized Spatial Audio. They also come with a USB-C charging case. Apple claims significantly improved noise cancellation capabilities and longer battery life. The new H2 chip inside the AirPods Pro is responsible for these improvements.

Apple Intelligence is woven throughout the new devices and software, powering features like advanced image recognition in Photos, improved Siri capabilities, and proactive suggestions in various apps. Apple stressed the privacy aspects of their AI implementation, highlighting on-device processing and secure cloud computing when necessary. The goal is to make technology more intuitive and helpful while respecting user privacy.

The event also touched on software updates, including iOS 17 and watchOS 10, which bring new features and improvements to existing devices. These updates further enhance the overall Apple ecosystem experience, making devices more connected and user-friendly.

Key Points:

  • iPhone 15 Lineup: New models with camera upgrades, A17 Bionic chip (Pro models), and USB-C charging.
  • Apple Watch Series 9: New S9 SiP enabling Double Tap gesture, brighter display, and on-device Siri processing.
  • AirPods Pro (Next Generation): Improved audio quality, enhanced Active Noise Cancellation, Personalized Spatial Audio, and USB-C charging case.
  • Apple Intelligence: AI integration for personalized experiences, improved Siri, and advanced image recognition, with a focus on privacy.
  • Software Updates: iOS 17 and watchOS 10 bring new features and improvements to existing devices.
  • Environmental Initiatives: Focus on carbon neutrality for select Apple Watch models.
