Circular AI deals among OpenAI, Nvidia, AMD are raising eyebrows

Summary

The Bloomberg article, published on October 8, 2025, discusses the interconnected financial relationships between OpenAI, Nvidia, and AMD, raising concerns about a potential tech bubble. These companies are heavily reliant on each other, creating a circular dependency. OpenAI, a leading AI company, requires substantial computing power, primarily supplied by Nvidia and AMD's advanced chips. Nvidia and AMD, in turn, benefit significantly from the demand generated by OpenAI and other AI developers. This symbiotic relationship has fueled rapid growth and high valuations for all three companies.

The article highlights the potential risks associated with this circular arrangement. If OpenAI's growth slows down, or if the AI sector experiences a downturn, it could negatively impact Nvidia and AMD's revenue. Conversely, any supply chain issues or technological setbacks at Nvidia or AMD could hinder OpenAI's progress. Moreover, the high valuations of these companies may not be sustainable if the underlying growth drivers weaken.

Several analysts and industry experts are quoted in the article, expressing concerns about the concentration of power and the lack of diversification in this ecosystem. They warn that the current situation resembles the dot-com bubble of the late 1990s, where inflated expectations and unsustainable business models led to a market crash. The article suggests that investors should be cautious and carefully assess the long-term prospects of these companies before investing.

The piece also explores the regulatory scrutiny that these companies may face in the future. Antitrust authorities are likely to examine the close ties between OpenAI, Nvidia, and AMD, as well as their dominant positions in the AI and chip markets. Regulatory actions could potentially disrupt their business models and impact their valuations.

In conclusion, the Bloomberg article paints a picture of a highly interconnected and potentially vulnerable tech ecosystem centered around OpenAI, Nvidia, and AMD. While these companies have experienced tremendous success, the article suggests that their reliance on each other and the broader AI market creates significant risks that investors should be aware of.

Key Points

  • OpenAI, Nvidia, and AMD have a circular relationship where OpenAI relies on Nvidia and AMD chips, and Nvidia/AMD rely on OpenAI's demand.
  • This relationship has fueled rapid growth and high valuations for all three companies.
  • The circular dependency creates risks: a slowdown in OpenAI's growth or AI sector downturn could hurt Nvidia and AMD.
  • Supply chain issues or technological setbacks at Nvidia or AMD could hinder OpenAI's progress.
  • Concerns are raised about unsustainable valuations and similarities to the dot-com bubble.
  • The concentration of power and lack of diversification in the ecosystem are highlighted as potential problems.
  • Increased regulatory scrutiny from antitrust authorities is expected.
  • Investors are advised to be cautious and carefully assess long-term prospects.
