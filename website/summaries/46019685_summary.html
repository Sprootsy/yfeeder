A monopoly ISP refuses to fix upstream infrastructure

Summary

The article on sacbear.com details the author's frustrating experience with Xfinity's internet service and their inability to resolve a persistent internet outage. The author, a Sacramento resident, had been a loyal Xfinity customer for years, consistently paying for their service. However, starting in October 2023, they began experiencing frequent internet outages, sometimes multiple times a day. Despite numerous calls to Xfinity support, the issue remained unresolved.

The author meticulously documented their attempts to get the problem fixed, outlining each interaction with Xfinity representatives. They explain how they spent hours on the phone, repeating the same information to different agents, and undergoing troubleshooting steps that proved ineffective. Xfinity representatives would often schedule technician visits, but either the technicians wouldn't show up, or they would arrive and be unable to diagnose or fix the root cause of the outages.

The article highlights the recurring theme of Xfinity's customer service failing to take ownership of the problem. The author describes situations where representatives would pass the blame to external factors, such as neighborhood issues or scheduled maintenance, without providing concrete solutions. Even when technicians did identify potential problems, such as a faulty line or a signal issue, the follow-up actions were often delayed or incomplete.

The author also emphasizes the time wasted waiting for support agents to perform diagnostics or schedule appointments, time during which they are unable to work or use their internet service. The situation became increasingly disruptive to their daily life, especially since their work relies heavily on a stable internet connection.

The author expresses their disappointment and frustration with Xfinity's lack of responsiveness and inability to resolve the persistent internet outages. They feel that Xfinity is not taking their concerns seriously and is failing to provide the service they are paying for. They are now considering switching to another internet provider.

Key Points

  • Frequent and persistent internet outages started in October 2023.
  • Multiple calls to Xfinity support failed to resolve the issue.
  • Technician visits were often missed or unsuccessful.
  • Xfinity customer service was unresponsive and ineffective.
  • The author spent hours on the phone with support agents.
  • The outages disrupted the author's work and daily life.
  • The author is considering switching to another internet provider.
