Anthropic, Google, OpenAI and XAI Granted Up to $200M from Defense Department

According to a CNBC article published on July 14, 2025, the Department of Defense (DoD) has granted up to $200 million to AI companies, including Anthropic, Google, OpenAI, and XAI, to advance explainable AI (XAI). The DoD aims to improve the transparency and reliability of AI systems used in national security applications. The initiative reflects the increasing reliance on AI in defense and the necessity for these systems to be understandable and trustworthy.

The grants will fund research and development efforts focused on making AI decision-making processes more transparent. This involves creating AI models that can explain their reasoning and justify their conclusions, enabling human operators to understand and validate the AI's actions. The DoD emphasizes that XAI is crucial for ensuring accountability and preventing potential errors or biases in AI systems.

Anthropic, known for its work on constitutional AI, plans to use the funding to further develop AI models that adhere to pre-defined ethical and safety guidelines. Google will focus on enhancing the interpretability of its AI algorithms, making them easier to understand and debug. OpenAI aims to improve the transparency of its large language models, allowing users to trace the steps leading to specific outputs. XAI, a newer company specializing in explainable AI, will concentrate on developing tools for visualizing and interpreting complex AI models.
